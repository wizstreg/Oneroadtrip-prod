Rhône Road Trip: Lyon, Golden Stone Villages of Beaujolais & Condrieu Vineyards

Where silk was woven in Croix-Rousse workshops, Romans once applauded at Fourvière, and golden stones shimmer in the Beaujolais sun—the Rhône combines gastronomy, heritage, and the art of living.

📅 Best period May, June, September, October
🚗 Recommended vehicle Standard car (public transport for central Lyon)
👥 Ideal for Couples, families, or groups of friends
🔄 Route type Central Lyon → northern Beaujolais → Golden Stones → Monts d'Or → southern Rhône
📏 Daily average 22 km

From the secret traboules of Old Lyon to Fourvière's Roman theaters, from Croix-Rousse's monumental frescoes to Beaujolais's golden villages. The Rhône unfolds a journey between the capital of Gaul and exceptional vineyards, from Lyon quenelles to bottles of Condrieu Viognier.

  • Old Lyon: UNESCO-listed traboules and Renaissance courtyards
  • Fourvière: basilica, Roman theaters, and Lugdunum museum
  • Les Halles Paul Bocuse: temple of Lyon gastronomy
  • Croix-Rousse: silk weavers, frescoes, and traboules
  • Oingt: Most Beautiful Village in France in the heart of the Golden Stones
  • Condrieu: Viognier vineyards on vertiginous slopes

Lyon

6 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Saint-Jean-Baptiste Cathedral, built between 1175 and 1480, blends Romanesque and Gothic styles across 260 feet of stone. Its 14th-century astronomical clock—one of Europe's oldest—calculates the positions of the sun, moon, and stars above Lyon. Henry IV and Marie de Medici were married here in 1600.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Old Lyon's traboules form a network of over 300 covered passages connecting streets through interior courtyards. The earliest date to the 4th century, others to the Renaissance. During World War II, the Resistance used them to escape the occupiers. A gilded lion's head marks their entrances.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Rue du Boeuf and Rue Saint-Jean concentrate the finest Renaissance townhouses in the quarter. Italian merchants, drawn by the four great trade fairs granted by Charles VII, built mansions with interior courtyards and Italian-style galleries. The Gadagne Museum, in the Hôtel de Pierrevive, traces Lyon's history and houses the world puppetry museum.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Place du Change, the 15th-century bankers' square, preserves the Exchange Lodge built by Soufflot in 1748. The half-timbered houses surrounding it recall when Lyon was Europe's leading financial center, before Paris seized that title.
⏱️ 20 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Guided tour of Old Lyon's traboules. Guides unlock Renaissance courtyard doors normally closed to the public. Spiral staircases, carved wells, and Italian-style galleries reveal themselves behind golden stone façades.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Morning visits recommended—courtyards often close in afternoon
→
Dinner at a traditional Lyonnais bouchon in Old Lyon. Tablier de sapeur, pike quenelle, cervelle de canut, and pink pralines compose the traditional menu. The mâchon—a sort of workers' brunch inherited from silk weavers—is savored in the morning.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Sunset stroll along the Saône quays. Warm light illuminates the cathedral façade and Renaissance houses. The buildings' reflections in the water give the quarter its nickname: 'little Florence.'
⏱️ 30 min

Lyon

6 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
Notre-Dame de Fourvière Basilica, completed in 1884, towers over Lyon from the 'hill that prays.' Its neo-Byzantine interior astonishes with exuberant golden mosaics, stained glass, and sculptures. Guided visits access the rooftop and Observatory Tower for 360° panoramas of the city, the Alps, and the Pilat mountains.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The great Roman theater, built around 15 BC under Augustus, is the oldest in Roman Gaul. Expanded in the 1st century, it could seat 10,000 spectators. Its orchestra preserves polychrome marble paving and a stage curtain pit whose mechanism is reconstructed in the neighboring museum.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The odeon, smaller than the theater, was reserved for musical performances and poetry recitals. Its pavement of colored marbles, sourced from across the Empire, is among the finest in the Roman world. Together, the two structures formed a complex unique in ancient Gaul.
⏱️ 20 min
4
The Lugdunum Museum, embedded in the hillside by architect Bernard Zehrfuss (1975), houses one of France's richest archaeological collections. The Claudian Tablet—Emperor Claudius's speech before the Senate—and the Vaise Treasure rank among the masterpieces. Virtual reality tours available to relive the Roman city.
⏱️ 90 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Funicular ride from Old Lyon to Fourvière. Two lines: one to the basilica, another to the Roman theaters (Minimes station). Descend on foot through the Rosary Gardens—flowered staircases with views of the Saône and Old Lyon.
⏱️ 30 min💡 Ride the funicular up, walk down through the Rosary Gardens
→
Les Nuits de Fourvière transforms the ancient theaters into a summer festival stage each year. Concerts, plays, circus acts, and dance under the stars—the antique setting gives performances magical dimension.
⏱️ 150 min
→
Visit Lugdunum Museum with virtual reality journey. Six immersive stations plunge you into the Roman city of Lugdunum, capital of the Three Gauls. Mosaics, sculptures, and a 3D model of 80 km² of the 2nd-century metropolis.
⏱️ 90 min

Lyon

6 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Place Bellecour, one of Europe's largest pedestrian squares, opens between the Rhône and Saône. Louis XIV's equestrian statue has stood at its center since 1825. The name likely derives from 'Bella Curtis'—beautiful garden in Latin. At no. 29, lion statues guard the pedestrian street entrance. The Little Prince and Antoine de Saint-Exupéry, Lyon-born, have had their statue on the square since 2000.
⏱️ 20 min
2
Place des Terreaux, at the foot of Croix-Rousse, aligns the Bartholdi fountain (originally intended for Bordeaux), the Fine Arts Museum in the former Saint-Pierre Convent, and the City Hall renovated after an 1674 fire. At night, Daniel Buren's 69 water jets illuminate the ground.
⏱️ 20 min
3
Lyon's Museum of Fine Arts, installed in a 17th-century former convent, is France's second most important after the Louvre. Its collections span from antiquity to contemporary art. The cloister garden, free and open to all, is a peaceful haven in the city center.
⏱️ 90 min
4
Lyon Opera House, renovated by Jean Nouvel in 1993, preserves its neoclassical façade crowned by a contemporary glass dome—a striking contrast between 18th-century elegance and modernity. Eight muses on the pediment contemplate the Place de la Comédie.
⏱️ 20 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Gourmet stroll through Les Halles Paul Bocuse. Sample quenelles, aged cheeses, and oysters paired with a glass of Condrieu at the counter. Early morning brings a producers' market atmosphere; late morning, starred chefs do their shopping.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Go in the morning for market atmosphere
→
Visit the Museum of Fine Arts. The collection spans five millennia: Egyptian antiquities, paintings by Rubens, Rembrandt, Monet, Picasso, and a fine contemporary art ensemble. The cloister garden is free and open to everyone.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Shopping and wandering on Rue de la République, carved out in the 19th century by Prefect Vaïsse (Lyon's Haussmann). Covered passages—Passage de l'Argue, Passage Thiaffait—preserve the charm of 19th-century galleries.
⏱️ 60 min

Lyon

6 photos
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
The Mur des Canuts, on Boulevard des Canuts, is Europe's largest trompe-l'oeil: 13,000 square feet of fresco depicting neighborhood life. Created in 1987 by CitéCréation, it's updated every ten years—characters age, shops change. Spot Guignol, the Vélo'v bikes, and the loom between painted windows.
⏱️ 20 min
2
La Maison des Canuts, on Rue d'Ivry, is the living conservatory of Lyon's silk-making heritage. Weaving demonstrations on hand looms, explanations of 19th-century techniques. The canuts—silk-weaver workers—lived in workshop apartments with 13-foot ceilings, necessary to accommodate Jacquard looms.
⏱️ 50 min
3
Croix-Rousse's traboules, built in the 18th century, served to protect silk bales transported from street to street. During the 1831 Canut Revolt—one of history's first workers' uprisings—they allowed insurgents to move under cover. Their motto: 'Live working or die fighting.'
⏱️ 45 min
4
The Fresque des Lyonnais, on Quai Saint-Vincent, features 30 local personalities across 8,600 square feet of façade: Paul Bocuse, the Lumière brothers, Abbé Pierre, Antoine de Saint-Exupéry, Louise Labé. Recognizing them is a game for visitors—painted balconies and windows blend with real ones.
⏱️ 20 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Tour of Croix-Rousse's painted walls. From the Mur des Canuts, descend the slopes passing by iconic frescoes. Allow 90 minutes for the loop to the Fresque des Lyonnais, trabouling through courtyards.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Metro C to Hénon station for starting point
→
Croix-Rousse market, one of Lyon's largest and liveliest. Boulevard de la Croix-Rousse, a UNESCO site, fills with producers' stalls on Tuesday, Thursday, Friday, and Saturday mornings. Cheeses, charcuterie, fruits, and vegetables from the Lyon region.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Visit La Maison des Canuts. Weaving demonstration on period Jacquard looms. Soierie Vivante workshop, in the quarter's last remaining workshops, extends the experience with guided tours in authentic facilities.
⏱️ 50 min

Villefranche Sur Saone

3 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Rue Nationale, Villefranche's founding axis, is lined with buildings from the 15th to 18th centuries. Façades are narrow—a tax was levied per toise (about 6.4 feet) of width! Behind these cramped fronts hide Renaissance courtyards and sumptuous private mansions.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Notre-Dame-des-Marais Collegiate Church, built from the 13th to 19th centuries, towers over the town with its 236-foot spire. Legend has it shepherds discovered a statue of the Virgin in the Morgon marshes. Its façade blends Flamboyant Gothic and Renaissance styles.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Push open the carriage doors on Rue Nationale to discover hidden treasures: the Maison de l'Italien (no. 407) with its polygonal turret, the Maison de la Tourelle (no. 761), the Auberge de la Coupe d'Or (no. 528, the town's oldest, 14th century), and the former town hall (no. 816, completed 1660). The Hidden Treasures circuit reveals these gems in 24 stages.
⏱️ 60 min
4
The Paul Dini Museum, in the town center, presents a modern and contemporary art collection focused on painters from the Lyon region. Housed in a renovated former Grenette market hall, its industrial architecture offers a surprising setting for the canvases.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Hidden Treasures circuit of Villefranche. Twenty-four marked stages through the old town, with explanatory panels. Renaissance interior courtyards, spiral staircases, ironwork, and ogive vaults emerge behind narrow façades.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Beaujolais wine tasting in a town cellar. Villefranche is Beaujolais's administrative capital—cellars offer all ten crus, from Morgon to Fleurie to Brouilly. Every third Thursday in November, the town celebrates Beaujolais Nouveau Day.
⏱️ 60 min

Beaujeu

3 photos
Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
The Romanesque Saint-Nicolas church, founded in the 12th century by the Lords of Beaujeu, is recognizable by its Cluniac-style bell tower. Gothic chapels were added over the centuries. The Lords of Beaujeu, as brilliant politicians as they were formidable warriors, gave their name to the entire territory between the Saône and Loire rivers.
⏱️ 30 min
2
La Maison du Terroir Beaujolais, in a half-timbered Renaissance mansion, presents the traditions, gastronomy, and personalities who shaped Beaujolais. An artistic exhibition hall and local products shop complete the visit.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Place de la Fontaine Clémentine owes its name to an anecdote: in 1831, the mayor asked King Louis-Philippe to fund a fountain. His daughter, Princess Clémentine, reading over the king's shoulder, had to add 25 louis from her personal allowance. The fountain has borne her name ever since.
⏱️ 15 min
4
The Hospices de Beaujeu, founded in the 18th century, house a former apothecary and chapel. The Hospices de Beaujeu wine auction is said to be the world's oldest charity candle auction—67 years before the far more famous one in Beaune.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Tasting in Beaujeu's wine cellars. Le Comptoir Beaujolais, beneath a stone vault, offers crus from 15 partner estates. Beaujolais-Villages, Brouilly, Morgon, and Crémant de Bourgogne to discover alongside farmhouse cheeses and grattons.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Join Les Sarmentelles if you're here on the third Thursday of November: the Beaujolais Nouveau celebration ignites Beaujeu. Torchlight parade, bonfire of vine cuttings in the square, and barrel-tapping at midnight sharp. 'Le Beaujolais Nouveau est arrivé!'
⏱️ 180 min
→
Walks through the vineyards around Beaujeu. The village sits at the border between wine-growing Beaujolais and forested Beaujolais. Trails wind between gamay vines on steep slopes, with views of the Beaujolais mountains.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Several marked trails from the village

Oingt

2 photos
Day 7
🔍

Discover

1
Oingt, the Rhône's only village classified as one of France's Most Beautiful Villages, perches its golden stone houses above Beaujolais vineyards. The ochre color comes from iron oxide in the Jurassic limestone—in the Secondary Era, the region was covered by an inland sea.
⏱️ 30 min
2
Oingt Tower, a 12th-century keep, houses a museum on the village's history. Its panoramic terrace offers 360° views of the Beaujolais and Lyon mountains. On clear days, you can see the Jura, the Alps, and sometimes Mont Blanc and Mont Ventoux.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Porte de Nizy, sole remnant of the 14th-century rampart, marks the old village entrance. At that time, Oingt was Lyon's last stronghold facing the Beaujolais domain of the Lords of Beaujeu. Restored in the 19th century, it opens onto lanes with Rabelaisian names: rue Trayne-Cul, rue Tyre-Laine, rue Coupe-Jarret.
⏱️ 15 min
4
The former castle chapel, now Saint-Mathieu church, preserves 19th-century stained glass. From the church square, sweeping views over the vineyards and Azergues valley. The parrot press, on Rue du Puits, recalls the village's winemaking past.
⏱️ 15 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Hiking through the Golden Stones around Oingt. Trails between vineyards and woods, cadoles (stone shelters built by winegrowers), villages with ochre façades. The 'Sur les hauteurs d'Oingt' circuit (4 miles) offers a condensed view of Beaujolais landscapes.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Tasting with Golden Stones winegrowers. Gamay and Chardonnay produce fruity and elegant Beaujolais here. The Confrérie des Grapilleurs des Pierres Dorées has guarded the winemaking tradition for decades.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Discover neighboring villages: Châtillon-d'Azergues with its 12th-century fortress wrapped around a rock, Bagnols with its stonemasons' houses, Theizé and Château de Rochebonne. Beaujolais's 'Little Tuscany' reveals itself village by village.
⏱️ 120 min

Saint Cyr Au Mont Dor

3 photos
Day 8
🔍

Discover

1
Ternand, a Small Town of Character, nestles its old village on a rocky spur partly composed of white marble, overlooking the Azergues valley. Former summer residence of Lyon's archbishops, the village preserves a rampart walk along the old fortifications with views over vineyards and the Beaujolais mountains.
⏱️ 30 min
2
The Church of the Nativity of Saint John the Baptist, Romanesque in style, shelters beneath its choir an 11th-century crypt decorated with Carolingian frescoes—rare and precious, they likely date to the 8th century. Access to the crypt is restricted (heritage days or by request), but reproductions are visible.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The ruins of the 12th-century castle, destroyed in 1562 by Huguenot troops under Baron des Adrets, are laid out as a public garden. The keep's remains and lanes with golden stone houses, mullioned windows, and medieval vaults compose a village frozen in time.
⏱️ 20 min
4
Mont Thou (2,005 feet), the accessible high point of the Monts d'Or, offers exceptional panoramas over greater Lyon, the Saône plain, Beaujolais, and the Alpine chain—from Mont Blanc to Vercors. The orientation table at the summit helps identify peaks on clear days.
⏱️ 30 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Morning walk through old Ternand. The rampart walk, medieval lanes, and views over the Azergues valley can be explored in a good hour. Sample gamay with a village winemaker before hitting the road.
⏱️ 75 min
→
Afternoon hike from Mont Thou to Mont Cindre. The 6-mile loop connects both summits through forests, meadows, and heathland. Wild orchids in spring, dry-stone cabornes, and the Birds of Prey trail marked with panels about local species.
⏱️ 200 min
→
Mont d'Or villages—Saint-Cyr, Saint-Romain, Curis, Poleymieux—preserve surprising rural charm at the metropolis's doorstep. Golden stone houses, washhouses, and fountains compose a preserved Lyon countryside landscape.
⏱️ 30 min

Condrieu

2 photos
Day 9
🔍

Discover

1
Condrieu, ancient city of river merchants on the Rhône's right bank, had connections with the Phocaeans of Massalia. Its steep hillsides carry one of the Rhône Valley's most prestigious appellations: 540 acres of Viognier clinging to granite rock on narrow terraces supported by dry-stone walls—the 'chaillées.'
⏱️ 30 min
2
Condrieu's vineyard, an AOC since 1940, nearly vanished in the 20th century: after phylloxera, wars, and industrialization, only about 25 acres remained in the 1950s. Passionate winegrowers saved it by replanting abandoned hillsides in the 1980s. Viognier expresses itself here like nowhere else.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Rhône riverbanks at Condrieu offer a peaceful walk between river and vineyards. The landscape of terraced hillsides, supported by centuries-old walls, ranks among the most spectacular in French wine country. Across the river on the left bank, the village of Les Roches-de-Condrieu reflects in calm waters.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Three miles north, Ampuis is the birthplace of Côte-Rôtie appellation, one of the world's greatest Syrah crus. Slopes steepen to 60%! The 'cheys,' stone walls supporting the vines, sometimes date to Roman times. The landscape bears the Vignobles & Découvertes label.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Tasting of Condrieu and Côte-Rôtie in winegrowers' cellars. Condrieu, 100% Viognier, unfolds aromas of peach, apricot, and honeysuckle. Côte-Rôtie, Syrah on vertiginous slopes, is an age-worthy red of remarkable depth.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Book 48 hours ahead, especially in high season
→
Walk through Condrieu's terraced vineyards. Trails between dry-stone chaillées lead to natural viewpoints over the Rhône valley. Work is almost entirely manual—mechanization is impossible on these slopes.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Closed-toe shoes recommended, steep trails
→
Water sports on the Rhône and Condrieu lake. Paddleboarding, kayaking, wakeboarding, and inflatable obstacle courses at the leisure base. The water ski cable system lets you learn water skiing without a boat.
⏱️ 120 min
