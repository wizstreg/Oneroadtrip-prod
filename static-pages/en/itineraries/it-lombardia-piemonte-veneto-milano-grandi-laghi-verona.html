Milan, Great Lakes and Verona Itinerary: 17 Days between Art and Nature

From Milan's Duomo to the Borromean Islands, from Bellagio's villas to Verona's Arena: a complete journey through Northern Italy.

This 17-day itinerary takes you through Northern Italy, from Milan to the shores of the great Alpine lakes all the way to Verona. Visit world-renowned art museums, historic villas and picturesque villages on Lakes Como, Maggiore and Garda. A complete journey between culture, landscapes and history.

  • Milan: Duomo, Leonardo's Last Supper and Pinacoteca di Brera
  • Lake Como: Bellagio, Varenna and Villa Carlotta
  • Lake Maggiore: Borromean Islands, Stresa and Villa Taranto
  • Lake Garda: Sirmione, Desenzano and medieval fortresses
  • Bergamo and Brescia: art cities with UNESCO heritage
  • Verona: Roman Arena, Juliet's House and historic center

1
Day

Milano

Fashion capital with Gothic Duomo and Leonardo's Last Supper

10 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Milan's Duomo, the Gothic cathedral that dominates the main square. The building has 3,500 statues, slender spires and the golden Madonnina standing 4 meters tall. The cathedral is dedicated to Santa Maria Nascente. Climb to the terraces to see Milan and the Alps on clear days.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Walk through the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, a covered passage opened in 1867 that connects Piazza Duomo with Piazza della Scala. The gallery has an iron and glass dome and was designed by architect Giuseppe Mengoni. It houses luxury boutiques, historic cafés and mosaic floors.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the Museo del Novecento in the Palazzo dell'Arengario, which holds over 400 works of 20th-century Italian art. The collection includes Pellizza da Volpedo's Quarto Stato and works by Boccioni, Modigliani and Fontana. The museum presents movements like Futurism, Metaphysics and Conceptual Art. Lucio Fontana's room offers views of the Duomo.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll along the Navigli, the canal system also designed by Leonardo da Vinci to connect Milan with the lakes. The Darsena has been recently restored. The neighborhood offers artisan workshops, bars and a lively atmosphere.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Day

Milano

Fashion capital with Gothic Duomo and Leonardo's Last Supper

10 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
Admire Leonardo da Vinci's Last Supper in the refectory of Santa Maria delle Grazie. Leonardo painted the Last Supper between 1494 and 1498 using an innovative technique. The work shows Leonardo's mastery in composition and character expression. The visit requires advance booking.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit the Pinacoteca di Brera, a museum founded in 1776 with one of Italy's most important collections. You can see Caravaggio's Supper at Emmaus, Mantegna's Dead Christ, works by Piero della Francesca and Raphael. Hayez's The Kiss is the iconic work of Italian Romanticism.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the Church of San Maurizio al Monastero Maggiore, called Milan's Sistine Chapel. The frescoes cover 4,000 square meters and are the work of 16th-century Lombard masters, including Bernardino Luini. The simple facade on Corso Magenta hides an interior rich in decorations.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Visit the Castello Sforzesco, one of Europe's largest fortresses. The castle houses Michelangelo's Pietà Rondanini and Leonardo's frescoes. Behind the castle lies Parco Sempione, which extends to the Arch of Peace.
⏱️ 60 min

Day

Como

Elegant city on the lake's southern shore with medieval historic center

5 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Como's Duomo, a cathedral built between 1396 and 1740. The Renaissance facade features elaborate sculptures and decorations. The interior holds artworks and a dome designed by Filippo Juvarra in 1730.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Take the funicular up to the village of Brunate, located at 720 meters altitude. The 7-minute journey starts from Como's center. From Brunate you can see the lake and the Alps. The village has narrow streets and Liberty-style villas.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit Villa Olmo, a neoclassical residence built in 1797 for the Odescalchi family. The villa overlooks the lake and has a large Italian garden. It hosts art exhibitions and cultural events.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Walk through Como's historic center with its medieval squares and Renaissance palaces. The city preserves Roman remains and Romanesque churches. The lakefront offers views of the surrounding mountains.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Take a boat trip on Lake Como to see historic villas and picturesque villages. The navigation service connects the lake's main towns. The route offers views of the mountains and villa architecture.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Walk along Como's medieval walls, built in the 12th century. The walls start from Porta Torre, one of Italy's best-preserved towers at 40 meters tall. The route crosses the historic center.
⏱️ 60 min

Bellagio

The pearl of Lake Como with historic villas and panoramic gardens

6 photos
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Basilica San Giacomo, a 12th-century Romanesque church located in Bellagio's center. The interior preserves ancient frescoes and an apse with original decorations. The bell tower is one of the village's symbols.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore Villa Melzi d'Eril, a neoclassical residence built in 1808. The English garden extends along the lake with rare plants, statues and a museum housing Napoleonic works. The Moorish chapel is a distinctive feature.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit Villa Serbelloni, located on the hill above Bellagio. The terraced gardens offer panoramic views of the lake and mountains. The property belongs to the Rockefeller Foundation and is visited through guided tours.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk along Bellagio's lakefront among craft shops, cafés and boutiques. The village sits at the point where the three arms of Lake Como meet. The narrow alleys climb from the shore up the hill.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Take the ferry to Varenna or Menaggio to see other lake towns. Boats regularly connect villages on different shores. The boat trip lets you appreciate the beauty of the lake landscape.
⏱️ 60 min

Varenna

Colorful village with medieval castle and romantic lake walk

6 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Villa Monastero, a former residence transformed into a museum and conference center. The botanical garden extends for 2 kilometers along the lake with exotic plants and panoramic terraces. The villa houses art collections and scientific instruments.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore Vezio Castle, a medieval fortress that dominates Varenna from above. The castle offers 360-degree views of Lake Como. In the gardens live hawks and birds of prey that you can see during demonstrations.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the Church of San Giorgio, a Baroque building constructed in the 14th century. The interior preserves frescoes and a polyptych from the Lombard school. The bell tower can be seen from all over the village.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk the Lovers' Walk, a panoramic path that follows the lake between Varenna and Fiumelatte. The route passes through gardens, stairs and colorful houses. The lake view accompanies the entire walk.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit the village of Varenna with its steep alleys and pastel-colored houses. The historic center maintains the atmosphere of a fishing village. From the main square, stairs lead up to the castle.
⏱️ 60 min

Menaggio

Ideal base for exploring the lake and surrounding mountains

4 photos
Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Villa Carlotta in Tremezzo, an 18th-century residence famous for its botanical garden. The villa houses works by Canova and Hayez. The garden has over 150 varieties of azaleas and rhododendrons that bloom in spring.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore the Church of Santo Stefano in Menaggio, a 17th-century Baroque building. The interior preserves period paintings and decorations. The bell tower dominates the village center.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk along Menaggio's lakefront to the Lido, the public beach with pool. The route offers views of the lake and mountains. Menaggio is a starting point for mountain excursions.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Drive up to Rifugio Menaggio at 1,400 meters altitude. The route crosses forests and offers panoramic views. From the refuge, trails depart for mountain walks.

Day 7

3 photos
Day 8
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Sanctuary of Madonna del Carmine in Luino, a 17th-century Baroque building. The interior preserves period frescoes and decorations. The sanctuary is located in the village center.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore Luino's lakefront, which extends for several kilometers. The port is the departure point for boats crossing the lake. On Wednesdays, a large market takes place along the entire lakefront.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Visit Luino's weekly market, held every Wednesday. The market is one of Northern Italy's largest with over 350 stalls. You can find food products, clothing and local crafts.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Take the boat to cross Lake Maggiore and reach Cannobio or other towns on the opposite shore. The navigation service offers regular routes. The journey lets you see the lake from a different perspective.

Laveno Mombello

Ceramic center with cable car to Sasso del Ferro

4 photos
Day 9
🔍

Discover

1
Take the cable car up to Sasso del Ferro, a mountain offering panoramic views of Lake Maggiore. The cable car departs from Laveno's center. From the top you can see the Alps and the lake. The restaurant at altitude serves typical dishes.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit MIDeC, the ceramics museum that tells Laveno's ceramic tradition. The museum displays historic and contemporary pieces. Laveno has been an important ceramic production center since the 19th century.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Cross the lake by ferry from Laveno to Verbania. The ferry service also transports cars. The crossing takes about 15 minutes and offers beautiful lake views.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Walk along Laveno's lakefront with views of the surrounding mountains. The port is the arrival and departure point for ferries. The village center preserves historic buildings related to ceramic production.

Orta San Giulio

Medieval lakeside village with island and UNESCO Sacro Monte

9 photos
Day 10
🔍

Discover

1
Visit San Giulio Island, a small island in the center of Lake Orta accessible by boat. The island houses the Basilica San Giulio, founded in the 4th century and rebuilt over the centuries. The meditation path circles the island with 20 reflection panels.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore the Sacro Monte of Orta, a complex of 20 chapels built between 1591 and 1770. The chapels contain frescoes and statues depicting episodes from the life of St. Francis. The Sacro Monte is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Stroll through the medieval village of Orta San Giulio with its narrow streets and aristocratic palaces. Piazza Motta is the heart of the village, overlooking the lake with views of the island. The Palazzo della Comunità has 16th-century frescoes.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk along Lake Orta's shore to Pella or other nearby villages. The route offers views of the lake and San Giulio Island. The villages along the lake maintain a peaceful atmosphere.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Climb up to the Rocca of Orta, a hill offering panoramic views of the lake and village. The Rocca park is a quiet place for a walk. The view from above allows you to appreciate the lake's shape.
⏱️ 60 min
🚗

Stresa

Elegant tourist resort with access to the Borromean Islands

5 photos
Day 11
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Borromean Islands, an archipelago on Lake Maggiore accessible by boat from Stresa. Isola Bella has a 17th-century Baroque palace and Italian terraced gardens. Isola Madre houses a botanical garden with rare plants and a palace with historic collections. Isola dei Pescatori preserves its character as a fishing village.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Take the cable car up to Mottarone, a 1,491-meter mountain between Lake Maggiore and Lake Orta. From the summit you can see 7 lakes and the Alps. Mottarone offers ski slopes in winter and trails for excursions in summer.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit Villa Pallavicino in Stresa, a 19th-century residence with a 20-hectare park. The botanical garden hosts over 40 animal species in semi-freedom. The park has tree-lined avenues and lake views.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll along Stresa's lakefront, which extends for several kilometers. The route passes historic villas and Belle Époque hotels. The view of the Borromean Islands accompanies the entire walk.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit Stresa's center with its elegant streets and shops. Piazza Cadorna is the heart of the village, where the pier for the islands is also located. The village has a long tourist tradition since the 19th century.
⏱️ 60 min
🚗

Verbania

Garden city with Villa Taranto and nature reserves

2 photos
Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Villa Taranto in Verbania, a botanical garden created in 1931 by Captain Neil McEacharn. The park has over 20,000 plant species from around the world. The gardens extend over 16 hectares with fountains, terraces and greenhouses. Villa Taranto is considered one of Europe's most beautiful gardens.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore the Fondotoce Nature Reserve, a protected area at the mouth of the Toce river into Lake Maggiore. The reserve hosts numerous aquatic bird species. The paths allow you to observe the wetland's fauna and flora.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk through the village of Pallanza, part of Verbania, with its lakefront and historic center. The village preserves ancient churches and aristocratic palaces. The weekly market takes place in the main square.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit Intra, the other part of Verbania, with its industrial port and commercial center. Intra's lakefront offers a peaceful walk. From here ferries depart for other lake towns.
⏱️ 60 min
🚗

Bergamo

Medieval upper town with UNESCO Venetian walls

3 photos
Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Città Alta, Bergamo's medieval historic center surrounded by Venetian walls. The walls have been a UNESCO World Heritage site since 2017. Piazza Vecchia is the heart of the upper town with the Palazzo della Ragione and Torre Civica.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Enter the Basilica of Santa Maria Maggiore, a 12th-century Romanesque church with Baroque interior. The basilica preserves precious tapestries and the organ played by Gaetano Donizetti. The adjacent Colleoni Chapel houses the tomb of condottiero Bartolomeo Colleoni.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the Accademia Carrara, an art museum founded in 1796 with works from the 15th to 19th centuries. The collection includes paintings by Botticelli, Mantegna, Raphael and Canaletto. The museum reopened in 2015 after complete restoration.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Climb the Torre Civica (Campanone), a 52-meter tower offering views of Bergamo and the plain. The tower dates from the 12th century. Every evening at 10 PM, the bells ring 100 times.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Take the funicular from Città Bassa to Città Alta. The funicular has been operating since 1887 and takes just a few minutes. The route offers views of the modern city and surrounding hills.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Walk along the Venetian walls that surround Città Alta. The route along the walls offers views of the lower town and mountains. The gates and bastions are well preserved.
⏱️ 60 min

Brescia

Roman and Lombard city with UNESCO monastic complex

4 photos
Day 14
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Santa Giulia Complex, a city museum located in a former Lombard monastery. The complex has been a UNESCO World Heritage site since 2011. The museum preserves Roman, Lombard and medieval remains. Desiderius's Cross is one of the most precious pieces.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore the Archaeological Park of Roman Brescia with the Capitolium, a 1st-century AD Roman temple. The archaeological area includes a Roman theater and remains of public buildings. The Capitolium preserves original statues and decorations.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the Duomo Nuovo and Duomo Vecchio in Piazza Paolo VI. The Duomo Vecchio is an 11th-century Romanesque rotunda with medieval crypt. The Duomo Nuovo has an 80-meter dome built between the 17th and 19th centuries.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Climb up to Brescia Castle, a medieval fortress dominating the city from Cidneo hill. The castle has Venetian walls and medieval towers. The Arms Museum preserves a collection of historic weapons. The view from the top reaches Lake Garda.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk through Piazza della Loggia, a 16th-century Renaissance square designed in Venetian style. The Loggia is the town hall palace. The square has arcades with shops and cafés. The Clock Tower has automatons that strike the hours.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Walk along Via dei Musei, an ancient Roman road that crosses the historic center. The street connects the Capitolium with the Monastery of Santa Giulia. Along the route are historic buildings and Roman remains.
⏱️ 60 min
🚗

Sirmione

Lake Garda peninsula with Scaliger castle and Roman villa

6 photos
Day 15
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Grottoes of Catullus, remains of a 1st-century AD Roman villa. The archaeological site extends over 2 hectares at the tip of the Sirmione peninsula. The villa had thermal rooms and lake views. The museum displays finds from the excavations.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore the Scaliger Castle, a medieval fortress built in the 13th century by the Della Scala family of Verona. The castle has well-preserved towers and battlements. You can climb the walls to see the lake and historic center. The inner port is unique in Italy.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the Church of San Pietro in Mavino, an 8th-century Romanesque building located on a hill above Sirmione. The interior preserves frescoes from the 12th to 16th centuries. The bell tower is visible from many points in the village.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk through Sirmione's historic center with its narrow streets and shops. The village sits on a peninsula jutting into Lake Garda. Sirmione's sulfurous thermal waters have been known since Roman times.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Take a boat tour around the peninsula to see Sirmione from the water. Tourist boats depart from the port and circle the peninsula. The tour shows the Grottoes of Catullus, the castle and the villas on the lake.
⏱️ 60 min

Desenzano Del Garda

Lake's main town with historic port and nightlife

4 photos
Day 16
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Desenzano's Roman Villa, a 4th-century AD residence with well-preserved polychrome mosaics. The mosaics cover over 240 square meters and depict hunting scenes and daily life. The archaeological site shows the structure of the Roman villa.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Climb up to Desenzano Castle, a medieval fortress dominating the historic center. The castle has origins in the 10th century and was rebuilt several times. The walls offer panoramic views of Lake Garda. Inside are archaeological remains.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the Duomo Santa Maria Maddalena, a 16th-century church designed by Giulio Todeschini. The interior preserves paintings by Giovanni Battista Tiepolo and Andrea Celesti. The 60-meter bell tower is a landmark for the village.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk along Desenzano's Porto Vecchio, the largest and oldest port on Lake Garda. The port is surrounded by cafés and restaurants. In the evening, the lakefront comes alive with bars and shops open late.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Walk through Piazza Malvezzi, Desenzano's main square with Venetian buildings and arcades. The weekly market is held on Tuesdays. The square connects the historic center with the lakefront.
⏱️ 60 min

Peschiera Del Garda

UNESCO Venetian fortress at the Mincio river outlet

4 photos
Day 17
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Fortress of Peschiera del Garda, a defensive system that surrounds the historic center. The walls have been a UNESCO World Heritage site since 2017. The fortress was built by the Venetians in the 16th century and reinforced by the Austrians in the 19th century. You can enter through Porta Verona or Porta Brescia.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit the Sanctuary of Madonna del Frassino, a church built between 1511 and 1514. The sanctuary is located at the foot of the hills a few kilometers from Peschiera. The interior has a nave with two chapels decorated with frescoes. The sanctuary is kept by the Franciscan Friars Minor.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk along Lungolago Mazzini, a panoramic route of about 700 meters from the historic center to Lido dei Cappuccini. The lakefront is lined with bars and restaurants. The lake view is especially beautiful at sunset.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Go to Lido Cappuccini, a beach establishment where you can rent sunbeds and pedal boats. The beach has pebbles and clear water. The lido offers lifeguard service and a restaurant on the shore.
⏱️ 60 min

Day

Verona

City of Romeo and Juliet with Roman arena and historic center

5 photos
Day 18
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Verona Arena, a Roman amphitheater built in 30 AD. The arena could hold up to 30,000 spectators. Today it's one of the most important venues for operas and outdoor concerts. The acoustics are excellent and the atmosphere unique.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit Juliet's House on Via Cappello, the symbolic place of the love story of Romeo and Juliet. The courtyard has the famous balcony and the bronze statue of Juliet by Nereo Costantini. According to tradition, touching the statue brings good luck in love.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Explore Castelvecchio and the Scaliger Bridge, a medieval fortress with a fortified bridge over the Adige river. The museum preserves collections of medieval and Renaissance art in 29 rooms. The bridge offers beautiful views of the river.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Visit the Basilica of San Zeno Maggiore, a Romanesque church built between 967 and 1398. The bronze doors have 12th-century reliefs. The frescoes and Mantegna's altarpiece are important works. According to tradition, Romeo and Juliet married in the crypt.
⏱️ 75 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Walk through Piazza delle Erbe, Verona's central square surrounded by colorful buildings and frescoed palaces. The market is held every day with stalls selling fruit, vegetables and souvenirs. In the center stands the fountain of Madonna Verona.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit the Giusti Garden, a 16th-century Italian garden with geometric flower beds and centuries-old cypress trees. The garden has grottoes and terraces offering views of Verona. It's one of Italy's most beautiful Renaissance gardens.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Cross Ponte Pietra, an ancient Roman bridge connecting the historic center with the Veronetta district. The bridge offers beautiful views of the Adige river, the Roman Theater and San Pietro hill. It's an ideal place for a sunset walk.
⏱️ 60 min
