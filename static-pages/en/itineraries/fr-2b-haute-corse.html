Upper Corsica Road Trip: Cap Corse and Balagne

Where turquoise seas meet jagged peaks, Upper Corsica unveils Genoese towers, fishing harbors, and hilltop villages echoing with ancient Corsican songs.

📅 Best period May, June, September, October
🚗 Recommended vehicle Touring car or camper van

Journey through Upper Corsica from Bastia to the wild Cap Corse, discover Saint-Florent and the Agriates Desert, explore the artisan villages of Balagne, the citadel of Calvi, and the Restonica gorges in the island's heart.

  • Bastia: Old Harbor and citadel facing the Tyrrhenian Sea
  • Cap Corse: Genoese towers and the customs officers' trail
  • Agriates Desert: Paradise beaches of Saleccia and Lotu
  • L'Île-Rousse: Covered market and the red rocks of La Pietra
  • Calvi: Citadel overlooking the Gulf of Balagne
  • Corte: Restonica gorges and mountain lakes

Bastia

Discover

1
The Old Harbor unfolds its ochre and salmon facades around quays where fishing boats bob gently in the water. Dominated by the Church of Saint-Jean-Baptiste with its twin bell towers soaring 70 meters high, it invites leisurely strolls along the Quai des Martyrs de la Libération. Bar and restaurant terraces come alive from early morning when the boats return with their catch.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Terra Nova Citadel towers over the sea from its rocky promontory. Founded in 1380 by Genoese governor Leonello Lomellini, it houses the Palais des Gouverneurs with its ochre facade now home to the Bastia Museum. Through the cobbled lanes, the baroque Sainte-Marie Cathedral (17th-18th centuries) reveals its marble and stucco work, including the monumental statue of the Assumption cast in solid silver weighing nearly half a ton.
⏱️ 120 min
3
The Oratory of Sainte-Croix preserves its venerated Black Christ. Built in 1542, this baroque edifice contrasts with the austerity of the ramparts. The Romieu Garden connects the Citadel to the Old Port via a monumental staircase planted with Judas trees, Chinese parasol trees, and spindle trees offering shaded respite.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Place Saint-Nicolas stretches 300 meters long and 90 meters wide, making it one of France's largest public squares. Bordered by plane trees and palms, it features the war memorial, a statue of Napoleon dressed as a Roman emperor, and a music pavilion constructed in the late 19th century.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Aldilonda, a suspended walkway above the sea, hugs the citadel's rocky base for 200 meters. Inaugurated in 2020, it offers spectacular views of the ramparts and continues as the Spassimare promenade all the way to Arinella beach. Best experienced at sunrise over the Tyrrhenian Sea.
⏱️ 75 min
→
The market at Place du Marché buzzes to life on Saturday and Sunday mornings. Corsican producers offer charcuterie, sheep's cheese, maquis honey, and fresh vegetables. The authentic local atmosphere reveals Bastia far from the tourist circuits.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Arinella beach spreads its fine sand along Bastia's southern edge. The only true urban beach in the city, it's easily reached on foot or by bike via the Spassimare promenade. Clear waters and nearby amenities make it a popular spot for a swim break.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Erbalunga spreads its fishermen's houses tightly across a rocky peninsula. The ruined 16th-century Genoese tower dominates the small harbor while salt-weathered facades reveal authentic charm. The baroque-fronted Church of Saint-Érasme protects the processional crosses used during Holy Week.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The hamlets of Brando climb in tiers through the valley: Castello with its feudal castle ruins, Pozzo and its flagstone quarries, Mausoleu and its cipolin marble quarries. The Chapel of Notre-Dame-des-Neiges preserves 14th-century frescoes. A journey back in time among fountains and dry-stone washhouses.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Lavasina sanctuary has drawn pilgrims for centuries. The ex-voto museum can be visited by reservation and tells the story of the miracle. The former Franciscan convent overlooks a small pebble beach facing the sea.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
The coastal path from Erbalunga winds along coves between maquis and black rocks. Kayak rentals available at the harbor for exploring hidden caves and wild inlets. Diving clubs offer trips to the protected waters of the Cap Corse Marine Natural Park.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Erbalunga beach and Campinca cove offer swimming in a pristine setting. Fine sand and transparent waters bordered by rocks. Family-friendly atmosphere far from the crowds of beach resorts.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Rogliano gathers seven hamlets scattered across the mountains between 110 and 500 meters elevation. Bettolacce, Magna, Vignale, and Olivo reveal medieval chapels and square towers. The Church of San Michele in Bettolacce features a bell tower separated from the main building following the Pisan tradition.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Macinaggio centers around its marina, the largest in Cap Corse. This is where Pasquale Paoli landed in 1790 after 20 years in exile. The marina hosts shops and artisans in a peaceful atmosphere. Offshore, the Finocchiarola islands are classified as a nature reserve.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Barcaggio spreads its blonde sand beach at the tip of Cap Corse. The isolated hamlet faces the Giraglia islet crowned by its lighthouse. Corsican cows sometimes graze on the sand. The end-of-the-world atmosphere contrasts sharply with the bustle of the east coast.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
The customs officers' trail runs from Macinaggio toward Centuri for 35 kilometers of wild coastline. The first section to Tamarone beach crosses maquis and secret coves. Count on 3 hours to Barcaggio with spectacular views of the sea and islands.
⏱️ 180 min
→
The beaches of Macinaggio and Tamarone attract swimmers with their crystalline waters. Fine sand bordered by pines and eucalyptus. Popular snorkeling spot for observing fish and rocky seabeds in the marine reserve.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
The harbor of Centuri nestles in a cove lined with colorful houses. France's premier lobster fishing port, multicolored nets dry on the quays. Slate roofs and ochre facades compose a picturesque tableau. The round Genoese tower has stood watch since the 16th century near the Chapel of Saint-Antoine.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The seven hamlets of Centuri stretch in an arc between 110 and 130 meters elevation. Camera reveals the Church of Saint-Sylvestre from the 6th century with its separate square bell tower and the Chapel of Sainte-Croix. Ortinola houses the blue Mattei house, an "American's home" built by Corsicans who returned from Puerto Rico.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Mattei Mill dominates the western point of the Cape from the D80. Restored in the early 20th century, it symbolizes the Cap Corse aperitif created by Louis-Napoléon Mattei in 1872. Exceptional panorama over the sea on both sides of the Cape and the village of Centuri below.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The harbor restaurants serve freshly caught lobster, simply grilled or tossed with pasta. A culinary tradition perpetuated for centuries by fishing families. Reservations recommended in high season.
⏱️ 90 min
→
The Roman road connects Centuri to Macinaggio via the Col de Cataro at 192 meters. An old mule track crossing abandoned sheepfolds and fragrant maquis. A 12-kilometer section lined with dry-stone walls bearing witness to past agricultural activity.
⏱️ 240 min

Discover

1
The Saint-Florent Citadel overlooks the gulf from its 1440 construction. A circular edifice 30 meters in diameter flanked by round and square towers, built by Janus Campofregoso to survey the bay. The interior is open for visits in July and August. The esplanade offers panoramic views of the harbor and the Cap Corse coastline.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The Church of Santa Maria Assunta, former cathedral of the Nebbio, rises at the edge of Saint-Florent. A 12th-century Romanesque building classified as a Historic Monument since 1840. Sober and elegant architecture typical of the Pisan style. Limestone golden in the Mediterranean sun.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The historic center unfolds its lanes around Place Paoli bordered by century-old plane trees. Founded in the 18th century by Pasquale Paoli as a free port to rival Genoese Calvi, Saint-Florent preserves its grid layout. The covered market offers local products and crafts.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Agriates Desert stretches across 15,000 hectares between Saint-Florent and L'Ostriconi. Maritime shuttles reach the beaches of Lotu and Saleccia several times daily from the harbor. Turquoise waters, white sand, and maquis compose a paradisiacal landscape. Return trips available late afternoon.
⏱️ 240 min
→
The Patrimonio wine route crosses vineyards and estates just 10 minutes from Saint-Florent. AOC Patrimonio produces powerful reds, fresh whites from Vermentinu grapes, and sweet Muscat wines. Seven communes display their limestone terraces. Tastings available at the cellars.
⏱️ 120 min
→
La Roya beach borders the town center with fine sand and calm waters. Ideal for families with a supervised swimming area. Starting point for the customs trail toward the Agriates beaches. Parking available nearby in low season.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Place Paoli spreads its century-old plane trees through the heart of L'Île-Rousse. Founded in 1758 by Pasquale Paoli to compete with Genoese Calvi, the town retains its orthogonal layout. The covered market with its 21 columns in antique style, built between 1844 and 1846 and classified as a Historic Monument, hosts stalls of charcuterie, cheese, and honey every morning.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The island of La Pietra is reached by a causeway from the marina. Red porphyry rocks give the town its name (Isola Rossa in Italian). The white lighthouse built in 1875, fully automated and solar-powered, offers panoramic views of the coast and the islets of Roccio, Roccetto, and Piano. The 15th-century Genoese tower stands watch over the waves.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Church of the Immaculate Conception overlooks Place Paoli. Inaugurated in 1893, partially destroyed by fire in 1914, then rebuilt in 1935. Architecture blending baroque and neoclassical styles. Two 17th-century paintings from the Fesch collection adorn the interior.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Marinella promenade runs along Napoléon beach on the waterfront. A pedestrian path beneath palms with views of the island of La Pietra. The bronze Siren statue (A Sirenella di l'Isula Rossa) has perched on a granite rock since 2016. Best at sunset when the rocks turn crimson.
⏱️ 60 min
→
The beach train connects L'Île-Rousse to Calvi along the coast. U trinichellu stops at all the Balagne beaches: Bodri, Ghjunchitu, Algajola. A scenic journey at leisurely pace discovering coves and maquis. A relaxing alternative to driving for exploring the coastline.
⏱️ 180 min
→
The beaches of Bodri and Ghjunchitu stretch to the southwest 3-5 kilometers away. The finest sand in Corsica, translucent waters. Naturist zone at the northern end of Bodri. Access via coastal path from L'Île-Rousse or paid parking in season.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Pigna perches at 400 meters elevation overlooking the Balagne valley. The quintessential artisan village, its lanes reveal workshops of potters, glass blowers, luthiers, and sculptors. The Casa Musicale perpetuates the tradition of Corsican polyphonic singing and hosts concerts and workshops.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Sant'Antonino crowns its peak at 500 meters, one of Corsica's oldest villages. Classified among France's Most Beautiful Villages, its granite houses spiral around the rocky pinnacle. Vaulted lanes and covered passages create a medieval labyrinth. 360-degree panorama over Balagne and the sea.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Corbara terraces its ochre houses between olive groves and vineyards. The 19th-century Convent of Saint-Dominique dominates the village. The baroque Church of the Annunciation preserves paintings and sculptures. The terraces offer plunging views of the sea and L'Île-Rousse.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Balagne artisan route winds through Pigna, Corbara, Lumio, and Sant'Antonino. Workshops open to visitors offer demonstrations of ancestral techniques: pottery, ceramics, lutherie, woodworking. A hilltop village atmosphere far from mass tourism.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Polyphonic music concerts are organized at the Casa Musicale in Pigna. Male voices a cappella interpreting traditional Corsican repertoire. Reservations recommended for summer evening performances. Intimate atmosphere beneath stone vaults.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Panoramic hikes depart from the villages toward the Balagne ridges. Marked trails crossing maquis and century-old olive groves. Sea views at every turn. Easy to moderate difficulty depending on chosen routes.
⏱️ 150 min

Discover

1
The Citadel of Calvi towers over the gulf from its rocky spur. A 15th-century Genoese fortress, it preserves ramparts, bastions, and cobbled lanes. The Palais des Gouverneurs and the baroque Church of Saint-Jean-Baptiste dominate Terra Nova. Exceptional panorama over the bay, Balagne, and all the way to Monte Cinto on clear days.
⏱️ 120 min
2
Legend claims that Christopher Columbus was born in Calvi in a house within the citadel. Though controversial, this tradition endures through commemorative plaques and local names. The town proudly maintains this link to the famous navigator.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The marina animates the lower town with its quays lined with bars and restaurants. A modern harbor welcoming sailboats and yachts facing the citadel. Cosmopolitan atmosphere reminiscent of Saint-Tropez. Colorful facades reflected in the calm basin waters.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Calvi beach stretches 4.5 kilometers of fine sand bordered by maritime pines. Shallow turquoise waters ideal for families. Water sports clubs offer sailing, kayaking, and paddleboarding. The pedestrian promenade extends to the Pointe de la Revellata, accessible on foot or by bike.
⏱️ 150 min
→
The Calvi on the Rocks Festival takes over the citadel each summer. Electronic stages and concerts facing the sea attract international artists. Festive atmosphere within the historic rampart setting. Varied programming from June through September.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Maritime excursions to the Scandola Reserve depart from Calvi harbor. A UNESCO site with red porphyry cliffs plunging into crystalline seas. Marine caves, natural arches, and protected wildlife. Full-day trip with a swimming stop at Girolata.
⏱️ 360 min

Discover

1
The Citadel of Corte rises from its rocky pinnacle 100 meters above the town. An eagle's nest built in the 15th century, it houses the Museum of Corsica tracing island history and traditions. From the Belvedere terrace, plunging views of the confluence of the Tavignano and Restonica rivers between mountains.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The old town climbs in cobbled lanes up to the citadel. Capital of Pasquale Paoli from 1755 to 1769 during independent Corsica, Corte preserves the soul of island resistance. The University of Corsica, founded by Paoli and reopened in 1981, brings life to this mountain city.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Chapel of Sainte-Croix and the Four Cannons fountain mark the old town. Genoese and Corsican architecture intermingle in the stone facades. Place Paoli at the center, shaded squares, and student bars give life to this mountain town.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Restonica Gorges climb 15 kilometers from Corte toward the mountain lakes. A spectacular road serpentining between rock walls and Laricio pine forests. The Grotelle sheepfolds at 1,370 meters mark the starting point for hikes to Lakes Melo and Capitello.
⏱️ 180 min
→
The hike to Lake Melo then Capitello starts from the Grotelle sheepfolds. 1.5 hours climbing to Lake Melo nestled at 1,711 meters in a granite cirque. An additional 45 minutes to Lake Capitello at 1,930 meters. Rocky passages with chains. Glacial waters reflecting the summits.
⏱️ 300 min
→
The natural pools of the Restonica invite swimming in basins of rushing water. Several spots accessible from the road before the sheepfolds. Cool waters and rocks polished by the torrent. Heavy summer crowds, prefer mornings or late afternoons.
⏱️ 120 min
