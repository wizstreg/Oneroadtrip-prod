Discover

1
Wander through Old Valence with its cobblestone lanes, weathered stone houses, and shaded squares. The monumental fountain stands as the city's iconic symbol. This historic center, designated a City and Land of Art and History, brims with architectural treasures waiting to be discovered.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit Valence's oldest monument, built between 1063 and 1099. Pope Urban II consecrated this Romanesque cathedral in 1095. The building showcases characteristic Auvergnat Romanesque architecture with its imposing bell tower-porch.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Marvel at this Renaissance masterpiece built around 1530. The mansion takes its name from the numerous sculpted heads adorning its façade: busts of Roman emperors, medallions of the Church Fathers, and allegorical figures. The inner courtyard is freely accessible year-round.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Stroll across this vast tree-lined esplanade created in the 1770s. This panoramic terrace overlooks the Rhône River and offers spectacular views of the Vivarais mountains and the ruins of Crussol Castle. The site houses the famous Peynet Kiosk, a listed Historical Monument.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
Discover this imposing collegiate church built from 837 onwards. Listed as a Historical Monument since 1840, it blends Romanesque and Gothic architecture. Admire the 14th-century murals in the choir and the embroidered tapestry of the Mystery of the Passion from the 16th century.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Immerse yourself in 4,000 years of footwear history in this former 17th-century convent. Romans was France's luxury shoe capital. The museum displays 16,000 pairs from around the world, including creations by Charles Jourdan and André Pérugia.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Wander the cobbled streets of the historic center, designated a City and Land of Art and History. Admire the 47-meter-high Jacquemart Tower built in 1370, and visit the Old Bridge spanning the Isère since the 11th century. The Sunday morning market was voted France's favorite market.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Explore this medieval village ranked among France's Most Beautiful Villages and voted the French People's Favorite Village 2025. Founded in 1070 around the abbey that became a major pilgrimage site. Navigate the maze of picturesque cobbled alleys and discover artisan workshops.
⏱️ 75 min

Discover

1
Climb the 257 steps leading to the summit of France's tallest keep. Standing 52 meters high, the Tower of Crest is an architectural masterpiece comprising three nested towers. Transformed into a state prison from the 17th to 19th centuries, it held Protestants, libertines, and insurgents. From the top, enjoy exceptional 360° panoramas over the Drôme valley, Saou forest, and Vercors mountains.
⏱️ 150 min
2
Wander the narrow, winding alleys of the medieval old town that snake beneath the tower. Discover calades paved with river stones, covered passages, and remnants of ancient ramparts. The Saint-Sauveur church with its Romanesque bell tower stands as a remarkable edifice.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Experience something unique with the immersive audio system. A 19th-century guard guides you through nine centuries of history. Discover prisoners' living conditions, escape attempts, and hundreds of poignant graffiti covering the walls.
⏱️ 150 min
→
For the truly daring, attempt the 44-meter rappel down the east wall, reliving Philippe Rivoire's 1789 escape attempt. Supervised by certified instructors, you'll enjoy an incredible view over Crest.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Take the Ramparts Path that climbs from downtown to the Tower. This steep ascent offers beautiful valley views. Below, stroll along the emerald-watered Drôme riverbanks.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Admire these exceptional 3rd-century ramparts, witnesses to the grandeur of Dea Augusta Vocontiorum. These fortifications extend nearly 2 kilometers with a thickness of 3.6 meters and rise up to 14 meters high. The Saint-Marcel Gate, the only Roman monument still standing in the department, ranks among France's best-preserved.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Visit this listed cathedral built between the 12th and 13th centuries atop the vestiges of an ancient Roman temple. The building blends Romanesque and Gothic architecture. The remarkable 12th-century Romanesque portal ranks among the finest in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Discover an exceptional treasure in this chapel of the former episcopal palace: the 12th-century Romanesque mosaic of the Four Rivers. This unique altar carpet in France offers a symbolic representation of the universe according to medieval cosmogony.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Housed in an 18th-century mansion, the municipal museum designated a Museum of France holds archaeological collections dating back to the Neolithic. The Gallo-Roman period shines through tauroboic altars listed as Historical Monuments.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Head to the cellars to taste the famous Clairette de Die, France's first sparkling wine to receive PDO status in 1942. This naturally effervescent wine is crafted using the ancestral Diois method dating back to Gallo-Roman times. The Jaillance Cooperative Cellar, founded in 1950, brings together over 240 winemakers.
⏱️ 75 min
→
Wander through the medieval cobbled streets of the historic center with its colorful old houses. Admire the Protestant temple housed in a former 18th-century Jesuit chapel. Enjoy the Wednesday and Saturday morning markets to discover Clairette, Picodon PDO, and local products.
⏱️ 60 min
→
A few kilometers from Die, visit the Notre-Dame de Valcroissant Abbey, a listed monument founded in the late 12th century. Though partially ruined, it still presents a cloister surrounded by an abbey church in a preserved natural setting testifying to over 800 years of history.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Admire this elegant medieval bridge built in the 14th century that spans the Eygues with a single daring arch 43 meters long and 18 meters high. Construction began in 1340 but wasn't completed until 1409. Listed as a Historical Monument in 1925, it's a masterpiece of medieval engineering and Nyons' iconic symbol.
⏱️ 30 min
2
Wander the picturesque alleys of the old village with its ancient houses bathed in the warm atmosphere of Drôme Provençale. The Randonne Tower emerges from the rooftops, crowned with a curious pyramid of arcades topped by a Virgin statue. Nyons has earned the France's Most Beautiful Detours and Remarkable Taste Site labels.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Visit one of the historic olive oil mills at the foot of the Roman Bridge. The Dozol-Autrand Mill, established since 1750, has seen 14 generations of millers pass through. A video traces an olive's journey to its transformation into oil. The Vieux Moulins Mill offers unique heritage in France with two old mills from the 18th and 19th centuries and a soap factory.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Contemplate the olive groves stretching across the surrounding hills, often terraced. The Tanche olive, a unique local variety grown only in the Baronnies Provençales territory, can withstand temperatures down to -10°C. The Nyons black olive stands out with its monk's robe color and gentle taste with red fruit aromas. Nyons olive oil, France's first PDO oil in 1994, is characterized by its golden color and buttery taste.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the traditional mills and discover the complete olive oil production process. Possibly attend a demonstration of scourtins weaving at La Scourtinerie, France's only such workshop. Taste different Nyons PDO olive oils and preserved black olives. The ideal period to observe production is December through January during harvest and pressing.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Don't miss Thursday morning's colorful market, a true Nyons institution. Browse stalls overflowing with local products: Nyons PDO olives and oils, Picodon goat cheese, lavender honey, aromatic herbs. The market comes alive beneath century-old plane trees.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Discover local craft shops, art galleries, and creator workshops. Visit the World of Olives Institute and the Olive & Sens exhibition that engages all five senses to explore the olive universe. Culinary and cosmetic workshops centered on olives are regularly offered.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Visit the largest Renaissance château in southeastern France, perched on a 33-meter rocky promontory. Mentioned as early as the 11th century as a fortress, it was transformed during the Renaissance into a sumptuous residence. The Marquise de Sévigné stayed here during three long visits and died here in 1696. Admire the Adhémar Gallery, the Tinel hall, the Prelates' wing, and Madame de Grignan's chamber in the former medieval keep.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Stroll the famous terraces built atop the Saint-Sauveur collegiate church that offer unforgettable 360° panoramas of Provence. The view extends to La Lance mountain, Mont Ventoux, and the Dentelles de Montmirail. Madame de Sévigné wrote: 'It is even pleasant not to be tempted to leave your beautiful terraces.'
⏱️ 30 min
3
Explore this charming medieval village ranked among France's Most Beautiful Villages that hugs the contours of the rocky promontory. Reach the village via steep alleys where you can admire the 14th-century belfry and superb 15th-century houses. The village cultivates both its literary heritage and its terroir: PDO vineyards, truffle grounds, lavender fields.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Visit this collegiate church built in the 16th century, one of the first buildings listed as a Historical Monument in 1840. The Renaissance façade features two symmetrical square towers. Madame de Sévigné was interred in this collegiate church on April 18, 1696. She still rests in this history-laden place.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Discover Madame de Sévigné's apartments, the painting collection from the 16th to 20th centuries, and the interpretation space tracing the château's architectural evolution. Guided tours illuminate daily life of Provençal nobility in the 17th century. Possibly attend one of the many concerts held in the Adhémar Gallery.
⏱️ 75 min
→
Walk through the surrounding landscapes where vineyards, lavender, and truffle grounds create a typically Provençal mosaic of colors. In June-July, blooming lavender fields offer an unforgettable spectacle with the château as backdrop.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Visit producers directly and the village's fine restaurants to discover local gastronomy. Taste Grignan-les-Adhémar PDO wines, Côtes-du-Rhône, Tricastin black truffles, Nyons olive oil, and goat cheeses.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Perched on Montélimar's heights and accessible within minutes on foot from downtown, this château testifies to the Adhémar family's power. Built in the 12th century, it comprises a seigneurial residence, the 11th-century Saint-Pierre chapel, a square tower, and a fortified enclosure. From the keep, access the sentry path offering 360° panoramic views over the Drôme Pre-Alps and Rhône valley.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Stroll through the entirely pedestrian historic center with alleys lined by Provençal-colored houses. Admire the rehabilitated ramparts and private mansions. Discover Diane de Poitiers' House, a beautiful 16th-century Renaissance building. If you're there Saturday morning, the highly reputed weekly market takes over the market square.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Enter the delicious world of Montélimar nougat at artisanal factories perpetuating tradition since the 19th century. The Arnaud Soubeyran Nougat Museum, the oldest factory founded in 1837, traces this confection's history and offers live viewing of artisanal production. Nougat consists of honey, sugar, egg whites, almonds, and sometimes pistachios.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the château through multiple playful visit routes enriched with models, treasure hunts, sound atmospheres, tactile experiences, and augmented reality. This family-friendly discovery imagined by children and Drôme professionals teaches the château's history, lords' lives, and medieval practices.
⏱️ 150 min
→
Visit traditional factories like Le Chaudron d'Or or Arnaud Soubeyran where local expertise unfolds during regular demonstrations. Taste different nougat varieties: white, black, soft, hard, with pistachios, with candied fruits. Learn to distinguish quality genuine Montélimar nougat.
⏱️ 75 min
→
Complete your visit with the Montélimar Museum presenting local history, the European Museum of Fighter Aviation with combat aircraft still in flying condition, or the Miniature Museum displaying incredibly meticulous miniatures.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Stroll down the pedestrian shopping street running from the Protestant temple to the clock tower. Designated a City of Art and Crafts, Dieulefit concentrates workshops, boutiques, museums, and galleries. The village sits beside the Jabron River. The riverside house façades have been repainted in pastel tones creating a colorful, joyful atmosphere.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Discover the 40 pottery and ceramics workshops, heirs to a 2,000-year tradition. Dieulefit's pottery history begins in Gallo-Roman times thanks to clay renowned for its qualities. Today, the Dieulefit region perpetuates this tradition with a revival of contemporary production. Enter galleries to admire original creations bearing the Dieulefit Original label.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Visit this cultural center created in 1995 in Baume Park that presents the region's pottery history. The museum space displays over 200 pieces illustrating local production evolution, particularly the great 19th-century era. Discover pottery's various uses through the ages and manufacturing techniques employed.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Get hands-on during a pottery initiation workshop offered by the House of Ceramics or a local potter. Discover coiling, slab building, modeling, and throwing. You can create your own unique piece guided by passionate artisans.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Tour the workshop circuit open to the public in downtown and surroundings. Engage with artisans in their workspaces, observe shaping techniques, discover kilns and various firing methods. Each artisan has developed their own style and specialty.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Enjoy the orienteering sport space set up by the department to discover the old village in a fun family way. The goal is to find wooden markers shown on the map. Three routes of varying difficulty exist. Cool off along the Jabron's banks, whose source lies in neighboring Comps.
⏱️ 90 min
