Corrèze Road Trip: Villages and Dordogne Valley

Red stone radiates warm beauty, emerald river water flows, waterfalls plunge in rushing torrents. Corrèze is water and heritage.

Discover

1
Notre-Dame Cathedral of Tulle. Built from 1103 on the site of a Merovingian abbey, it blends Romanesque and Gothic styles. Its octagonal spire bell tower rises between 73 and 75 meters depending on sources - it's the tallest in Limousin. Inside, the Great Organ from 1839 is listed as a Historic Monument, and the large blue stained glass window by Jean-Jacques Grüber (1979) tells the story of the Lunade, a procession that ended a plague epidemic in the 14th century.
⏱️ 45 min
2
13th-century Gothic cloister. The only remaining cloister in Bas-Limousin, it adjoined the former Saint-Martin Abbey. The "gallery of angels" features sculpted vaults. The chapter house preserves two 14th-century murals depicting the Last Supper and Christ's Entry into Jerusalem. Listed as a Historic Monument since 1840.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Cité de l'Accordéon and Heritage Center. Opened in April 2024 in the former Bank of France, it presents three exhibition paths: the accordion (Tulle is the French capital of the instrument with the Maugein manufacture since 1919), lace in "poinct de Tulle" and the history of the Arms Manufacture. Interactive museum with audio guide available in Occitan.
⏱️ 90 min
4
Loyac House. Architectural masterpiece from the 16th century located in the Enclos quarter. The facade blends Renaissance and Flamboyant Gothic styles, adorned with pediments sculpted with plants and animals (lion, greyhound, wild boar, mermaid). Two watchtowers frame the columned doorway.
⏱️ 15 min
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll through the Trech quarter and the Enclos. Tulle's medieval heart revolves around the cathedral. Sloping streets, staircases, tall 15th-16th century houses with corbelling and wooden balconies. Don't miss the Coin des Clampes fountain, "the meeting place of the gossipers" in Occitan, immortalized by a 1984 sculpture.
⏱️ 60 min💡 The town extends 4 km along the Corrèze river, nicknamed "La Coureuse" (The Runner)
→
Tulle Market. The banks of the Corrèze come alive on Wednesday and Saturday mornings. Local products, friendly atmosphere along the river.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Saint-Pierre Church and its sculpted tympanum. Romanesque building from the 11th century with a rare detail: the tympanum representing Christ's Ascension is made of white Turenne limestone, which contrasts with the surrounding red sandstone. The octagonal gabled bell tower exceeds 20 meters. The church was divided in two during the Wars of Religion to accommodate both religious services simultaneously.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Penitents' Chapel (Saint-Maximin). 14th-century Gothic chapel, it served as a burial place for noble families including the Maussacs (coat of arms visible on a vault keystone). From the mid-17th to the late 19th century, the Black Penitents brotherhood met here. Saved from ruin in 1927 by the Friends of Collonges, it houses a stained glass window by Francis Chigot (1937) depicting the Resurrection, and two contemporary stained glass windows by Didier Mencoboni and Olivier Juteau (2016).
⏱️ 20 min
3
Vassinhac Castle. Built in 1583, this manor belonged to the lords of Collonges who were also governors of the Viscounty of Turenne. The facade features typical Renaissance mullioned windows, and two round towers frame the structure. Not open for interior visits.
⏱️ 15 min
4
Mermaid House. 16th-century residence that owes its name to the mermaid sculpted to the right of the entrance door. It houses a small museum of folk arts and traditions with a reconstruction of a traditional Corrèze interior.
⏱️ 40 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Walk through the streets with 25 towers. Allow 2 hours for the complete tour. The noble houses with blue schist or slate roofs were built by officers of the Viscounty of Turenne between the 15th and 16th centuries. Vines climb the red facades - the green/red contrast is striking. The red sandstone comes from a geological fault between Brive and Meyssac, rich in iron oxide.
⏱️ 120 min💡 Arrive early in the morning or late afternoon to avoid summer crowds
→
Tasting of Corrèze straw wine. This sweet wine is produced from grapes dried on straw racks. A few local producers offer tastings in the village or around Meyssac.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Meyssac Market. 1.5 km from Collonges, the neighboring village holds a market on Tuesdays and Fridays year-round, and on Sundays in July-August.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Caesar's Tower (13th century). Circular keep 18 meters high. The 63 steps of the spiral staircase lead to the top offering a 360° panorama over the Corrèze causse. Turenne was the seat of a powerful viscounty for 10 centuries, virtually independent from the Kingdom of France: the viscounts minted coins and levied taxes. On May 8, 1738, Charles-Godefroy, the last viscount riddled with gambling debts, sold everything to Louis XV. End of the saying "Happy as a Turenne subject".
⏱️ 45 min
2
Treasure Tower (13th century). Rectangular keep with buttresses measuring 9.50m by 12.50m where the viscounty's archives and treasure were kept. The Guards' Room, 10 meters high under vaulted ceiling, features a beautiful fireplace and a collection of Louis XIII salt chests.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Collegiate Church of Notre-Dame Saint-Pantaleon. 17th-century church built by the viscounts of Turenne. The interior contains a gilded altarpiece and period woodwork.
⏱️ 20 min
🎯

Activities

→
Climb via Rue Droite. The main street climbs from the bottom of the village to the towers. Houses with turrets, watchtowers and Renaissance windows testify to past prosperity. Good shoes recommended, the slope is steep and the cobblestones sometimes slippery.
⏱️ 45 min💡 Parking at the bottom of the village, then walking uphill required

Discover

1
Saint-Pierre Abbey Church and its Romanesque tympanum. Founded in the 9th century, the Benedictine abbey features a tympanum sculpted around 1125 representing the Parousia (Christ's return at the end of times). It's one of the masterpieces of Limousin Romanesque sculpture, comparable to Moissac or Conques. Christ in Majesty is surrounded by apostles, angels and monsters symbolizing evil.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Penitents' Chapel. On the banks of the Dordogne, this 12th-century chapel with its bell gable is reflected in the water. Iconic view of Beaulieu from the river.
⏱️ 20 min
3
Renaissance House. 16th-century turreted residence in the village center, witness to the prosperity of the era.
⏱️ 15 min
🎯

Activities

→
Gabare boat ride on the Dordogne. The gabare "Adèle et Clarisse", built in 2002 using traditional methods, offers 45-minute narrated cruises about the history of the gabariers. These boatmen once transported wood, Auvergne cheeses and coal to Bordeaux.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Tasting of Beaulieu strawberries. The mild climate of the "Limousin Riviera" allows strawberry cultivation. Local producers at Wednesday market.

1
Quai Lestourgie. Row of houses with wooden balconies and slate roofs that testifies to Argentat's gabarier past. It was the main loading port and departure point to Aquitaine - gabares were also called "argentats" because many were built here. Three historic ports line the Dordogne.

Heritage House. Exhibition on the history of the gabariers and life in the Dordogne valley before the construction of the dams.

Theatrical gabare cruise through the Dordogne gorges. Theatrical cruises with actor departing from Grafeuille (10 min drive from Argentat). Duration 1h15, minimum 8 people per departure. A storyteller brings to life the epic of the gabariers in the wild gorges upstream of the town.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Canoe trip down the Dordogne. Several routes possible from Argentat: sporty 8 km route (1h30-2h) with class 2 rapids to Monceaux, or adventure route of 22-25 km (full day) to Beaulieu. Wild landscapes, Corrèze forests, possibility of picnic break on pebble beaches.
⏱️ 180 min
→

Discover

1
Saint-Pierre Abbey Church. Former Benedictine abbey church built from 1030, consecrated in 1095. The gabled bell tower, typically Limousin, rises from a square base to an octagonal plan. The 11th-century crypt, unique in Bas-Limousin, housed the relics of Breton saints Léon and Coronat, transferred here for protection from Vikings. Listed as Historic Monument in 1840.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Bécharie Gate. 14th-century fortified gate, sole remnant of the medieval ramparts that protected the town.
⏱️ 15 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk on the rocky promontory. Uzerche is nicknamed the "Pearl of Limousin". The local saying goes "Who has a house in Uzerche has a castle in Limousin". The village perched on a spur overlooks a meander of the Vézère. The towers and turrets of noble houses testify to the city's past importance. Panoramic view from the Lunade esplanade.

Saint-Pardoux Church. Church from the late 15th century housing an exceptional treasure: the reliquary shrine of Saint Stephen, a masterpiece of 12th-century Limoges enamel. Also several ancient statues and liturgical objects.

Notre-Dame du Rosaire Chapel. White Penitents' chapel located on the village's main street.

Ruins of Roche Haute Castle. Medieval ruins overlooking the village, visible from the main street.

Activities

→
Gimel Waterfalls - Vuillier Park. The Montane river plunges in three spectacular falls totaling 143 meters: the Grand Saut (45 m), the Redole (38 m) and the Queue de Cheval (60 m) which dives into the "Inferno abyss". Painter Gaston Vuillier developed this park between 1893 and 1899 with staircases and observation points. Abel Hugo, Victor's brother, was the first to describe the site's beauty in "Picturesque France" (1883). Listed site since 1912.
⏱️ 75 min💡 Café-terrace with view of the Grand Saut in the park⚠️ Good shoes essential, not accessible for strollers
→
Hike to the 4th waterfall and Pont du Péage. Free circuit of about 1h30 one way to discover an additional waterfall outside the paid park. The "cascadelles" are also accessible via the Short Circuit in the forest.
⏱️ 120 min💡 Map available at Tulle Tourist Office

Discover

1
Labenche Museum. Located in a 16th-century Renaissance mansion listed as Historic Monument, this art and history museum presents 17 rooms. Unique collection in France of 10 tapestries from the English Royal Mortlake Manufactory (17th century). Also features Claude Debussy's piano, prehistoric and Gallo-Roman archaeological collections, and sections on local traditions.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Saint-Martin Collegiate Church. 12th-century Romanesque church in the heart of the old town. The 6th-century baptistery, discovered during excavations, testifies to the site's antiquity.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Saint-Libéral Chapel. 15th-century Gothic building converted into a contemporary art space, programmed by the Labenche Museum.
⏱️ 20 min
🎯

Activities

→
Brive Market. On Saturday morning, it's one of the most renowned in the region. Under the Georges Brassens hall and on the surrounding squares, local producers and market gardeners offer regional specialties. Don't miss the violet mustard of Brive, a specialty made with grape must.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Tasting of Corrèze specialties. The region offers generous gastronomy: farcidures (grated potato dumplings with bacon), mique (poached dough ball served with salted pork), millassou (potato pancake), tourtous (buckwheat crepes), potato pie, flognarde (apple flan).

Day 9

🔍

Discover

1
Pompadour Castle. Fortress rebuilt in the 15th century by the Hélie family. In 1745, Louis XV bought the estate and gave it with the title of Marquise to his favorite Jeanne-Antoinette Poisson - who never visited it, deeming the property too far from the Court! She resold it in 1761. The following year, Louis XV bought it back and transformed it into a Royal Stud Farm. Tour of 11 rooms including the library, with furniture, Aubusson tapestries and Limoges porcelain.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Marquise Stables and Orangery. House the National Stud horses. The Orangery hosts equestrian shows in summer.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Chignac Estate Stud Farm. France's only national stud farm, birthplace of the Anglo-Arab breed created in Pompadour in the 19th century. You can see foals being born and growing (present from March to October). Visit to discover breeding and selection methods.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Horse racing at the racecourse. Classified 1st category for steeplechase and 2nd category A for flat racing, it's one of the most beautiful racecourses in France with its 2000-meter natural sand track facing the castle. 11 race days per year in summer.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Equestrian shows at the castle. In July-August, equestrian cabarets are organized in the evening on the castle terraces or in the Orangery riding hall. The company "Christophe et Max Hasta Luego" notably offers internationally renowned shows.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Horse Festival - August 15th. Major annual event: Saint Hubert mass at the racecourse, show jumping competition, horse races, demonstrations and equestrian shows.
⏱️ 240 min
