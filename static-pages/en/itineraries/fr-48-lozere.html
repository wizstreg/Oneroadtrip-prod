Lozère Circuit: Tarn Gorges and Cévennes

Gorges slicing through granite, deserted causses stretching to the horizon, rivers trapped between towering cliffs. The Cévennes, untamed and pristine.

≈ 9
Days
249
Km
8

📖 5 min read 🔄 Last updated: 2026-02-13
📅 Best period May, June, September, October

Explore Lozère between wild gorges and wide-open spaces. Discover Mende and its central role in the territory. Follow the valley to Florac. Visit Sainte-Énimie, a village perched above the gorges. Cross La Malène and its spectacular landscapes. Here, river and stone shape the scenery.

  • Florac: gateway to the river valley
  • Sainte-Énimie: stone village on the cliff edge
  • La Malène: riverside refuge in the gorges
  • Meyrueis: mountain spring oasis

Discover

1
Mende's Notre-Dame-et-Saint-Privat Cathedral dominates the skyline with its mismatched twin towers. Built in 1368 at the request of Pope Urban V—a native son of Gévaudan—it once housed the Non-Pareille, the largest bell in Christendom when cast in 1516. Only its clapper survives, now displayed at the organ's base.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Step into the Gévaudan Museum, spread across 1,200 m² and three floors. Twelve galleries showcase over 500 artifacts spanning prehistory to the present day, organized around two themes: Nature and Culture. The legend of the Beast of Gévaudan is documented here with scholarly rigor.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The medieval Pont Notre-Dame spans the Lot River upstream from the center. Riverside houses sport distinctive upturned boat-hull roofs, a hallmark of Mende's architectural heritage.
⏱️ 30 min
4
The Tour des Pénitents stands as the last remnant of the medieval ramparts. From its summit, views sweep across the old town's slate-topped roofs and frame both cathedral spires.
⏱️ 30 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Climb Mont Mimat to the summit cross. The orientation table offers a complete panorama: Mende at your feet, the Margeride mountains to the north, the Mende causse plateau to the south. Best at sunset.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Craving thrills? Mende's via ferrata features routes for all levels on cliffs above town. Suspension bridges, ladders, and a zipline punctuate the course.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Florac's château, beside the Vibron stream, houses the headquarters of Cévennes National Park. Its courtyard, shaded by centuries-old plane trees, provides a peaceful setting. The building hosts exhibitions on Cévenol heritage.
⏱️ 45 min
2
In a quiet park, the Pêcher spring gushes from a rocky scree through several outlets. It channels water from the Méjean causse toward the Tarnon. Its name derives from the Occitan "pesquièr" (fish pond), inherited from the old mills that once lined the watercourse.
⏱️ 40 min
3
Cross the stone Pont du Tarn, Florac's architectural emblem. It offers unobstructed views of the river and surrounding cliffs. Downstream, the metal bridge of the old station, built in 1890 following Eiffel's design, recalls when trains opened up the Cévennes.
⏱️ 30 min
4
The Tourism and National Park Information Center occupies the renovated old train station building. An interactive exhibition presents the Park, its ecosystems, and conservation challenges. Nature shop and bookstore on-site.
⏱️ 40 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Paddle down the Tarn by canoe from Florac to Sainte-Énimie. The gorges gradually tighten, cliffs rise higher, pebble beaches invite breaks. Rental companies provide shuttle service back.
⏱️ 180 min💡 Bring sunscreen and water shoes
→
The Rochefort via ferrata offers two intertwining routes along the river, with an optional 50-meter zipline. Swimming breaks possible between passages.
⏱️ 180 min
→
The GR70, Robert Louis Stevenson's trail, passes through Florac. Follow a section to reach Col de Jalcreste or Barre-des-Cévennes, a hilltop village with history-laden facades and panoramic views over the National Park.
⏱️ 150 min

Discover

1
Listed among France's Most Beautiful Villages, Sainte-Énimie clings to the slopes of the Tarn gorges with its limestone houses. Cobbled calades climb between archways, vaulted passages, and stone stairways.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The former Benedictine monastery, founded in the 6th century according to the legend of Saint Enimie, preserves a Romanesque chapter house and a Gothic ribbed-vault refectory. The "Vieux Logis" museum recreates daily life in the old Cévennes.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Burle spring gushes at the village's heart. Legend tells how Enimie, a Merovingian princess afflicted with leprosy, was healed by these miraculous waters. The resurgence drains the Sauveterre causse.
⏱️ 20 min
4
Take the path up to Sainte-Énimie's hermitage. The remains of the cave chapel overlook the gorges. The panorama plunges to the Tarn 500 meters below and opens across the causses.
⏱️ 60 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Les Détroits form the Tarn gorges' narrowest passage. Cliffs squeeze to within a few meters. Best discovered by flat-bottomed boat or canoe when light plunges between the walls.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Drive to Point Sublime, a natural viewpoint on the Sauveterre causse. Your gaze plunges 400 meters into the Tarn canyon. The ochre and gray rock changes hue with the hour. Accessible by road or trail from the Saint-Chély cirque.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Best light in morning or late afternoon
→
Descend by flat-bottomed boat, propelled by a boatman, through the gorges' most confined section. Silence, rock, and water's reflection compose an almost surreal scene.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
La Malène occupies a Tarn meander at the gorge floor. The Montesquieu barons' château, rebuilt in the 16th century, dominates the slate rooftops. Narrow lanes descend to the boatmen's landing.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Drive up to Roc des Hourtous or Roc du Serre. These two viewpoints, accessible by causse road, deliver a 600-meter vertical drop into the gorges. Griffon vultures often soar below.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Aven Armand, on the Méjean causse, shelters a forest of over 400 stalagmites. The tallest reaches 30 meters—a world record. A funicular descent leads to an underground chamber of 60,000 m².
⏱️ 75 min
4
The Vulture Observatory, above the Jonte gorges, is a museum space dedicated to raptors reintroduced since the 1970s. Telescopes and live cameras let you track griffon vultures, monk vultures, and Egyptian vultures on the cliffs.
⏱️ 60 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Embark from La Malène for the flat-bottomed boat descent to the Baumes cirque. Boatmen navigate through Les Détroits, where cliffs plunge vertically into the Tarn.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Hike down the Jonte gorges from the Méjean causse. The trail winds between cliffs where vultures nest. Simply look up to spot raptors with nearly 3-meter wingspans.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Swim in the Tarn at pebble beaches upstream or downstream from La Malène. The turquoise water is brisk but invigorating. Cliffs frame natural spots sheltered from wind.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Meyrueis, at the confluence of the Jonte, Béthuzon, and Brèze, is ringed by mountains. Its narrow streets weave between Renaissance houses and covered passages. The Tour de l'Horloge, a remnant of medieval fortifications, marks the town's heart.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The Château de Roquedols, 2 km from the center, is a restored 16th-century manor house. It hosts the Cévennes National Park information center for the Méjean causse's southern slope.
⏱️ 40 min
3
The Dargilan cave, known as the "pink cave" for its formations' hue, opens onto the Causse Noir. Immense chambers—including the 142-meter-long Grand Hall—display draperies, columns, and stalactites with orange reflections. Discovered in 1880 by a shepherd.
⏱️ 75 min
4
The Mont Aigoual Observatory, at 1,567 meters elevation, is France's last inhabited mountain weather station. On clear days, panoramas extend from the Alps to the Pyrénées and out to the Mediterranean. The exhibition traces meteorology's history.
⏱️ 60 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Hike on the Méjean causse starting from Meyrueis. The trail crosses dry grasslands, skirts lavognes (pastoral ponds), and passes causse farmsteads built of stone. A landscape of magnificent solitude.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Canyoning in the Tapoul or Jonte gorges. Jumps, natural slides, and rappels in crystalline water between limestone walls. Outfitters provide full equipment.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Meyrueis market runs Wednesday and Friday mornings beneath the plane trees. Causse sheep cheeses, chestnut honey, country charcuterie, and seasonal porcini mushrooms.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
Lake Villefort's 127 hectares nestle in the Cévenol mountains, born from a dam built between 1956 and 1964 on the Bayard valley. Its Blue Flag-certified waters are bordered by developed beaches and an 8.5-km loop trail.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The Château de Castanet, a 16th-century historic monument, was preserved during the dam's flooding. Its name comes from the Occitan "castan" (chestnut tree). Open for self-guided visits in summer, with temporary exhibitions in the vaulted rooms.
⏱️ 45 min
3
La Garde-Guérin, listed among France's Most Beautiful Villages, is a medieval castrum perched at 900 meters elevation. Founded in the 12th century by the Bishop of Mende, this fortified settlement protected travelers on the Voie Régordane. Its cobbled streets, lined with mullioned-window houses and slate roofs, remain intact.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Climb the five levels of La Garde-Guérin's keep, a 12th-century watchtower standing 21.5 meters high. From the summit, your gaze plunges into the Chassezac gorges 400 meters below and extends across the Vivarais and Gévaudan uplands.
⏱️ 30 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Swimming and water sports on Lake Villefort: sailing, windsurfing, canoe-kayak, pedal boats. Lifeguarded beach in July-August.
⏱️ 120 min💡 Mountain water—bring a wetsuit early in season
→
Villefort's via ferrata unfolds on cliffs above the lake. Two routes (easy and difficult) link ziplines and suspension bridges, with the lake as backdrop.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Fishing on Lake Villefort: brown trout, rainbow trout, and perch populate its waters. Accessible banks and calm zones make it a prized mountain fishing spot.
⏱️ 120 min💡 Fishing permit required

Discover

1
Saint-Gervais-Saint-Protais Church, founded in 998, preserves a 12th-century Romanesque nave flanked by two side aisles. Fifteenth-century Gothic chapels house a 17th-century Virgin statue. The flamboyant Gothic portal opens to a sober, light-filled interior.
⏱️ 40 min
2
The 18th-century grain hall is one of France's largest preserved market halls. Its Tuscan-order granite columns support an imposing timber frame covered with Tournel slate. The market still meets here every Saturday.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Filature des Calquières, a historic monument, is France's oldest mill still in operation. Its 19th-century machinery, powered by a larch water wheel on the Langouyrou stream, transforms fleece into yarn before your eyes. A living witness to when Lozère had three times more sheep than people.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Langogne is a thousand-year-old town with a circular layout unique in Lozère. Remnants of ramparts and six medieval towers—one housing the town hall—still trace the fortified perimeter. The 15-monument heritage walk comes with a brochure from the Tourist Office.
⏱️ 45 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Lake Naussac, 1,050 hectares at 950 meters elevation, is France's fifth-largest reservoir. Sailing, paddleboarding, kayaking, kitesurfing, and electric boats operate from two water sports centers. Lifeguarded beach in summer.
⏱️ 180 min💡 Multiple hiking and mountain biking trails around the lake
→
Langogne sits at the crossroads of major trails: the GR70 (Stevenson Trail), GR700 (Voie Régordane), GR4, and GR470 (Sources et Gorges de l'Allier). Follow a section toward the Mercoire forest, 11,000 hectares where the Allier and Chassezac rivers rise.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Langogne's market runs Saturday mornings under the grain hall and in adjacent streets. A second market operates Tuesdays in summer. Cheeses, charcuterie, mountain honey, and Margeride lentils.
⏱️ 60 min

🔍

Discover

1
Aumont-Aubrac is a major stop on the GR65, the Via Podiensis pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela, inscribed on UNESCO's World Heritage List. The village sits at the crossroads of the ancient Roman Via Agrippa (Lyon-Toulouse) and the Santiago trail.
⏱️ 30 min
2
Saint-Étienne Church blends Romanesque and Baroque. Its 11th-century semi-circular apse is the oldest section, vaulted with pointed-arch ribs. The 14th-century nave features ribbed vaults resting on corbels carved with human figures and grotesques.
⏱️ 40 min
3
The Maison du Prieuré, a 1684 building with a pointed-arch portal and mullioned windows, houses the Tourist Office. Its vaulted cellar hosts exhibitions and regional product sales.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Javols-Anderitum, 6 km away, was the Gallo-Roman capital of the Gabales, a Gallic people whose territory corresponds to present-day Lozère. The archaeological site covered 40 hectares in the 2nd century. A circuit of 19 information panels and a "Javols 3D" augmented reality app reconstruct the forum, baths, and theater-amphitheater.
⏱️ 60 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Reach the Aubrac plateau via the Route des Lacs from Nasbinals. Vast pastures, stone burons (former shepherds' huts), Aubrac cattle with "mascara eyes." The Déroc waterfall plunges 33 meters through a basalt chaos.
⏱️ 180 min💡 Bring a windbreaker—the plateau is exposed
→
Taste aligot, the Aubrac's emblematic dish: mashed potatoes worked at length with fresh Laguiole AOP tome cheese and garlic. Village restaurants prepare it by hand, in the dining room, in front of guests.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Hike the GR65 between Aumont-Aubrac and Nasbinals (27 km). The trail crosses vast high-altitude pastures, passes the Domerie d'Aubrac—a hospice founded in 1120 for pilgrims—and its "lost bell," which guided travelers stranded in fog.
⏱️ 360 min
