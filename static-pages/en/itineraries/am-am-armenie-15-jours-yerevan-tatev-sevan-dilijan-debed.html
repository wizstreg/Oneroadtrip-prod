15 Days in Armenia: Grand Tour from Yerevan to Gyumri

The world's first Christian nation, Armenia is a palimpsest of stones and symbols. Every monastery, every khachkar, every limestone cliff tells a story of resistance and faith.

≈ 15
Days

📅 Best period May, June, September, October

From the millennial vineyards of Areni to the rock-cut monasteries of Geghard, from the fortress in the clouds at Amberd to the Byzantine frescoes of Akhtala, Armenia reveals in fifteen days a 1,700-year-old Christian civilization carved in stone.

  • Etchmiadzin: world's oldest cathedral still in operation
  • Tatev: record-breaking cable car + medieval monastery
  • Sevan: alpine lake at 1,900m, endemic trout and khachkars
  • Debed: UNESCO twin Haghpat-Sanahin + Akhtala frescoes

Yerevan

4 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Republic Square is Yerevan's neoclassical heart. Five buildings in pink and yellow tuff frame the plaza, including the Government House with its 4-meter clock dating from 1941.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The Cascade Complex is a monumental staircase of 572 steps designed in 1971, transformed into a contemporary art museum thanks to a $35 million investment by patron Gerard Cafesjian.
⏱️ 90 min
3
The Cascade gardens showcase monumental sculptures by Botero, Plensa, and Flanagan. The view from the top encompasses Yerevan and Mount Ararat.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Vernissage is Armenia's largest craft market, open on weekends near Republic Square. Carpets, stone khachkars, silver jewelry, and vintage Soviet memorabilia.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Stay for the evening musical fountains at Republic Square: the sound and light show begins at 9 PM from April to November.
⏱️ 60 min💡 April to November only
→
Take the free indoor escalators of the Cascade to the top for a nighttime view of the illuminated city.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Free entry
→
Dine in the Kentron district - the terraces along Abovyan Street and Northern Avenue buzz until late evening.
⏱️ 60 min

Yerevan

4 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
The Matenadaran preserves over 23,000 medieval manuscripts - one of the world's most important collections. Inscribed in UNESCO's Memory of the World Register, it displays illuminated gospels from the 5th century and treatises on medicine, mathematics, and astronomy.
⏱️ 90 min
2
On Tsitsernakaberd Hill, the Armenian Genocide Memorial is Armenia's most solemn site. Twelve inclined slabs surround an eternal flame in memory of the 1.5 million victims of 1915.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Genocide Museum, underground and silent, traces through testimonies, photographs, and documents the story of the 20th century's first genocide.
⏱️ 75 min
4
The Tsitsernakaberd stele rises 44 meters and splits in two, symbolizing the Armenian nation divided between those who remained on their land and those of the global diaspora.
⏱️ 20 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk along the memorial's avenue of plane trees, planted by visiting heads of state - each tree bears an identification plaque.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Take the metro from Republic Square to Yeritasardakan station to reach the Matenadaran in minutes.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Metro very inexpensive (~100 drams)
→
In the evening, explore the contemporary art galleries on Mashtots Street, concentrated within a 500-meter radius.
⏱️ 60 min

Yerevan

4 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Etchmiadzin Cathedral is the world's oldest cathedral still in operation. Built between 301 and 303 AD by Saint Gregory the Illuminator after Armenia's conversion to Christianity - the first nation in the world to adopt it as the state religion.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The Etchmiadzin Treasury Museum preserves unique relics: the Holy Lance that pierced Christ's side, Saint Gregory's right hand, and a presumed fragment of Noah's Ark.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The circular ruins of Zvartnots, 3 km from Etchmiadzin, are what remains of a three-level cathedral built in the 7th century and destroyed by an earthquake in the 10th. This rotunda's unique architecture inspired builders far beyond Armenia.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Saint Hripsime Church (618 AD) and Saint Gayane Church (630 AD) are both UNESCO-listed. These two edifices are among the best-preserved Christian structures in the world.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Leave early in the morning to attend the morning ceremony at Etchmiadzin - the black-robed monks and Armenian liturgical chanting create an unforgettable atmosphere.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Modest dress required
→
Take minibus #23 from Yerevan's Kilikia station - frequent departures, 30-40 minute journey for less than 300 drams.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Alternative: taxi ~15 minutes
→
Have lunch in Etchmiadzin before visiting Zvartnots - roadside restaurants serve fresh lavash, khorovats, and Armenian salads.
⏱️ 60 min
🚗

Yerevan

4 photos
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
Khor Virap means 'deep dungeon': King Tiridates III imprisoned Saint Gregory here for 13 years. Released, Gregory healed the king of his madness and converted him - making Armenia the world's first Christian nation in 301 AD.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The Temple of Garni, built in 77 AD by King Tiridates I, is the only preserved Hellenistic temple in the entire post-Soviet space. Its Ionic columns and Roman baths with Greek mosaics testify to a deeply rooted Greco-Roman culture.
⏱️ 75 min
3
One kilometer from the temple, the Symphony of Stones reveals natural basalt columns shaped like a giant organ along the Azat River - a spectacular geological formation.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Geghard - 'the Lance' - is a 13th-century monastery partially carved into the cliff in the Azat Gorge. UNESCO-listed in 2000, it housed the Holy Lance for centuries, now kept at Etchmiadzin.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Descend into Saint Gregory's underground cell at Khor Virap via the metal ladder - 6 meters underground, 4.5 meters in diameter, 13 years of imprisonment.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Narrow space, not for the claustrophobic
→
Listen to Armenian liturgical chants in Geghard's cave-churches: the acoustics carved into the rock amplify voices supernaturally.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Monks chant some mornings
→
Photograph Mount Ararat from the platform behind Khor Virap - one of the Caucasus's most iconic panoramas, with the Turkish border visible 100 meters away.
⏱️ 60 min

Yeghegnadzor

4 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Areni-1 Cave, discovered in 2007, is the world's oldest known winemaking facility: 6,100 years old. Excavations have yielded a press, 65-liter fermentation vats, and storage jars still impregnated with malvidin, the pigment of red wine.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Noravank is nestled in the Amaghu Gorge between blood-red limestone cliffs. Founded in 1105 and developed in the 13th-14th centuries, its masterpiece is Surb Astvatsatsin Church (1339): a two-story edifice whose exterior staircase is so narrow you climb it sideways.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The reliefs sculpted by Momik on Noravank's facade - Christ in mandorla, angels, vegetal interlacing - are among the most refined examples of medieval Armenian art.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Taste wines from the Areni Noir grape at one of the village wineries - this indigenous variety has been cultivated here for at least 6,000 years without interruption.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Hin Areni Winery offers a 6-wine tasting
→
Photograph Noravank at sunset: the crimson cliffs encircling the monastery ignite in spectacular light.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit the Areni archaeological cave with a local guide - explanations about 6,100-year-old winemaking techniques give an unprecedented dimension to Armenian culture.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Entry ~1,000 drams

Jermuk

4 photos
Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
Jermuk's Gallery of Waters features five stone public fountains, each at a higher temperature than the previous: from 30°C to 57°C. Rich in magnesium, calcium, and bromine, these mineral waters have been considered curative for generations.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Jermuk Waterfall plunges 70 meters into the Arpa Gorge - Armenia's second highest. Local legend calls it 'Mermaid's Hair': a forsaken young woman threw herself from the clifftop, her hair transforming into an eternal cascade.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Jermuk sits at 2,000 meters altitude in the Vayots Dzor mountains. A spa town since Soviet times, its 40 natural springs now supply hotels, sanatoriums, and bottled water sold throughout Armenia.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Bring a cup and taste each of the five springs at the Gallery of Waters - locals have been doing their morning cure here for decades.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Free access, cup essential
→
Take the cable car up to 2,480 meters for panoramic views of the town, Arpa Gorge, and surrounding peaks.
⏱️ 60 min💡 ~1,500 drams round trip
→
Book a thermal bath at one of the hotel establishments - hydrotherapy, mineral baths at 37-42°C, and massages with local essential oils.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Grand Resort or Olympia Hotel: ~5,000-8,000 drams
🚗

Goris

3 photos
Day 8
🔍

Discover

1
Goris stands radically apart from other Armenian cities: its houses are built of gray volcanic stone rather than pink tuff, with arched windows and carved wooden balconies. The surrounding rock formations shelter the 'Old Goris' caves, inhabited until the 18th century.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Khndzoresk is an entire village carved into the cliffs of a volcanic canyon, inhabited until the 1950s-1970s by about 15,000 people. Four churches, three schools, and hundreds of dwellings are carved into the rock on multiple levels.
⏱️ 120 min
3
In the 18th century, Khndzoresk was a stronghold of Armenian resistance in Syunik. The tomb of hero Mkhitar Sparapet, his wife Goar, and their son Aaron still stands here.
⏱️ 20 min
🎯

Activities

→
Cross the 160-meter suspension bridge over Khndzoresk Canyon - the vertiginous view of the caves and volcanic formations is one of Armenia's most striking.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Free access. Avoid in bad weather.
→
Descend into the canyon from the new village to explore the inhabited caves from inside - some interior spaces are remarkably well preserved.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Good shoes essential
→
Have lunch in Goris before departing for Khndzoresk - the town is the last supply point before Syunik's more isolated sites.
⏱️ 60 min

Goris

3 photos
Day 9
🔍

Discover

1
The Wings of Tatev cable car holds the Guinness record for the world's longest reversible cable car at 5.75 kilometers. In 12 minutes, the cabins traverse 320 meters above the Vorotan Gorge, revealing the monastery on its basalt plateau.
⏱️ 30 min
2
Founded in the 9th century, Tatev was one of medieval Armenia's great intellectual centers. Its University, active in the 14th-15th centuries, trained generations of philosophers, theologians, and musicians who profoundly shaped Armenian culture.
⏱️ 90 min
3
The Gavazan of Tatev is an 8-meter stone column mounted on a pivot: this medieval seismograph tilted to signal the approach of an earthquake or enemy army. No other such device is known from the medieval world.
⏱️ 20 min
4
The Devil's Bridge (Satani Kamurj) is a natural travertine arch formed over centuries by thermal mineral springs. Its ochre and white hues in the gorge setting create a surreal mineral landscape.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Take the cable car up from Halidzor and walk down via the road - a 45-minute walk through the forest with different views of the canyon.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Cable car ~9,000 drams round trip. Closed Mondays.
→
Bathe in the natural hot springs near the Devil's Bridge - the mineral waters at comfortable temperature are perfect after a day of walking.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Swimsuit recommended
→
Explore Tatev's fortified ramparts to understand its defensive dimension - the plateau's isolation was also strategic protection against invasions.
⏱️ 60 min

Sevan

6 photos
Day 10
🔍

Discover

1
Sevanavank ('black monastery') was founded in 874 by Princess Mariam Bagratouni on an island in Lake Sevan. Soviet drainage in the 1930s-1960s lowered the lake by 20 meters, transforming the island into a peninsula connected to shore.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The Noratus cemetery, 12 km from the lake, is the world's largest collection of khachkars. Several hundred carved stone crosses - some dating from the 9th century - spread across the plain, each unique in its patterns of interlacing, angels, and scenes of daily life.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Lake Sevan is the Caucasus's largest lake: 1,360 km² at 1,900 meters altitude between the Armenian mountain ranges. Formed 25,000 years ago, it shelters the Ishkhan - Sevan's endemic trout, renowned throughout Armenia.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Lunch at one of the lakeside restaurants with fresh Ishkhan trout - this endemic fish is Sevan's unmissable gastronomic highlight.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Numerous restaurants on the north shore
→
Climb the steps of the Sevanavank peninsula for the panoramic view of the lake's blue immensity and encircling mountains - ideally at sunrise or sunset.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Photograph the Noratus khachkars in raking light - the medieval sculptures gain remarkable depth when backlit.
⏱️ 60 min

Dilijan

6 photos
Day 11
🔍

Discover

1
Sharambeyan Street, restored to its 19th-century appearance, is the heart of Dilijan's old town - called 'the Armenian Switzerland.' Its dark tuff houses with carved wooden balconies shelter artisan workshops and galleries.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Haghartsin ('eagle's flight') is a 10th-13th century monastery nestled in a forested bowl within Dilijan National Park. Its 13th-century refectory is the largest monastic hall preserved in Armenia.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Goshavank was founded in 1188 by fabulist Mkhitar Gosh to replace Getikvank monastery, destroyed by earthquake. The khachkar by master Poghos (1291), erected facing the basilica, is considered among Armenia's most finely sculpted.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Don't leave Haghartsin without tasting the hot gata filled with apricots and thyme from the bakery at the monastery entrance - an irreplaceable regional specialty.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Bakery open during the day
→
Pick up a trail map at the national park information center in Dilijan's municipal park before visiting the monasteries.
⏱️ 60 min💡 HikeArmenia app essential for hiking
→
Optional: from Goshavank, the 1.5-hour trail climbs to Gosh Lich mountain lake - ideal in late afternoon if time permits.
⏱️ 60 min

1
Parz Lake ('crystal') is the natural jewel of Dilijan National Park. Surrounded by pine and beech forests, its limpid waters reflect the wooded peaks with remarkable clarity.

2
The monasteries of Matosavank and Jukhtakvank, accessible on foot from Dilijan center, are little-known medieval retreats buried in the woods. Their bareness and centuries-old moss make them moving in their authenticity.

Alaverdi

3 photos
Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Akhtala is Armenia's most surprising monastery: while Armenian churches are generally austere, its walls explode with color. Byzantine frescoes painted between 1205 and 1216 cover the entire interior in deep blues and golds - artworks among the finest preserved outside the Byzantine Empire's borders.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Sanahin - 'this one is older than that one' - is a 10th-century monastic complex UNESCO-listed. Its scriptorium from 1063 still preserves the niches where illuminated manuscripts were stored. The collection of khachkars scattered across the site is among Armenia's richest.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Haghpat, founded in 976 by Queen Khosrovanush, is the other UNESCO jewel of the canyon. Its austerity is deliberate - the monks believed any decoration distracted from prayer. Beauty resides in the perfection of proportions and harmony with the Debed landscape.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Spot the fresco of bearded Persians in Akhtala - according to legend, it was painted to dissuade enemy armies from attacking the church by giving them the impression of being watched.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit the Mikoyan Brothers Museum next to Sanahin: a MiG-21 is displayed outside in tribute to Artyom Mikoyan, co-designer of the famous fighter jets.
⏱️ 60 min💡 200 m from Sanahin monastery
→
Hike the World Heritage Trail between Sanahin and Haghpat (HikeArmenia app) - 3-4 hours walking through forests and villages of the Debed Canyon.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Last marshrutka return from Haghpat around 5 PM

Gyumri

4 photos
Day 14
🔍

Discover

1
Gyumri's Kumayri historic quarter is one of the Caucasus's most remarkable architectural heritages. Hundreds of 19th-century houses in black volcanic tuff, with arched windows and wrought-iron balconies, compose a unique coherent ensemble in Armenia.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Amenaprkich Church (1858-1872) is Gyumri's architectural symbol. Its neo-Gothic facade in dark tuff, pointed arches, and slender silhouette make it Armenia's most singular religious edifice - severely damaged by the 1988 earthquake, restored in the 2000s.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Dzitoghtsyan Museum, housed in a beautiful 19th-century mansion, presents the daily life, costumes, and crafts of Gyumri's bourgeoisie, plus a section documenting the 1988 earthquake that killed 25,000 people.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Push open the doors to Kumayri's flowered inner courtyards - residents will often welcome you with hospitality to share an Armenian coffee.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Observe the contrast between the meticulously restored black tuff houses and Soviet concrete buildings - Gyumri's architectural history in a single glance.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Dine at one of the lively downtown restaurants - Gyumri is known for its cultural life and arts scene more vibrant than its streets might suggest.
⏱️ 60 min

Yerevan

4 photos
Day 15
🔍

Discover

1
Amberd fortress ('fortress in the clouds') perches at 2,300 meters altitude on the slopes of Mount Aragats, Armenia's highest peak. Built in the 7th-11th centuries by the Pahlavid princes, this nearly impregnable citadel protected trade routes crossing the massif.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The church adjoining the fortress, from the 11th century, is one of the best preserved from this period in Armenia. In clear weather, you can see both Mount Ararat to the south and Aragats summit (4,090 m) above.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Explore Amberd's towers and ramparts - the site's defensive logic becomes evident from the battlements: two deep gorges protect three sides of the fortress.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Free access. Car essential.
→
Photograph the alpine landscape around Amberd depending on season - wildflowers in July, snow possible from October.
⏱️ 60 min
→
On the return journey to Yerevan (70 km, 1.5 hours), stop at Byurakan village to visit the Soviet Astronomical Observatory open for tours.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Guided tours available by reservation

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Peak Season (May-September): Perfect weather for hiking and sightseeing, warm temperatures, minimal rainfall, best for mountain activities and festivals.

Spring (April-May): Mild temperatures, wildflowers blooming, excellent for cultural sites, fewer crowds than summer months.

Fall (September-October): Pleasant temperatures, harvest season, beautiful autumn colors, wine harvest celebrations, ideal hiking weather.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, snow in mountains, skiing season, fewer tourists, cozy indoor cultural experiences.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 12 and 18 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Armenia?

Armenia boasts the world's oldest Christian sites, stunning mountain monasteries, and exceptional wine culture. Mount Ararat views and ancient khachkars create spiritual experiences while Yerevan offers vibrant nightlife. The country combines deep history with warm hospitality and incredible landscapes. Perfect for cultural enthusiasts and adventure seekers.
