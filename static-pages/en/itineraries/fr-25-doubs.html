Doubs Circuit: Watchmaking and Swiss Border

Discover

1
The Citadel of Besançon spans 11 hectares atop Mount Saint-Étienne, 130 meters above the Doubs. A masterpiece by Vauban whose first stone was laid on September 29, 1668, it is one of 12 fortifications inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage list since 2008. Its ramparts, 600 meters long and 15 to 20 meters high, house three museums: the Musée Comtois on Franche-Comté traditions, the Museum of Resistance and Deportation, and a Museum with zoological garden, aquarium, insectarium, and noctarium. It is the second most visited paid monument in Bourgogne-Franche-Comté with 200,000 to 300,000 visitors per year.
⏱️ 180 min
2
The Musée du Temps occupies the Palais Granvelle, a Renaissance building in the heart of Besançon, the capital of French watchmaking. The collections trace the history of timekeeping from the 16th century to the present day: watches, clocks, and scientific instruments. The centerpiece is the Leroy 01, a 1904 watch with 975 parts and 24 complications, considered the most complicated in the world until 1989. A Foucault pendulum swings beneath the magnificent oak framework with views over the city.
⏱️ 90 min
3
The astronomical clock of Saint-Jean Cathedral has been classified as a Historic Monument since 1991. Installed in a lower room of the bell tower, it was designed by clockmaker Auguste-Lucien Vérité between 1858 and 1860 at the request of Cardinal Matthieu. It contains more than 30,000 mechanical parts, 11 movements, and 57 dials providing 122 different indications: calendars, planetary movements, eclipses, tide times in different ports. The clock is currently stopped awaiting restoration.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Victor Hugo's birthplace is located at 140 Grande Rue, where the writer was born on February 26, 1802. The contemporary scenography pays tribute to the committed man he was and presents his fights for social justice, the abolition of the death penalty, and human rights. The visit reveals the family and historical context of his birth in Besançon.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk the loop of the Doubs on foot from the city center. This natural meander that encircles Besançon offers views of the quays lined with old houses, the Porte Noire (Roman triumphal arch), and the Citadel perched on its hill. The towpath runs along the river for several kilometers in a green setting.
⏱️ 90 min
→
The Museum of Fine Arts and Archaeology houses the oldest public collection in France, initiated in 1694. Renovated in 2018 after 4 years of work, it presents paintings, graphic arts, and sculptures from all periods. The archaeological collections bear witness to the rich past of Vesontio, ancient Besançon.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
The Gustave Courbet Museum occupies the painter's birthplace, inaugurated in 1971 and renovated between 2008 and 2011. Its 2,000 m² spread across 21 rooms present 60 works by the artist: paintings, sculptures, and drawings. Notable works include The Oak of Flagey, The Bridge of Nahin (1837), and The Mirror of Ornans (1872). Glass slabs and skylights offer views of the Loue that Courbet painted 14 times. The museum is labeled Maison des Illustres.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Ornans is nicknamed the 'Little Comtoise Venice' for its houses suspended above the Loue or built on stilts. The village has preserved its old buildings and their reflections in the water that inspired Courbet throughout his career. The studio he occupied from 1860 to 1873 features a ceiling entirely painted by the artist.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The source of the Loue springs from a 104-meter cliff in the shape of a hemicycle, in a porch 60 meters high and 32 meters wide. This karst resurgence is one of the most powerful in France with a flow varying from 1 to 100 m³/s. In 1901, a fire in a distillery in Pontarlier revealed that the Loue was fed by losses from the Doubs: the spilled absinthe reappeared at the source a few days later, confirmed by fluorescein tracing in 1910. Courbet painted this cave in 1864. Allow 5-10 minutes walk from the parking lot.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Follow the path along the Loue from Ornans toward the Grand Saut de la Loue (12 m waterfall), located 900 m downstream from the source. The river with crystal-clear waters flows through wooded gorges dotted with small waterfalls. The landscape changes with the seasons and constantly recalls Courbet's paintings.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Pontarlier was the world capital of absinthe in the 19th century. In 1805, the first distillery opened its doors. In 1900, 25 distilleries employed 3,000 people (38% of the population) and produced 66,000 liters per day. Banned on March 17, 1915, absinthe was rehabilitated in 2001 and has enjoyed PGI status since 2010. The municipal museum recounts this history with a collection of objects, posters, and period documents.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The last historic distillery in Pontarlier, founded in 1890, perpetuates the tradition at 49 rue des Lavaux. Its century-old copper stills and vats produce absinthe using ancestral methods. Labeled Living Heritage Company, it offers free tours with tasting of its products: absinthe, Pontarlier-Anis, and fir liqueur.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Château de Joux overlooks the Pontarlier cluse since the 11th century, controlling the passage to Switzerland. Its 5 enclosures, 3 moats, and 3 drawbridges extend over 2 hectares with 250 rooms. Classified as a Historic Monument, it was fortified by Vauban at the end of the 17th century then served as a state prison. Toussaint Louverture, hero of Haitian independence, died there on April 7, 1803, after seven months of imprisonment in a freezing cell. The site is inscribed on the Route of the Abolitions of Slavery.
⏱️ 120 min
🎯

Activities

→
Participate in a guided tour of the Château de Joux to discover the evolution of fortification from the Middle Ages to Vauban. Guides also recount the history of famous prisoners: Mirabeau, Toussaint Louverture, and German writer Heinrich von Kleist. Toussaint Louverture's cell is a memorial site for the abolition of slavery with commemorations every April 7 and May 10.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Taste Comtoise specialties in Pontarlier restaurants: Morteau sausage, Comté fondue, mushroom croute, warm Mont d'Or in winter. The city maintains a gastronomic tradition linked to its past as a commercial crossroads between France and Switzerland.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Lake Saint-Point is the 3rd largest natural lake in France at 7.2 km long, 950 m wide, and 40 m deep at an altitude of 849 m. Crossed by the Doubs, it is separated from Lake Remoray by a protected wetland. It frequently freezes in winter and becomes a natural ice rink. Five villages border its shores: Saint-Point-Lac, Les Grangettes, Labergement-Sainte-Marie, Montperreux, and Malbuisson.
⏱️ 120 min
2
Malbuisson has been a Station Verte since 2009, recognized for its hotel and gastronomic tradition. The village overlooks the lake and serves as the starting point for the lake loop trail (23 km, pedestrians only). The Aqua2lacs water complex offers pools overlooking with panoramic lake views, jacuzzi, and relaxation area.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Mont d'Or rises to 1,463 meters, the highest point in the Doubs department. From its summit, accessible by the Morond chairlift or on foot via the GR5, you discover an exceptional panorama of more than 300 Alpine peaks including Mont Blanc, as well as the Haut-Doubs, Haut-Jura, and neighboring Switzerland. The site is classified as a Sensitive Natural Area for its remarkable flora including some protected species.
⏱️ 150 min
🎯

Activities

→
Four water sports centers offer sailing, catamaran, stand-up paddle, canoe-kayak, and pedal boats on Lake Saint-Point. The beaches at Les Grangettes and Oye-et-Pallet are supervised in July-August. The water remains cool even in summer (18-22°C in August).
⏱️ 120 min
→
The Mont d'Or ridge hike (approximately 2h round trip, 6 km) connects the Morond summit to the Mont d'Or summit via a panoramic trail. A museum space in the snow factory presents the history and biodiversity of the massif. Free trail guides are available at the station ticket offices.
⏱️ 150 min

Discover

1
The Saut du Doubs is a 27-meter waterfall on the Franco-Swiss border, classified as a Grand National Site. Created 12,000 to 14,000 years ago by a landslide, it marks the end of Lake Brenets (or Lake Chaillexon). The Basins of the Doubs that precede it form a 3.5 km long canyon, 100-200 m wide and 30-40 m deep, bordered by high limestone cliffs with sometimes strange shapes. The level varies by nearly 20 meters between floods and low waters. The site attracts 200,000 visitors per year.
⏱️ 150 min
2
Morteau is the birthplace of the eponymous sausage, protected by PGI since 2010 and Label Rouge since 2006. Its cylindrical shape with wooden peg is recognizable among all. Smoking with coniferous wood (fir, spruce, juniper) in traditional tuyés is inscribed on UNESCO's intangible heritage. Production reaches approximately 5,000 tons per year. The town, labeled Cité de Caractère, preserves beautiful monuments including the Town Hall (1590) and the Renaissance-facade Château Pertusier.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Pays Horloger saw the birth of the French watchmaking industry in the mid-18th century. This know-how is inscribed on UNESCO's intangible cultural heritage. The watchmaking museum, installed in Château Pertusier, is currently closed for renovation and will reopen in 2026 under the name Cité des Horlogers with new exhibition spaces.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Two boat companies offer commented one-hour cruises to the Saut du Doubs from Villers-le-Lac. You can also access it on foot (2.5 km from Vions parking), by carriage from La Courpée, or by car to the viewpoint. The sporty hike to the Châtelot dam offers superb views of the gorges.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Visit a tuyé farm to discover traditional Comtoise architecture and the meat smoking process. These buildings with large roofs house a central room with a chimney that can reach 15 meters where coniferous smoke slowly perfumes sausages, bresi, and hams for several weeks. The Ferme-musée du Beugnon in Grand'Combe-Châteleu presents this heritage.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Goumois is a Franco-Swiss village split in two by the Treaty of Vienna in 1815, with the Doubs serving as the border. Nestled in the valley hollow, it is surrounded by the Corniche road, the Rocher du Singe, and the ruins of the medieval castle of Franquemont. Jean-Jacques Rousseau came to botanize here as early as 1748 with botanist Gagnebin. The bridge over the Doubs connects the two namesake communes.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The Corniche de Goumois viewpoint offers an exceptional panorama over the Doubs gorges and the Swiss Jura. The very picturesque road that leads there dominates the gorges and offers a series of varied viewpoints. It is the meeting place for lovers of wild and authentic nature.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Maîche plateau extends between 800 and 1,000 meters altitude, bounded by the deep valleys of the Doubs and Dessoubre. This characteristic area of the Haut-Doubs offers vast expanses dotted with old Comtoise farms. Maîche, the canton's chief town near the Swiss border, is integrated into the Doubs Horloger regional natural park.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Goumois is an internationally renowned fishing spot for trout and grayling. The Franco-Swiss Doubs offers 150 km of marked hiking trails. The most daring will take the Échelles de la Mort, installed in a wild and spectacular site near Charquemont.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Le Russey, at nearly 1,000 meters altitude on the Maîche plateau, offers panoramas over the surrounding plateaus. The 19th-century town hall is listed as a Historic Monument. Saint-Nicolas church and Saint-Roch chapel testify to the local religious heritage. It is the birthplace of the Almanach Vermot, founded by Joseph Féréol Vermot born here in 1828.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
The Cirque de Consolation is a wild 350-meter-deep reculée with magnificent limestone cliffs, in the heart of the Doubs Horloger regional natural park. This is where the Dessoubre is born, fed by the springs of Lançot, Tabourot, and Source Noire. The Minimes monastery, built in 1670 with stones from the castle of Châtelneuf-en-Vennes destroyed during the Ten Years' War, forms a 40-meter square. The 1682 chapel preserves art objects and the mausoleum of Ferdinand-François de Rye.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The Lançot waterfall plunges 47 meters into the Cirque de Consolation. It is fed during heavy rains or snowmelt. The path leading there passes through a magical atmosphere of streams, tufa formations, and small caves. The lush vegetation creates an atmosphere conducive to contemplation.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Saint-Hippolyte, a character town of Bourgogne-Franche-Comté, nestles at the confluence of the Doubs and Dessoubre (the 'Revirot'). Freed by Jean II de la Roche in 1298, it preserves its 14th-century collegiate church of Notre-Dame where the Shroud of Turin was kept for 35 years (1418-1452), the 18th-century Ursuline convent, and 16th and 17th-century mullioned houses. It is the historic capital of the Franche-Montagne.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Roche du Prêtre viewpoint offers a vertiginous panorama over the Cirque de Consolation and the Dessoubre valley, 350 meters below. Accessible by car or on foot via a sporty hike. According to legend, a priest was thrown from the top of the cliff by the friends of a giant he had locked in a cave. Picnic tables provided.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Descend the Dessoubre valley to Saint-Hippolyte. This picturesque 33 km valley, where many water mills once sang, offers exceptional environmental richness. It is paradise for fishermen (trout, grayling) and walkers who appreciate the ambient calm. In summer, the cool water invites you to dip your feet.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
The Château de Montbéliard has dominated the city since the 10th century. Its two hectares include 250 rooms divided between the Henriette and Frédéric towers. The museum presents 15 renovated rooms (875 m²) with history collections (furniture, silverware, weapons from the 15th to 18th centuries), archaeology, natural sciences linked to naturalist Georges Cuvier native to the city, and contemporary art.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Saint-Martin temple, built between 1601 and 1607 by architect Heinrich Schickhardt, is the oldest French building assigned to Reformed worship. Montbéliard was the only French-speaking Lutheran territory, attached to the Duchy of Württemberg for four centuries. The temple is currently closed for restoration.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Musée de l'Aventure Peugeot traces the history of the Lion brand from its 19th-century origins. The collections present not only iconic automobiles but also the coffee, pepper, and electric mills that made the company's reputation before the automobile. An immersion into the region's industrial history.
⏱️ 120 min
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll through the historic center of Montbéliard, a City of Art and History. Les Halles (former governmental center of the Principality), the Hôtel du Lion Rouge, the Hôtel Forstner, and the colorful facades testify to the Germanic influence inherited from four centuries of attachment to Württemberg. Saint-Maimboeuf church (1850) completes the architectural panorama.
⏱️ 60 min
→
The Lumières de Noël in Montbéliard is one of the most beautiful Christmas markets in Europe. For four weeks at year-end, 160 artisans set up around the Saint-Martin temple. Shows, entertainment, ice rink, and children's village animate the city. The castle lights up and the streets are adorned with magical decorations.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
The Abbey of Baume-les-Dames was founded around the 5th century by Saint Germain, Bishop of Besançon. It was around it that the city developed. In the 7th century, Saint Odile, blind daughter of the Duke of Alsace fleeing death, was baptized there and regained her sight. The current abbey church, rebuilt in the 18th century by architect Nicolas Nicole, features a profusion of pink and white marble, black Arguel stone, and gilding. To enter this monastery, one had to prove 16 quarters of nobility.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Baume-les-Dames, a character town labeled Station Verte, owes its name to the caves ('baume' in Celtic) and the canoness ladies of the abbey. The historic center preserves the 17th-century Saint-Martin church, the Hôtel des Sires de Neufchâtel (Renaissance) with its reconstructions of medieval life, and mullioned houses. The Affiche Moilkan typography workshop perpetuates the art of traditional printing.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Cusancin valley and the Château de Belvoir deserve a detour from Baume-les-Dames. The castle, guardian of the Sancey valley with its perched medieval silhouette, offers a tour of its rooms and a remarkable viewpoint. The 14th-century market halls host night markets in summer.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
The EuroVelo 6, the famous river cycle route connecting the Atlantic to the Black Sea, runs along the Doubs at Baume-les-Dames. The flat route on the old towpaths allows you to discover the valley in complete tranquility. The town is also a river stopover for boaters.
⏱️ 120 min
→
The Baume cliffs offer 460 climbing routes from levels 4 to 9, a renowned climbing ground in the region. For hikers, the viewpoint of the Virgin of Cholera offers the prettiest view of the historic heart of Baume-les-Dames. The Sous-Buen caves, accessible on foot or by bike, invite exploration.
⏱️ 90 min
