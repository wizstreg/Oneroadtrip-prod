Var Road Trip: French Riviera & Green Provence

Where golden Mediterranean light sears the landscape and palm trees sway above turquoise coves. Mimosa petals drift on hillside breezes. The French Riviera enchants eternally—entirely customizable to your dreams.

Discover Toulon and its harbor, Hyères and the Golden Islands, Saint-Tropez, Porquerolles island, and Bormes-les-Mimosas. The Var—where Provence embraces the Mediterranean.

  • Toulon: Historic naval harbor and coastal gateway
  • Hyères: Palm-fringed portal to island paradise
  • Saint-Tropez: Bohemian soul of the Riviera
  • Porquerolles: Protected island sanctuary and national park

Toulon

Day 1

Discover

1
Behind the monumental 18th-century gateway, the National Maritime Museum showcases ship models, galleys, and paintings tracing four centuries of naval tradition. The museum reopened with immersive scenography, including a 3D reconstruction of the Arroyo shipwreck that brings maritime archaeology to life.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Board the cable car for a six-minute ride and 378-meter ascent to the summit of Mont Faron. At the top, the Memorial Museum of the Provence Landing recounts the August 1944 operation through period artifacts and multimedia exhibits that honor the liberation forces.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Notre-Dame de la Seds Cathedral, founded in the 5th century, blends Romanesque and Gothic elements with a unified Gothic nave, a bell tower crowned by a campanile, and an 18th-century organ. The side chapels house the Chapel of the Virgin and the Corpus Domini chapel.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Built in 1524 on Louis XII's orders and classified as a historic monument since 1947, the Royal Tower rises with seven-meter-thick walls and moats—a rare example of Italian 'torrione' fortification. It hosts cultural events in summer against a backdrop of Mediterranean vistas.
⏱️ 60 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Wander through the stalls of Cours Lafayette market, open Tuesday through Sunday. Herbs de Provence, olives, soaps, and fresh produce line the stands in a quintessential Provençal market atmosphere buzzing with local color.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Embark on a narrated one-hour boat tour of Toulon's harbor. You'll discover military vessels at anchor, fortifications guarding the bay, mussel farms in Lazaret Bay, and the haunting naval cemetery—a floating journey through maritime history.
⏱️ 75 min💡 Regular departures from Quai Cronstadt
→
Head to the Mourillon beaches, bordered by landscaped gardens, lawns, and playgrounds. Beyond, the coastal path leads to the wild coves of Méjean and Magaud—secluded inlets at the foot of pine-studded cliffs where the Mediterranean feels gloriously untamed.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Built in the 1920s by architect Robert Mallet-Stevens, Villa Noailles is a classified modernist masterpiece. Gabriel Guévrékian's cubist garden offers panoramic views of the Golden Islands. The villa hosts year-round exhibitions and festivals celebrating fashion, design, and avant-garde art.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Below the villa, Parc Saint-Bernard unfolds its narrow terraces linked by stone staircases. The botanical garden gathers Mediterranean and exotic species: rosemary, wisteria, and rare plants that thrive in this microclimate.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Jardins Olbius-Riquier spreads across seven hectares of greenery. An exotic greenhouse shelters palms, bamboo, banana trees, and cacti. The pond and its cascade offer refreshing coolness in summer. Free admission year-round.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Climb up to the Collégiale Saint-Paul, adorned with ex-votos, then wind back down through the steep alleys of the old town. The Templars' Tower, a medieval vestige of the order, can be visited with a treasure hunt for children.
⏱️ 60 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
On Saturday mornings, the Îles d'Or market takes over the old town. Seasonal fruits, tapenade, soaps, and local crafts fill the stalls under the plane trees in a scene of timeless Provençal charm.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Arrive early to enjoy the atmosphere
→
Follow the coastal trail around the Giens peninsula. Between salt marshes, pine groves, and coves, the route traces the salt road and its marshes where flamingos and grey herons pause—a walker's paradise of biodiversity.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Craving the beach? L'Almanarre beach, a long ribbon of sand between sea and salt pans, is renowned for windsurfing and kitesurfing thanks to steady winds that make it a mecca for wave riders.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Fort Sainte-Agathe, strengthened in 1531 under François I, has dominated the harbor since Antiquity. Managed by Port-Cros National Park, it hosts exhibitions on the island's marine and terrestrial biodiversity. Views from the ramparts sweep over the village and Courtade beach.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Notre-Dame beach, voted Europe's most beautiful beach, stretches in a bay bordered by eucalyptus. Its turquoise, shallow waters make it ideal for swimming. Count on 25 minutes on foot or 10 minutes by bike from the village.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Near the fort, the Moulin du Bonheur—a Provençal windmill restored by the National Park—offers a glimpse of island agricultural life. Free guided tours for visitors under 18, from May to September.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Porquerolles village nestles behind the harbor, around the shaded Place d'Armes. Sainte-Anne church and houses with colorful shutters compose a postcard scene. In the evening, when the ferries have departed, tranquility reigns supreme.
⏱️ 45 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Bike rental shops line the harbor. Four trails marked by the National Park allow you to traverse the island north to south between vineyards, pine forests, and fragrant maquis—cycling heaven on car-free paths.
⏱️ 120 min💡 Rent upon ferry arrival to avoid sold-out stock
→
Follow the forts circuit: 9.5 km of trails linking Forts Lequin, Alycastre, Repentance, and Sainte-Agathe. The route reconstructs the island's military life through four centuries of fortifications—history on foot.
⏱️ 150 min
→
The island's wine estates produce 70% rosé AOC Côtes de Provence. Tastings are by reservation in a unique island setting, between vines and pine groves—sipping pink wine where salt air meets the terroir.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
A 12th-century medieval village classified among France's Most Beautiful Villages, Bormes unfolds its flowered alleys, 'cuberts' (vaulted passages), and ochre-tinted facades. Over 700 plant species color the village—bougainvillea in summer, mimosa cascades in winter.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Climb up to the 13th–14th-century castle, a historic monument since 1931. From the village's highest point, the panorama embraces Lavandou Bay, the islands of Levant and Port-Cros, Favière harbor, and Cap Bénat—a view that captures the entire coast.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Saint-Trophyme church, built in the 18th century in Romanesque style, replaced the old castle church that had become too small. Its Chapel of Saint Francis honors the saint who reportedly delivered the town from plague in 1483.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Parc Gonzalez, classified as a Remarkable Garden, brings together 3,000 m² of plants from Australia and around the world, with 250 species arranged by landscape designer Gilles Augias. The terraces offer views over the Mediterranean.
⏱️ 45 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Fort de Brégançon, a state residence overlooking the sea, can be glimpsed from the coast. Head to Cabasson beach below: white sand, crystalline waters, and a direct view of the fort—presidential luxury from a beach towel.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Nine wine estates and one cooperative cellar offer tastings of AOC Côtes de Provence. The 'Vignobles & Découvertes' label guarantees quality hospitality in the cellars, where the rosé flows freely.
⏱️ 75 min💡 Ask for the local rosés
→
From Favière harbor, boat excursions head to Port-Cros, Porquerolles, or offshore to observe dolphins. Departures run from April to October, weather permitting.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
The Musée de l'Annonciade, housed in a 16th-century chapel, holds a world-class collection: Pointillist, Nabis, and Fauvist paintings. Signac, Matisse, Derain, and Marquet are all represented—a reminder that Saint-Tropez was a major center of avant-garde painting.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The Citadel, erected in 1602 under Henri IV, has dominated the harbor for four centuries. The hexagonal keep, a historic monument since 1921, houses the Museum of Maritime History. Models, artifacts, and immersive scenography recount the Tropezian sailors—from whaling to trade with the Ottoman Empire.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Place des Lices, shaded by century-old plane trees, has been the village's beating heart since the 19th century. On Tuesdays and Saturdays, the Provençal market takes over the square. The rest of the time, pétanque games animate the café terraces.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Notre-Dame de l'Assomption church, with its characteristic ochre and pink bell tower, has watched over the village since the 16th century. Inside, the bust of Saint Torpès recalls the founding legend of the town.
⏱️ 30 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Follow the coastal path from Graniers beach. This seaside trail hugs the coves and headlands, offering panoramas between pine groves and turquoise waters. Swimming is possible in the wild coves—nature's own infinity pools.
⏱️ 120 min
→
The old harbor invites aimless wandering: fishing boats mingle with yachts, and colorful facades reflect in the water. Head to Quai Jean-Jaurès for the permanent spectacle of maritime life.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Push on to Ramatuelle, a medieval village perched on Paillas hill overlooking Pampelonne Bay. Its spiral lanes and ramparts offer a tranquil counterpoint to Tropezian bustle—old Provence above the glitz.
⏱️ 90 min

Grimaud

4 photos
Day 6
Top hotels
9.3 Hotel Le Verger Maelvi
9.1 Mobil Homes Vacances

Discover

1
Grimaud Castle, built in the 11th century, preserves its three enclosures, two massive round towers, and a medieval keep. Pillaged during the Revolution, its ruins still offer a panorama over the Gulf of Saint-Tropez and the Maures massif. In summer, the ramparts host the Grimaldines festival.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Saint-Michel church, a 12th-century Romanesque structure, is a classified historic monument. Its typically Provençal architecture contrasts with the Penitents' chapels and Saint-Roch, whose restored mural paintings illuminate medieval devotion.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Get lost in the village's cobbled lanes: Rue des Arcades houses the Templars' house, a fine 16th-century Renaissance building adorned with three Gothic arches. Mullioned windows and doorways framed with serpentinite punctuate the stroll.
⏱️ 60 min
4
The Saint-Roch mill, a 17th-century windmill restored in 1990 by the Companions of the Tour de France, crowns the village on its hill. On the ground, a large slab was used for threshing wheat; beside it, an old press produced olive oil.
⏱️ 30 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Port-Grimaud, a lakeside town created in the 1960s by architect François Spoerry, can be explored by boat on its canals. Each house has direct water access and a mooring ring. The 'Provençal Venice' can also be discovered on foot from the village.
⏱️ 90 min
→
The Provençal markets of Grimaud and Port-Grimaud alternate throughout the week. Local producers and artisans offer fruits, cheeses, olive oil, and art objects in a lively atmosphere.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Several wine estates around the village offer tastings of AOC Côtes de Provence wines. The Grimaud wine cooperative allows you to sample local vintages straight from the source.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Check with the tourist office for schedules

Discover

1
The Roman amphitheater, built in the 1st century, could hold 10,000 spectators who came to watch gladiatorial combat. Its tiers, corridors, and vomitoria can be visited freely. The venue still hosts concerts and shows—ancient stones echoing with modern music.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Saint-Léonce Cathedral forms, along with the 5th-century Paleo-Christian baptistery, one of France's oldest episcopal complexes. The octagonal baptistery, supported by recycled ancient columns, preserves its original baptismal font—a tangible link to early Christianity.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The 13th-century Romanesque cloister is a medieval jewel. Its 14th-century carved larch wood ceiling presents 300 painted panels of fantastic creatures, saints, and daily life scenes. A bestiary unique in France—look up and lose yourself.
⏱️ 75 min
4
The Roman aqueduct stretched 42 kilometers with a 481-meter drop and 36 aqueduct bridges. At La Roche Taillée, a 50-meter canal was carved directly into the rock. Classified as a historic monument since 1886, its vestiges can be visited on foot from downtown.
⏱️ 45 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll through the pedestrian lanes of the historic center, between Place Formigé with its terraces, Rue Sieyès with its good addresses. The Provençal market animates the squares every morning with local flavor.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Villa Aurélienne and its 24-hectare park offer a green break in town. This Palladian-style residence, surrounded by umbrella pines, hosts temporary exhibitions in a setting of aristocratic elegance.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit the Roman theater, more modest than the amphitheater but fascinating. Its restored tiers allow you to imagine the performances of comedies and poetry from Antiquity—sit where Romans once sat.
⏱️ 30 min

Discover

1
San Rafeu church, a 12th-century Romanesque structure, is a classified historic monument. Its foundations rest on a site occupied since Roman times. The adjoining presbytery houses the municipal archaeological museum, with collections ranging from prehistory to underwater archaeology.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Climb the 129 steps of the San Rafeu Tower, a 13th-century fortified tower standing 25 meters high. From the summit, the 360° panorama embraces the basilica's domes, the red rocks of the Estérel, the Mediterranean, and Saint-Tropez Bay—a view worth every step.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Basilica of Notre-Dame de la Victoire, with its Byzantine domes, has dominated the seafront since the late 19th century. Its neo-Romanesque architecture blends golden stone and pink sandstone from the Estérel.
⏱️ 30 min
4
The archaeological museum displays amphorae, anchors, and objects recovered from wrecks off the coast. One section is devoted to the Paleolithic with vestiges discovered in the region—the deep past made tangible.
⏱️ 60 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Take the Corniche d'Or, a 40 km road between Saint-Raphaël and Cannes. Under umbrella pines, the red porphyry rocks of the Estérel plunge into the turquoise Mediterranean. Stops possible at the coves of Agay, Dramont, and Anthéor—one of France's most spectacular coastal drives.
⏱️ 120 min
→
The Agay roadstead, sheltered between Cap Dramont and Pointe de la Baumette, offers calm waters ideal for swimming, kayaking, and paddleboarding. The sandy beach is bordered by pines and red rocks—postcard Riviera.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Six dive clubs offer outings around the Golden Islands, Cap Dramont, and offshore wrecks. The seabed of the Cap Roux reserve shelters groupers, brown meagres, and gorgonians in protected waters.
⏱️ 120 min

Top hotels

Discover

1
The Clock Tower, rebuilt in 1662 and classified as a historic monument, rises on the town's central rock. Its 79 steps lead to a campanile offering a panoramic view over the Dracénie landscape. The monument stands 24 meters tall.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The Dolmen de la Pierre de la Fée, dating from 2,500 BCE, is one of Provence's most imposing: 60 tons, three slabs over 2 meters supporting a 6-meter-long table. Classified as a historic monument since 1889, it leans against a centuries-old oak—prehistoric monumentality.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Museum of Popular Arts and Traditions of Middle Provence, labeled Musée de France, holds one of the country's finest ethnographic collections. Exhibitions are built in partnership with the world's greatest museums—local heritage meets international scholarship.
⏱️ 75 min
4
The Museum of Fine Arts, located in the heart of the historic center, displays a collection of artworks and historical artifacts, including the ceremonial armor of François de Montmorency, restored by the Research Center of the Museums of France.
⏱️ 60 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
In Trans-en-Provence, 12 km north, cross the 70-meter footbridge spanning the Nartuby gorges. In summer, swimming is possible in the natural pools—a local secret of cool waters and canyon beauty.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Park in the village and walk down
→
Climb to the Malmont orientation table for a 360° panorama over the region. The trail starts from downtown and winds between garrigue and pine forest—a walk that rewards with sweeping vistas.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Wander the medieval lanes of the old town, between the ancient houses of the Maison de la Reine Jeanne and Saint-Hermentaire chapel. Parc Chabran, with its century-old trees, invites relaxation in the shade.

Discover

1
Cotignac's tuff cliff rises 80 meters high and 400 meters wide, sculpted by the ancient Cassole waterfall. Troglodyte dwellings carved into the rock served as refuge in the Middle Ages. Climb the 90 steps to discover the old caves and a panoramic view over the village.
⏱️ 75 min
2
At the cliff's summit, two watchtowers known as 'Saracen' once surveyed the horizon against invasions. An orientation table allows you to embrace the rooftops of Cotignac, the terraced hills planted with olive trees and cypress—a view of timeless Provence.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Cours Gambetta, the main artery shaded by century-old plane trees, is the hub of social life. The Fountain of the Four Seasons marks its center. Café terraces come alive especially on market days—the pulse of village life.
⏱️ 45 min
4
The sanctuary of Notre-Dame de Grâces, built in 1519 following apparitions of the Virgin Mary, nestles in the hills 1 km south of the village. King Louis XIV and his mother Anne of Austria made a pilgrimage here in 1660 to give thanks for the king's birth.
⏱️ 60 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Wine estates around Cotignac produce AOC Côtes de Provence wines. The cooperative cellar in the village center and several estates offer tastings and visits to the cellars—the terroir in a glass.
⏱️ 75 min💡 Ask for the rosé and local olive oil
→
On Tuesday mornings, the Provençal market takes over the Cours. In summer, a farmers' market gathers local producers: wine, oil, honey, truffles, and artisan crafts—the bounty of the land on display.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Six kilometers north, the Sillans-la-Cascade waterfall plunges 42 meters into a verdant setting. The access trail crosses an oak forest. Swimming is forbidden at the waterfall's base but possible downstream—nature's refreshment.
⏱️ 90 min

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather, blooming countryside, ideal for all regions, moderate crowds, excellent value, romantic atmosphere.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather, peak tourist season, highest prices, crowded attractions, perfect for Mediterranean coast, festival season.

Fall (September-October): Harvest season, comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, wine regions spectacular, excellent photography, good value.

Winter (November-March): Christmas markets, ski season Alps, fewer crowds, lowest prices, cozy indoor attractions, cultural experiences.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 9 and 13 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.
