Lot Itinerary: Cahors, Rocamadour & the Lot Valley

Villages gripping cliff faces, alleyways threading beneath vaulted passageways, limestone gorges carved by ancient waters—Quercy, suspended in time.

📅 Best period May, June, September, October

Discover the Lot between medieval citadels and limestone gorges. Cross the Pont Valentré in Cahors. Wander through Saint-Cirq-Lapopie, clinging to its cliff. Ascend to Rocamadour, a sacred pilgrimage site. Explore Figeac and its historic center. Visit Martel and its ramparts. Stone shapes every stage of this journey.

  • Cahors: medieval city of the fortified bridge
  • Saint-Cirq: crown jewel perched on limestone cliffs
  • Rocamadour: sacred sanctuary rising from stone
  • Figeac: beauty woven through medieval streets
  • Martel: medieval ramparts crowned with seven towers

Cahors

Discover

1
The Pont Valentré has spanned the Lot River since 1308. Three defensive towers rise 40 meters above the water, linked by eight stone arches stretching 172 meters. Look for the carved devil perched atop the central tower—a reminder of the legend about the architect's pact with Satan. The bridge is UNESCO-listed as part of the Way of Saint James pilgrimage routes.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Step inside the Cathedral of Saint-Étienne, consecrated in 1119. Two massive domes on pendentives cover the nave—the largest medieval domes in southwest France, soaring over 30 meters high. The western dome preserves 14th-century murals depicting the stoning of Saint Stephen and eight prophets. The northern portal, carved between 1140 and 1160, illustrates Christ's Ascension in intricate detail.
⏱️ 50 min
3
Behind the cathedral, the Flamboyant Gothic cloister was built between 1493 and 1553. Its galleries feature delicately carved arches sheltering sculptures and coats of arms of successive bishops. The Chapel of Saint-Gausbert, in the former chapter house, contains the Holy Coif—a relic believed to have wrapped Christ's head.
⏱️ 40 min
4
Wander the narrow streets of old Cahors: half-timbered medieval houses, Renaissance twin windows, and soleilhos—those distinctive open-air attics typical of Quercy. Rue Nationale, Place Saint-Urcisse, and the Badernes quarter showcase the finest facades. Beneath the underground parking, you can glimpse the remains of a 1st-century Gallo-Roman amphitheater.
⏱️ 60 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Follow the towpath along the Lot River at the foot of Pont Valentré. The trail passes the Chartreux Fountain—a natural spring—and the Maison de l'Eau, a converted 19th-century pumping station. Stunning views of the bridge's arches from the riverbank.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit Cahors market on Wednesday and Saturday mornings. Stalls fill the area around the cathedral and market hall—ranked among France's exceptional markets. Find Rocamadour cheese, Périgord walnuts, Quercy lamb, melons, saffron, local pastries, and Cahors wines.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Arrive early for the best produce
→
The Cahors AOC vineyards spread across the hillsides surrounding the town. Malbec, the region's signature grape, produces deep, full-bodied wines nicknamed "black wine." Several estates offer tastings by appointment.
⏱️ 75 min💡 Reservations recommended at estates

Discover

1
Saint-Cirq-Lapopie clings to a limestone cliff 100 meters above the Lot River. France's Favorite Village, it boasts thirteen listed historical monuments and fewer than 200 residents. Houses from the 12th to 15th centuries, with brown-tile roofs, cascade toward the river below.
⏱️ 30 min
2
Climb to the Rocher de Lapopie, the village's highest point. Ruins of a 10th–12th-century fortress crown the summit. The 360° panorama embraces the Lot Valley, limestone cliffs, and the village rooftops spread beneath your feet.
⏱️ 40 min
3
The 12th-century fortified church dominates the village from its rocky promontory. Its bell tower doubled as a defensive structure during troubled medieval times. Inside, simple furnishings and understated decor invite quiet contemplation.
⏱️ 25 min
4
Descend Rue de la Couronnerie, the main medieval thoroughfare lined with artisan workshops and stone-arched houses. Place du Sombral, the village heart, offers a shady rest stop. At the far end, the Porte de la Pélissaria opens onto the path leading down to the Lot.
⏱️ 40 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Walk the towpath between Bouziès and Saint-Cirq-Lapopie, carved into the cliff face 80 meters above the Lot between 1843 and 1847 for horses pulling barges. Along the way, a 30-meter bas-relief sculpted into the rock depicts the valley's fauna, flora, and fossils. The route passes through the manual lock at Ganil.
⏱️ 150 min
→
Board a traditional flat-bottomed gabare from Bouziès. These historic boats navigate the Lot, passing through locks with commentary on river navigation history and views of the cliffs and perched village.
⏱️ 80 min
→
Rent a canoe, kayak, or stand-up paddleboard at the river's edge below the village. Outfitters offer routes of varying lengths, with shuttle service or loop options.

Discover

1
The Champollion Museum—Writings of the World occupies the birthplace of the man who deciphered hieroglyphics. Its contemporary facade is inlaid with a thousand writing symbols in copper and glass. Inside, 5,300 years of writing history unfolds: Sumerian cuneiform, Egyptian hieroglyphics, Chinese ideograms, Mayan glyphs. The oldest piece, a Sumerian pictographic tablet, dates back to 3200 BCE.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Just beside the museum, Place des Écritures features a giant reproduction of the Rosetta Stone cast in black granite by American artist Joseph Kosuth in 1991. In the adjacent courtyard, the French translation is engraved on a glass plate suspended above a map of the Nile Delta.
⏱️ 20 min
3
On Place Champollion, the Maison du Griffon dates from the mid-12th century—the town's oldest dwelling. All around, half-timbered medieval houses and typical Quercy soleilhos compose an intact historic setting. The 14th-century former hospice completes the ensemble.
⏱️ 30 min
4
The Hôtel du Viguier du Roy, a 14th-century palace, served as the royal representative's residence. Its interior courtyard, cloister, formal French gardens, and royal tower are open to visitors.
⏱️ 30 min
+2 Places visited

⏱️ 90 min💡 Booklet available at the tourist office

Figeac's market fills Place Carnot every Saturday morning beneath the 19th-century metal hall. Find cabécous, Rocamadour cheeses, walnuts, Quercy saffron, and fouace—a local orange-blossom brioche.

Discover

1
Rocamadour unfolds on three levels clinging to a cliff face: the commercial village below, the sanctuaries midway, the castle at the summit. France's second most-visited religious site draws over one million visitors annually. The Grand Staircase of 216 steps, which pilgrims climb on their knees, connects the main street to the chapels above.
⏱️ 30 min
2
The sanctuary comprises seven chapels gripping the rock wall. The Chapel of Notre-Dame houses the Black Virgin, a 12th-century walnut statue darkened by time and candle smoke. Above the entrance, the miraculous 8th-century bell reportedly rang to announce sailors' rescues at sea. Ex-voto model ships hang from the vault.
⏱️ 50 min
3
The Romanesque-Gothic Basilica of Saint-Sauveur preserves the relics of Saint Amadour in its crypt. Legend says his body was found intact beneath the threshold of the Virgin's chapel. In the rock beside Notre-Dame Chapel, a sword embedded in the cliff is said to be Durandal, the legendary weapon of Roland.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Take the Way of the Cross from the sanctuaries: fourteen stations climb through the trees to the castle ramparts. From the crenellations, your view plunges into the Alzou canyon and sweeps across the entire citadel below.
⏱️ 45 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
At the Rocher des Aigles, over 400 birds representing 60 species live on the cliffs facing Rocamadour. Free-flight demonstrations launch eagles, vultures, condors, and falcons above the canyon, followed by macaws and cockatoos.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Multiple performances daily April–September
→
Walk the cliff-top promenade along the rock face. Several viewpoints offer successive perspectives of the citadel, valley, and plateaus. The most famous, at L'Hospitalet, frames the postcard view of Rocamadour.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Themed guided tours explore the citadel from different angles: general discovery, in pilgrims' footsteps, or tales of miracles. Town of Art and History guides lead these excursions.

Discover

1
Martel, the "Town of Seven Towers," is listed among France's Most Beautiful Villages. A merchant city founded in the 11th–12th centuries by the viscounts of Turenne, it has preserved its intact medieval layout encircled by 12th–14th-century ramparts.
⏱️ 20 min
2
Place des Consuls forms Martel's beating heart. An 18th-century market hall shelters stalls beneath stone pillars. All around, half-timbered houses and arcaded dwellings compose a preserved setting.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Palais de la Raymondie, built between 1280 and 1330, raises its square tower above the square. The former seigneurial residence houses the Uxellodunum Museum—Gallo-Roman collections and Quercy history. Designated a Museum of France.
⏱️ 45 min
4
The fortified Church of Saint-Maur impresses with its 40-meter bell tower-keep built for defense. A carved 12th-century tympanum adorns the portal. Inside, entirely painted decoration, surprisingly colorful, contrasts with the facade's austerity.
⏱️ 30 min

The Truffadou tourist train follows the former Bordeaux-Aurillac line carved into the cliff 80 meters above the Dordogne. The 13-kilometer route between Martel and Saint-Denis offers plunging views of the valley from open wagons.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Browse Martel's market on Wednesday and Saturday mornings beneath the 18th-century hall. Seasonal truffles, walnuts, Quercy terroir products, and local specialties.
⏱️ 45 min

Visit a traditional walnut oil mill near Martel. Périgord walnuts, with AOC designation, are pressed using artisanal methods. Tastings and on-site sales.

Discover

1
The Abbey Church of Sainte-Marie, a masterpiece of Romanesque art, crowns the town with its three domes on pendentives. Founded in the 10th century by Benedictines following the Abbey of Saint-Géraud d'Aurillac, the current church was constructed between 1145 and 1150.
⏱️ 30 min
2
The Romanesque portal, relocated inside the abbey church for protection from the elements, represents a treasure of medieval sculpture. The tympanum recounts the legend of Theophilus; the trumeau interweaves Isaac's sacrifice and original sin. The pillar of the prophet Isaiah, all movement and flowing drapery, is a unique piece of Romanesque art.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Inside, a painting attributed to Chasseriau, "Christ in the Garden of Olives" (1844), and a monumental Stoltz organ (1855), listed as a Historic Monument, complete the visit. The acoustics beneath the domes naturally amplify sound.
⏱️ 20 min
4
Explore Souillac's historic center, crossed by seven viaducts spanning streams and ravines. Ancient alleyways lead to the 19th-century market hall, still active on market days, and to the Dordogne riverbanks where the old river port once stood.
⏱️ 40 min
🎯

⏱️ 60 min

⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
The Church of Saint-Pierre sits atop Gourdon's hilltop. Its southern Gothic facade, flanked by two defensive towers, conceals a richly decorated interior: an 18th–19th-century tribune organ, altarpiece, and bas-reliefs. The 14th-century rose window on the main portal and the large apse window play with light throughout the day.
⏱️ 40 min
2
Reach the château esplanade at the hilltop's highest point, 286 meters elevation. From the site of the former castle and its ramparts, panoramic views extend across Gourdon's rooftops, the Bouriane countryside, and the distant plateaus.
⏱️ 25 min
3
The Maison du Sénéchal, built in the 13th century, was the residence of the royal official responsible for justice and local administration. Now converted into a Center for Architecture and Heritage Interpretation, it presents the history of the citadel and Bouriane region.
⏱️ 35 min
4
The Gardens of the Hilltop nestle in Gourdon's medieval heart. The Zig-Zag Gardens recreate the medieval atmosphere; the Sénéchal Gardens shelter a performance courtyard adorned with a topiary butterfly; the Fairy Tale Garden blends wicker structures, wooden characters, and ornamental grasses. All maintained by passionate volunteers.
⏱️ 30 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Original sensory tours invite you to discover Gourdon blindfolded, guided by touch, hearing, and smell. An unconventional approach to heritage that engages all the senses.
⏱️ 75 min

Walk the medieval lanes of the historic center: golden stone houses, pointed arches, covered passages. The marked pedestrian circuit leads from one building to the next in a village founded in the 11th century.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Gourdon's market gathers local producers and artisans. Black truffles in winter, porcini mushrooms in autumn, goat cheeses, walnuts, and honey year-round.

Discover

1
Puy-l'Évêque rises on a rocky spur 30 meters above the Lot River. Golden-stone medieval dwellings tier in concentric arcs from the seigneurial tower at the summit down to the old port below. From the bridge across the Lot, the overall view of the terraced citadel and its reflection in the water forms the village's iconic image.
⏱️ 30 min
2
The 13th-century episcopal tower rises 23 meters—the last vestige of the bishops' palace of Cahors, who gave the town its name in 1228. Its four levels, connected by a staircase within the wall thickness, served as a watchtower. The top floor preserves a fireplace and latrines carved into the stone.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Church of Saint-Sauveur, a Historic Monument from the 14th–15th centuries, occupies an unusual position: outside the castrum, on the heights. Its Gothic nave with ribbed vaults and its bell tower—a former watchtower—dominate the valley. A remarkable feature: the church possesses the Lot department's only historic carillon, composed of 15 bells, the oldest dating from 1632.
⏱️ 35 min
4
Descend through the hanging gardens, created in 2016 on the site of ruined medieval houses. This terraced pedestrian pathway connects the upper town to the river, punctuated by rest stops with open views across the Lot Valley and the citadel rooftops.
⏱️ 30 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Board an electric gabare at the foot of the village. This replica of 19th-century boats glides silently on the Lot. The guide recounts river navigation history while vineyards, cliffs, and mills slip past.
⏱️ 75 min
→
Puy-l'Évêque is the capital of the Cahors wine region. Malbec, planted here since Roman times, produces deep-colored, structured AOC wines. Several estates open their cellars to visitors for tastings led by the winemaker.
⏱️ 75 min💡 Contact estates in advance
→
The Lot Valley cycle route winds between vineyards and river. From Puy-l'Évêque, ride toward Albas and its steep lanes, or toward Luzech and its peninsula, for panoramas of the Lot's meanders.
⏱️ 120 min
