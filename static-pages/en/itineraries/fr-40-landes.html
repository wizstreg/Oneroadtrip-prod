Landes Road Trip: Atlantic Coast, Pine Forests & Gascon Heritage

Endless beaches bordered by dunes, pine forests as far as the eye can see, turquoise lakes, thermal heritage and preserved villages: the Landes reveal their multiple faces.

Discover the Landes between maritime pine forests and wild Atlantic coast. From Mont-de-Marsan and its sculpture heritage to Dax and its millennial thermal waters, from Hossegor surf capital to the authentic port of Capbreton, from Biscarrosse's inland lakes to the Marquèze ecomuseum, this circuit offers a concentrate of the Landes lifestyle.

  • Dax: Thermal bathing since Roman times and Art Deco heritage
  • Hossegor: European surf capital with legendary waves
  • Capbreton: The Landes' only port facing the underwater canyon
  • Biscarrosse: Seaplane epic and crystal-clear lakes
  • Marquèze Ecomuseum: Time travel by historic train

Discover

1
Musée Despiau-Wlérick: France's largest collection of 20th-century figurative sculpture housed in the 13th-century Donjon Lacataye medieval tower. The 2,400 works honor local sculptors Charles Despiau and Robert Wlérick. Sculpture gardens offer an outdoor trail that extends throughout the city via the Museum in the Street initiative, a pioneering project launched in 1988.
⏱️ 120 min
2
Plumaçon Arena: Built in 1889 and expanded in 1933, classified as 20th-century heritage. These arenas come alive with tradition during the famous Madeleine Feria every July. The course landaise (a regional form of bullfighting) has been passionately practiced here for over a century.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Historic center: Stroll along the Midou and Douze rivers that converge at the city's heart. The shaded quays and bridges offer beautiful perspectives on local architecture. The Parc Jean-Rameau stretches peacefully along the water's edge.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Museum in the Street trail: Discover monumental sculptures scattered throughout the city. This free outdoor urban art trail showcases major works in public spaces. Allow 1h30 for the complete walking circuit.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Saturday morning food market: Local producers offer foie gras, Tursan wines, and Landes asparagus. The convivial atmosphere and Gascon specialties make this market unmissable. Vendors set up from 8am around the central halls.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Fêtes de la Madeleine in July: 5 days of Landes tradition with course landaise in the arenas, bandas in the streets, and concerts. Over 500,000 festayres gather annually for this major southwestern feria. Accommodation must be booked several months in advance.
⏱️ 240 min

Discover

1
Fontaine Chaude: Gushing at 64°C since Roman times, this spring feeds the thermal establishments of Dax, France's premier spa town and one of Europe's most important. The neoclassical building erected between 1814 and 1818 houses the Nèhe spring. The péloïde, mud derived from Adour River silt, has been used for rheumatological treatments for 2,000 years.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Archaeological crypt: Remains of a 1st-century basilica bearing witness to ancient Aquae Tarbellicae (waters of the Tarbelli). The mosaics and Roman foundations reveal the importance of this ancient thermal city. Guided tours organized by the Borda Museum on Tuesday, Thursday and Saturday at 3pm from February to December.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Notre-Dame Cathedral: Built between 1662 and 1894 in an imposing neoclassical style. The Apostles Portal, a 13th-century Gothic remnant, contrasts with the overall architecture. The Gallo-Roman ramparts are still visible nearby, recalling the ancient city's fortifications.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Art Deco heritage: The Atrium and Hotel Le Splendid testify to the golden age of the 1930s. These buildings designed by Parisian architects Granet and Expert illustrate the splendor of grand spa resorts. The Parc du Sarrat, a remarkable 3.5-hectare garden, shelters over 1,000 trees and 130 species.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Heritage circuit: 2-hour urban route between Fontaine Chaude, Gallo-Roman ramparts and cathedral. The tourist office offers themed guided tours (Art Deco, thermal heritage, arenas). Dax has been labeled a City of Art and History since 2019.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Dax markets: The renovated halls (2021) offer local products Saturday and Sunday mornings. Fattened duck, Landes asparagus, Tursan wines and Bayonne ham. The friendly atmosphere and local producers make these markets essential stops.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Banks of the Adour: Walk along the river at Parc Théodore-Denis. The landscaped banks offer a peaceful setting for walking or running. Fishing possible with permit. Sunsets over the water create a special atmosphere.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
La Gravière and La Nord surf spots: Hossegor's legendary waves are influenced by the Gouf de Capbreton, an underwater canyon extending to nearly 4,000m depth offshore. The canyon begins at shallow depth near the coast and deepens progressively. These beach breaks, among the most powerful on the planet, attract the world's best surfers during World Surf League competitions each autumn.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Basque-Landes villas: Explore the seafront and its interwar architecture designed by renowned architects (Godbarge, Lagrange, Pomade, Prunetti). The Sporting Casino, a classified historic monument, dominates the beach. These elegant residences with characteristic half-timbering and steep roofs embody the Basque-Landes style of the 1920s-1930s.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Lake Hossegor marine lagoon: This reshaped natural lagoon offers 6km of promenade lined with traditional oyster cabins and elegant villas. The lake's calm waters contrast with the power of the nearby ocean. The complete loop on foot or by bike showcases this unique landscape between fresh and salt water.
⏱️ 90 min
4
Place des Landais: Hossegor's beating heart with its cafes, restaurants and designer boutiques. The laid-back atmosphere mixes surfers, families and vacationers. The mural of La Vague celebrates the town's surf identity. Evening markets in summer with local products and crafts.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Surf lessons: Schools offer beginner courses and advanced coaching on spots suited to each level. South Beach suits beginners with more accessible waves. Equipment provided, maximum groups of 8. Booking advised in high season.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Lake loop by bike: 6km of flat bike path around the marine lake. The route offers beautiful views of oyster cabins and allows for tasting stops. Bike rental downtown and near the lake. Allow 45min to 1h depending on stops.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Oyster tasting on the lake: Oyster cabins offer tastings facing the water. Marennes-Oléron oysters accompanied by local white wine. Authentic atmosphere lakeside, feet in the sand. Open year-round, arrive before 12:30pm in season.
⏱️ 60 min

Capbreton

Discover

1
Estacade: 189m wooden pier built under Napoleon III and completed in 1860, rebuilt and modified several times throughout its history. Built with 600 pine trees, it marks the port entrance and faces the Gouf de Capbreton. The lighthouse added in 1950 has a range of 14 miles. The walk on the planks offers a striking view of the ocean and the underwater canyon.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Port of Capbreton: The Landes department's only fishing and pleasure port with 1,000 moorings. Heir to the former prosperous Port d'Albret until the Adour was diverted in 1578 to Bayonne. The port's 19 boats sell their catch directly at the fish market without auction. Sea bass, sea bream, squid according to seasons.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Gouf de Capbreton: Underwater canyon opening right in front of the pier, comparable to the Grand Canyon and carved by tectonics over 270km. It progressively reaches nearly 4,000m depth offshore. This unique geological phenomenon shapes the waves and local marine life. The Gouf Day during Heritage Days features conferences and activities.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Historic center: Pedestrian street lined with medieval half-timbered houses testifying to Capbreton's maritime past. The Maison de l'Oralité et du Patrimoine tells the story of the port and local traditions. The Boudigau, former bed of the Adour, now winds as a developed canal.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Fish market: Daily direct sales by fishermen according to arrivals from 9am to 12pm and 3pm to 7pm. Ultra-fresh line-caught fish, langoustines, sole. Authentic atmosphere on the docks. Fishermen advise on preparation. Prices vary by season and weather.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Dune trail: Starting from the Coastal Ecosystem Research Center (CERS). Discovery of the dune and its specific flora through an educational route. Explanatory panels on the Landes dune ecosystem. Panoramic view from the summit over ocean and forest.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Fish restaurants on the port: Lunch facing the boats with freshly caught specialties. Ttoro (Basque fish soup), grilled squid, grilled sea bream. Lively terraces in season. Reservation recommended on weekends and July-August.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Port d'Albret marine lake: Created artificially between 1966 and 1975 over 40 hectares, this body of water stretches between Vieux-Boucau and Soustons. The central wooded islet nicknamed Temptation Island remains inaccessible but provides a bird refuge. The name Vieux-Boucau means old mouth, a memory of when the Adour flowed out here before 1578.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Promenade du Mail: Connecting downtown to the lake's outlet, this alley lined with maritime pines features restaurants, bars and ice cream shops. A summery laid-back atmosphere reigns on this pedestrian artery. Unobstructed view of the lake and water activities. Spectacular sunsets from the jetty.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The three beaches: Grand Plage in the town center (urban then wild), Sablères beach to the north (high dunes, naturism tolerated), and the marine lake beach with calm, warm waters. Each beach offers a different atmosphere. Lake waters are 3-4°C warmer than the ocean, ideal for families.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Swimming in the marine lake: Lifeguarded beach in season with calm waters maintained at constant level. Pleasant temperature higher than the ocean. Ideal for young children and water sports beginners. Inflatable games installed in summer. Lifeguard station and restrooms on site.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Water activities on the lake: Rental of pedal boats, paddleboards, kayaks and small sailboats. The lake's protected waters allow safe practice for beginners. Sailing lessons for children and adults in July-August. Family-friendly and convivial atmosphere.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Vieux-Boucau markets: Daily morning market except Sunday with local producers. Special evening markets in summer (crafts, clothing). Festive atmosphere with musical entertainment. Landes specialties and fresh products. Set-up from 6pm for evening markets.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Sainte-Marie bell tower-porch: Remnant of a 12th-century Benedictine priory inscribed as UNESCO World Heritage as part of the Santiago de Compostela Routes. The sculpted 13th-century portal ranks among the finest in southwestern France. Remarkably preserved 15th-century murals. The Priory Museum completes the visit with archaeological collections.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Courant de Mimizan: 7km sinuous coastal river connecting Lake Aureilhan to the ocean. This natural boundary separates Mimizan-Nord from Mimizan-Sud. Landscaped walks along the banks with views of fishing and pleasure boats. Renowned spot for sea bass and sea trout fishing.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Lake Aureilhan and Promenade Fleurie: Over 400 plant species line the lake loop. Open-air botanical garden with remarkable trees and exotic plants. The 350-hectare lake offers calm waters for swimming and water activities. View over the Landes forest and dunes in the distance.
⏱️ 90 min
4
Wings Monument: Commemorates the first North Atlantic crossing by the French team of the Oiseau Canari (Assolant, Lefèvre, Lotti) who landed on Remember Beach in June 1929. Stele erected facing the ocean. The Woolsack castle, Tudor architecture from 1910, testifies to the resort's aristocratic past.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Hiking on marked trails: 40km of routes through forest, dunes and lakes. Loops from 2h to 5h depending on itineraries. The coastal trail offers ocean panoramas and passage by blockhouses. Maps available at the tourist office. Welcome shade in summer.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Ocean beach swimming: 10km of beaches including 4 lifeguarded in season (Nord, Sud, Lespecier, Courant). Beware of baïnes (dangerous currents). Swimming flags must be strictly respected. PMR mats for disabled access on certain beaches. Lifeguard stations June to September.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Canoe on the Courant: Peaceful 7km descent between lake and ocean. Rental from the lake departure with shuttle return or one-way to the sea. Wildlife observation (herons, cormorants) and riparian flora. Easy outing accessible from age 6 with accompaniment.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Seaplane Museum: 1,250m² retracing the transatlantic seaplane epic on the former Latécoère base. Starting in 1930, engineer Pierre-Georges Latécoère chose the lake for his routes to America and the West Indies. Mermoz, Saint-Exupéry, Guillaumet took off from these waters. Collection of restored seaplanes, period uniforms, models, engines and giant propellers. Interactive experiences and documentary films.
⏱️ 120 min
2
Lake Cazaux-Sanguinet: 5,600 hectares, France's second-largest freshwater lake. Crystal-clear waters with turquoise reflections nicknamed the Biscaraïbes. Fine sand beaches (Maguide, Navarrosse, Caton) bordered by pines. Gently sloping sandy bottom ideal for families. Clear view of the Dune du Pilat to the north on clear days.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Petit étang de Biscarrosse: Preserved fishing reserve in the forest's heart. Wild banks and absolute calm. Hiking trails allowing a complete loop. Wildlife observation: ducks, moorhens, herons. Access from the D652, developed parking.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Seaplane discovery flight: Takeoff and water landing on the lake from Latécoère hydrobase. 20min flight over lakes, forest and ocean. Unique sensation of flying just above the water. Experienced pilots and maintained aircraft. Booking required, weather permitting.
⏱️ 20 min
→
Water activities on the lakes: Sailing, windsurfing, paddleboarding, water skiing. Several water sports centers around Lake Cazaux. Hourly or half-day rentals. Sailing lessons for children and adults in July-August. Designated zones for each activity. Mandatory life jackets provided.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Aquapark on the lake: Giant floating inflatable structures (slides, trampolines, obstacle courses). Accessible from age 6 who can swim. Renewable 1h sessions. Life jackets provided and mandatory. Lifeguards on site. Open June to September weather permitting.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Oil heritage: Discovery of Western Europe's largest oil field discovered on March 25, 1954. The deposit extends between 2,000 and 2,500m depth beneath Lake Parentis-Biscarrosse. View from the shores of drilling installations, wellheads and derricks operated by Canadian company Vermilion producing a few thousand barrels per day. This unique activity in the Landes financed the village's development.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Roland Portalier Arena: Built in 1927 and completely renovated in 2021, the only such structure in the northern Landes. Typical Landes arena architecture with wooden stands. The Feria Sen Bertomiu in August gathers thousands of festayres for course landaise, concerts and bandas over 5 days. Free exterior visits outside events.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Lake Parentis-Biscarrosse: 3,540 hectares shared with Biscarrosse, Gastes and Sainte-Eulalie. Lake beaches Moutéou Pipiou and Calède with fine sand and fresh waters. Picnic tables and children's games. Spectacular sunsets over the oil installations creating a unique atmosphere. 15th-century Saint-Pierre church in the village center.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Lake fishing: Paradise for predator anglers (pike, zander, perch) and carp. Numerous developed spots along the banks. Boats and float tubes authorized. Fishing permit required, sold at tobacco shop. Catch-and-release recommended to preserve stock. Best months: April-May and September-October.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Lake beach swimming: Moutéou Pipiou beach (tables, children's games) and Calède (wilder). Fresh warm waters in summer, pleasant temperature higher than ocean. Lifeguards in July-August. Sandy gently sloping bottom. Ideal for young children and swimming beginners.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Water sports: Paddleboard, canoe-kayak, pedal boat rental. The lake's calm waters perfect for beginners. Water sports centers on main beaches. Lessons and courses in July-August. Life jackets provided. Exploration of quiet coves and lake wildlife observation.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Écomusée de Marquèze: Exceptional immersion in an authentic 19th-century Landes hamlet, accessible only by historic train from 1903-1910 (Monument Historique carriages) from Sabres. After a 10min journey through the forest, discover the 25-hectare airial where 30 to 40 people once lived. Half-timbered manor houses and Landes farmhouses dating from the 18th and 19th centuries, sheepfolds, water mill on stilts relocated from Geloux, bread ovens under centuries-old oaks.
⏱️ 180 min
2
Traditional craft demonstrations: Costumed interpreters explain pine resin tapping, spinning, weaving, bread making, pastoral life with stilt shepherds. Traditional Gascon games (palet, rampeau, nine-pins). The ecomuseum testifies to the evolution of the Landes landscape, from wet moorland to industrial forest imposed by Napoleon III in 1857. Tasting of pastis landais baked in wood-fired oven.
⏱️ 120 min
3
Le Pavillon: Contemporary building presenting permanent exhibition on the history of Landes de Gascogne and annual temporary exhibition. Modern architecture contrasting with the airial's traditional buildings. Interactive and wheelchair-accessible museum route. Video projections and sound testimonies complete the visit.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Historic train journey: Boarding at Sabres station in 1903-1910 carriages classified as Historic Monuments. 10min forest crossing on the former Sabres-Labouheyre-Mimizan line. Rear platform recommended on return for panoramic view. Regular departures throughout the day. Single ticket includes train and airial access.
⏱️ 20 min💡 Rear platform on return
→
Participatory workshops: Introduction to Landes stilts, Gascon games, straw weaving, artisan soap making according to schedule. Activities for all ages. Activity booklets for children transforming the visit into a treasure hunt. Theatrical scenes on certain days with costumed actors. Digital tour with tablets available at reception.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Full day at the ecomuseum: On-site dining with Landes specialties. Shaded picnic area. Craft product shop (pastis landais, soaps, honey). Allow 4 to 6h to fully enjoy the site. Themed guided tours on certain days. Hours 10am-7pm in high season (April 1 - September 28). Booking advised for groups.
⏱️ 300 min

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather, blooming countryside, ideal for all regions, moderate crowds, excellent value, romantic atmosphere.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather, peak tourist season, highest prices, crowded attractions, perfect for Mediterranean coast, festival season.

Fall (September-October): Harvest season, comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, wine regions spectacular, excellent photography, good value.

Winter (November-March): Christmas markets, ski season Alps, fewer crowds, lowest prices, cozy indoor attractions, cultural experiences.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 7 and 11 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.
