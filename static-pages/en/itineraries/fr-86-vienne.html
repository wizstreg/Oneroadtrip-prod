Vienne Road Trip: Futuroscope and Romanesque Heritage

From the Romanesque frescoes of Saint-Savin to the attractions of Futuroscope, the Vienne bridges the Middle Ages and cutting-edge technology. A fully customizable itinerary among UNESCO heritage, medieval villages, and unspoiled nature.

📅 Best period May, June, September, October

Poitiers welcomes you with the sculpted façade of Notre-Dame-la-Grande and its medieval lanes. Five fortified castles stand watch over Chauvigny from the same rocky spur. The Abbey of Saint-Savin unfurls 700 square meters of Romanesque frescoes beneath its UNESCO-listed vaults. Montmorillon invites you into its City of Writing, among bookshops and chapels. Angles-sur-l'Anglin, the Vienne's only Most Beautiful Village of France, guards a sculpted frieze 15,000 years old. Richelieu closes the journey with its 17th-century ideal city—grid plan and townhouses intact.

  • Poitiers: Romanesque capital of a hundred bell towers and gateway to Futuroscope
  • Chauvigny: five fortified castles on a single rocky spur
  • Saint-Savin: 700 m² of UNESCO frescoes, the "Romanesque Sistine Chapel"
  • Montmorillon: City of Writing and traditional macarons
  • Angles-sur-l'Anglin: Most Beautiful Village of France and Roc-aux-Sorciers

Discover

1
The façade of Notre-Dame-la-Grande displays one of the richest 12th-century sculptural programs in Poitou. Prophets, apostles, and biblical scenes cover the stone across two tiers. Inside, restored Romanesque frescoes drape the vaults in vibrant colors.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Step into the Saint-Jean Baptistery, one of France's oldest preserved Christian buildings. Built in the 4th century, it houses an octagonal baptismal font, Merovingian sarcophagi, and Romanesque wall paintings.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Palace of the Counts of Poitou, residence of the Dukes of Aquitaine, preserves a Great Hall in Angevin Gothic style, rebuilt around 1200 by the Plantagenets. The Maubergeon Tower offers sweeping views over the old town.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Saint-Pierre Cathedral, commissioned by Eleanor of Aquitaine from 1160 onward, blends Angevin Gothic with influences from the Île-de-France. Marvel at the 12th-century Crucifixion window—one of Europe's largest Romanesque stained-glass panels—and the 13th-century choir stalls, the oldest in the country.
⏱️ 50 min

Discover

1
Five fortified castles stand on the same rocky spur in Chauvigny—a rare concentration in Europe. The Bishops' baronial castle, built in the 11th century, dominates the Vienne Valley from the promontory's edge.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The Saint-Pierre Collegiate Church, a 12th-century jewel of Romanesque art, is world-renowned for its polychrome capitals. Signed by master sculptor Gofridus, they depict scenes from Christ's life, monsters, and fantastical beasts.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Château d'Harcourt, best preserved of the five fortresses, dates from the 13th century. Its curtain walls and towers provide a clear glimpse into medieval military architecture.
⏱️ 40 min
4
Enter the Gouzon Keep, topped by a modern glass watchtower. Inside you'll find the Industrial Archaeology Center and a folk traditions museum showcasing a reconstructed Poitevin interior, traditional headdresses, and craftsmen's tools.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
The Abbey of Saint-Savin-sur-Gartempe, a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983, is nicknamed the "Romanesque Sistine Chapel." Look up: 700 square meters of 11th–12th-century frescoes cover the vault 17 meters overhead, unrolling the Old Testament from Creation to Moses.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The bell tower-porch presents an Apocalypse cycle, while the crypt preserves scenes from the lives of Saints Savin and Cyprien, two martyred brothers from the 5th century who gave the village its name.
⏱️ 40 min
3
Explore the monastic building: the former monks' cells, refectory, and monastic garden offer insight into the spiritual and material daily life of a Benedictine abbey founded under Charlemagne in the 9th century.
⏱️ 40 min
4
The 14th-century Gothic spire, rebuilt in the 19th century, rises nearly 80 meters. From the banks of the Gartempe, the panorama of the abbey and village is stunning.
⏱️ 20 min
🎯

Cross the 14th-century Gothic bridge over the Gartempe to enter the City of Writing and Book Crafts. Since 2000, some thirty booksellers, antiquarian dealers, bookbinders, and illuminators have occupied this restored medieval quarter.
⏱️ 75 min

Notre-Dame Church houses 12th-century murals in its Sainte-Catherine crypt. Outside, a statue of the Virgin, considered miraculous since 1789, is still venerated today.

3
The Octagon is a 12th-century funerary chapel, almost unique in Europe for its octagonal shape. It's part of the classified Maison-Dieu complex, founded in the 11th–12th centuries to shelter pilgrims and the sick.
⏱️ 40 min
4
Climb up to Saint-Laurent Chapel, whose façade and bell tower date from the 12th century. It preserves frescoes and the tomb of Étienne de Vignolles, known as "La Hire," Joan of Arc's companion and lord of Montmorillon.
⏱️ 40 min

Discover

1
Saint-Nicolas Church in Civray boasts one of the most ornate Romanesque façades in Poitou, rivaling Notre-Dame-la-Grande. Built in the 12th century, it displays the Wise and Foolish Virgins, the Assumption, the Labors of the Months, and the signs of the Zodiac across two tiers.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Step inside to see the 13th-century fresco recounting the legend of Saint Giles in the south transept. The rest of the interior was painted in the 19th century by Amédée Brouillet, creating a highly colorful atmosphere beneath the octagonal lantern tower.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Hôtel de la Prévôté, built in the late 15th century, hosted King Louis XIII and his young bride in 1616. Several other private mansions line the town's streets.
⏱️ 25 min
4
Fifteen minutes from Civray, the village of Charroux preserves a tall octagonal tower—a remnant of the 11th-century Saint-Sauveur Abbey—plus a cloister and chapter house. The village is labeled a Small Town of Character.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
Angles-sur-l'Anglin has been classified among the Most Beautiful Villages of France since 1982—the only one in the Vienne department. Medieval houses tumble down a rocky spur between the river and the plateau.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The 11th-century fortress towers 40 meters above the Anglin. Its majestic ruins—towers, ramparts, chapel—offer panoramic views over the valley and the village rooftops below.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Roc-aux-Sorciers Interpretation Center presents a reconstruction of a 20-meter sculpted frieze, 15,000 years old. Considered the "Lascaux of sculpture," the original site is closed to the public for conservation reasons.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Step into the village's craft workshops. The Jours d'Angles embroidery technique, invented here, once employed over 300 needleworkers in the 1950s. Demonstrations are offered in season.
⏱️ 30 min

Discover

1
The Pont Henri IV, in Renaissance style, has spanned the Vienne since 1611. An emblem of Châtellerault, it offers picturesque views of the river and quays. It's one of only two bridges in France classified as historic monuments.
⏱️ 20 min
2
The Auto Moto Vélo Museum occupies La Manu, a former arms factory that employed up to 8,000 workers in the early 20th century. Across 4,000 square meters, over 200 vehicles are on display: Bugatti, Delage, Ferrari, Terrot, and rare prototypes like the 1953 Longchamps-Decouçy.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Théâtre Blossac, a 19th-century Italian-style theater, was fully restored to its original state in 2013. Its intimate auditorium with gilded balconies hosts a regular program of performances.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Saint-Jacques Church, a stop on the route to Compostela, houses a classified carillon of 52 bells. Its neo-Romanesque façade overlooks the town.
⏱️ 30 min

Discover

1
The Square Tower dominates Loudun from its 156 steps. Built between 1162 and 1185 under Henry II Plantagenet, it's the sole remnant of the fortress dismantled by Louis XIII to suppress the Protestants. The panorama sweeps across the city and the crossroads of Anjou, Touraine, and Poitou.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Sainte-Croix Collegiate Church, an 11th-century Romanesque edifice, was the theater of exorcism sessions during the trial of Urbain Grandier in 1634. Accused of witchcraft by the Ursuline nuns, the priest was tortured and burned at the stake. The interior preserves painted decorations from the 12th and 14th centuries, restored in 1995.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Porte du Martray, sole survivor of the four gates in the fortified enclosure designed by Philip Augustus in the 14th century, opens onto one of Loudun's oldest quarters.
⏱️ 20 min
4
The Renaudot Museum occupies the birthplace of Théophraste Renaudot, physician to Louis XIII and founder of La Gazette de France in 1631. You'll discover a reconstructed print shop, a period herbalist's, and the library with original issues of the Gazette.
⏱️ 45 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
At the foot of the Square Tower, the medieval-inspired garden features medicinal plants in woven chestnut beds, including those in Renaudot's "Polychreston" remedy from 1619.
⏱️ 30 min
→
Walk the Philippe-Auguste and Foulques Nerra promenades that follow the old ramparts. Views of slate rooftops and bell towers compose a well-preserved tableau.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Join the guided tour "The Affair of the Possessed" offered by the tourist office. Various stops in Loudun illuminate this famous 17th-century witchcraft case, orchestrated by Richelieu against the priest Urbain Grandier.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Richelieu is a unique masterpiece of 17th-century urban planning. Founded in 1631 by the Cardinal and designed by Jacques Lemercier (architect of the Sorbonne), this "ideal city" follows a grid plan enclosed by ramparts and moats, accessible through three monumental gates.
⏱️ 30 min
2
Walk down the Grande Rue, 12 meters wide, lined with 28 identical townhouses—all listed as Historic Monuments. Regularity, symmetry, and perfect alignment: the Cardinal's spirit is readable in every façade.
⏱️ 40 min
3
The market halls, completed in 1638, are a fine example of functional architecture from the Grand Siècle. They still host a market on Friday mornings, opposite the Notre-Dame-de-l'Assomption Church in classical style.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Step into the Espace Richelieu, housed in a restored townhouse, for an interactive exhibit on the Cardinal, his city, and new towns. 3D glasses let you immerse yourself in the vanished splendor of the château.
⏱️ 45 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Hop on a bike along the Richelieu-Chinon Greenway, 20 km on a former railway line through unspoiled nature in the Loire-Anjou-Touraine Regional Nature Park.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Walk around the ramparts and moats. The fourth gate, a fake, was created solely to respect the ensemble's symmetry—a detail that sums up the Cardinal's spirit.
⏱️ 45 min
→
In summer, browse the craft shops and antique dealers installed in some fifteen boutiques around the city.
⏱️ 45 min
