Discover

1
The Palais des Papes has towered over the city since the 14th century. The world's largest Gothic palace spans 161,000 square feet across 25 rooms open to visitors: chapels adorned with Matteo Giovannetti's frescoes, the pope's private apartments, and restored pontifical gardens.
⏱️ 120 min
2
Cross the Pont Saint-Bénézet, where four of the original twenty-two arches still stand. Built between 1177 and 1185, the bridge once stretched 3,000 feet across the Rhône. Two superimposed chapels survive on the second pier.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Musée du Petit Palais occupies the former palace of Avignon's archbishops. Its collections bring together over 300 Italian medieval paintings and sculptures, including works by Botticelli and the Avignon School.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Climb to the Rocher des Doms, a public garden set on the rocky outcrop overlooking the Rhône. The panorama sweeps across the bridge, Villeneuve-lès-Avignon on the opposite bank, Mont Ventoux, and the Alpilles on clear days.
⏱️ 30 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Walk Avignon's ramparts on foot. The 14th-century fortified ring stretches 2.7 miles and retains its towers and monumental gates.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Les Halles covered market welcomes around forty local producers every morning. Candied fruits, goat cheeses, olive oil, and Provençal tapenades.
⏱️ 45 min
→
In July, the Festival d'Avignon takes over courtyards, cloisters, and squares throughout the city. Theater, dance, and performances unfold in dozens of venues, from the Palais des Papes to the smallest chapels.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Fort Saint-André crowns Mont Andaon facing Avignon. This 14th-century royal fortress marked the border between the Kingdom of France and papal lands. Its twin towers frame a fortified gate. From the ramparts, the panorama covers the Palais des Papes, Mont Ventoux, and the Alpilles.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Step through the gates of the Chartreuse du Val de Bénédiction, France's largest charterhouse. Founded in 1352 by Pope Innocent VI, it sprawls across five acres with three cloisters, Matteo Giovannetti frescoes in the chapel, and the founding pope's mausoleum.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Tour Philippe-le-Bel guarded the western end of Avignon's bridge on the French side. Climb its spiral staircase for a commanding view of the Rhône and Avignon's rooftops.
⏱️ 30 min
4
The gardens of Abbaye Saint-André, landscaped atop Mont Andaon, blend Italian-style terraces, centuries-old olive trees, and rose gardens. The Romanesque chapel ruins offer a silent setting facing the papal palace.
⏱️ 45 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Follow the path connecting Fort Saint-André to Tour Philippe-le-Bel along the ramparts. The walk winds through quiet medieval lanes with views of both banks of the Rhône.
⏱️ 45 min
→
In summer, the Chartreuse hosts artist residencies and performances in its cloisters. Check the schedule for the Centre National des Écritures du Spectacle based there.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Settle onto a terrace in Villeneuve's heights to watch the sunset over Avignon. The low-angled light sets the Palais des Papes' golden stone ablaze.
⏱️ 30 min

Discover

1
Orange's ancient theater is the best-preserved in the Western world. Its stage wall, intact across 338 feet long and 121 feet high, still bears Augustus's statue. Ten thousand spectators once filled the tiers carved into the Colline Saint-Eutrope. UNESCO World Heritage site.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The Roman triumphal arch rises at the city's northern entrance. Built around 20–25 CE, it measures nearly 66 feet long by 62 feet high. Its three bays display bas-reliefs celebrating Roman victories, with trophies of arms and maritime symbols. UNESCO-listed.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The Musée d'Art et d'Histoire occupies a 17th-century mansion opposite the theater. It displays fragments of Orange's Roman cadastre, ancient mosaics, and Provençal paintings.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Climb Colline Saint-Eutrope via the path behind the theater. At the summit, vestiges of the Princes of Nassau's castle and a 360° panorama over the Rhône Valley, Dentelles de Montmirail, and Mont Ventoux.
⏱️ 30 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
In summer, the Chorégies d'Orange transform the ancient theater into an open-air opera stage. The stage wall's exceptional acoustics carry voices to the highest tiers.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Wander the narrow streets of old Orange around Place de la République. The Provençal market sets up there Thursday mornings with stalls of olives, honey, and cheeses.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Taste wines from the Châteauneuf-du-Pape appellation, whose vineyards begin just a few miles south of Orange. Tasting cellars line the road between the two towns.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
The Puymin and Villasse archaeological sites form the largest Gallo-Roman complex open to the public in France. At Puymin, the Maison à l'Apollon Lauré spreads across 21,500 square feet of ruins, and the 1st-century BCE ancient theater still hosts summer performances.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Across the avenue, the Villasse quarter reveals the Rue des Boutiques lined with ancient shops, the 32,000-square-foot Maison à la Tonnelle, and public baths with their basins and lead pipes.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Musée Théo Desplans, located on the Puymin site, gathers over 2,000 objects from excavations: Roman marble statuary, mosaics, ceramics, and architectural elements.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Cross the single-arched Roman bridge that has spanned the Ouvèze for two thousand years. It links the lower town to the steep lanes of the medieval Haute-Ville, dominated by the ruins of the Counts of Toulouse's castle built in 1195.
⏱️ 45 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

⏱️ 60 min

Visit wine estates around Vaison to taste Côtes-du-Rhône Villages. The vines climb in terraces on the Ventoux foothills.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Loop hike from the Haute-Ville toward the surrounding hills. The trails cross garrigue, olive groves, and lavender fields with views of the Dentelles de Montmirail.

4
The Église Saint-Denis, built between the 10th and 13th centuries, houses a gilded wooden altarpiece and medieval frescoes. Its Romanesque bell-wall towers over the village rooftops.

Discover

1
The Synagogue of Carpentras, founded in 1367, is the oldest in France still in use. Remodeled in the 18th century with luminous rococo decor, it preserves in its basement a 14th-century mikvé, a ritual bath carved into rock.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The 15th-century southern Gothic Cathédrale Saint-Siffrein contains twelve side chapels with baroque decoration. The flamboyant Gothic Porte Juive recalls forced conversions. Inside, the treasury holds the Saint Mors, a relic attributed to Emperor Constantine.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The 1st-century Roman arch, embedded in the cathedral walls, bears bas-reliefs of chained captives. It's the only monumental vestige of ancient Carpentorate.
⏱️ 15 min
4
The 18th-century Hôtel-Dieu houses the Bibliothèque-Musée Inguimbertine. Its pharmacy preserves Moustiers faience jars aligned in period woodwork, and the museum displays paintings, manuscripts, and natural history collections.
⏱️ 60 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Friday morning's market spreads across several squares with over 350 vendors. Candied fruits from Apt, Carpentras berlingots candies, goat cheeses, and vegetables from the Comtat Venaissin.
⏱️ 60 min
→
From mid-November to March, the truffle market convenes every Friday morning. Brokers negotiate Ventoux black truffles in hushed voices, following an unchanging ritual.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Sample Carpentras berlingots, artisanal candies in mint, anise, or violet flavors. Confectioners continue a craft documented since the 14th century.

Discover

1
Nicknamed the "Venetian Comtat," L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue is crossed by willow-lined canals. About fifteen paddle wheels still turn, remnants of the textile and paper industries that once enriched the town.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The Collégiale Notre-Dame des Anges surprises with its 17th-century baroque interior: 222 sculpted and gilded angels populate the nave, chapels, and choir. The ensemble is a classified Historic Monument.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Over 300 permanent antique dealers occupy seven antique villages, making L'Isle Europe's third-largest art and antiques marketplace.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Visit the Partage des Eaux, a natural site where the Sorgue divides into two branches according to historic distribution: five-twelfths toward Velleron, seven-twelfths toward Entraigues. A small shaded path follows the river.
⏱️ 30 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Sunday and Thursday markets animate the Sorgue quays. In summer, the floating market parades boats loaded with goods along the canals, following the nego-chin ("wet bottom") tradition.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Rent a canoe or kayak to descend the Sorgue from Fontaine-de-Vaucluse to L'Isle. Transparent water flows between white cliffs and foliage.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Sunday browsers or serious collectors: move from stall to stall during the major antique fairs held at Easter and around August 15, attracting hundreds of vendors.

Fontaine De Vaucluse

6 photos
Day 8
Top hotels

Discover

1
The Sorgue springs from the base of a 755-foot cliff. France's most powerful resurgence and the world's fifth, it discharges an average 165–185 billion gallons annually. The chasm has been explored to 1,010 feet without reaching the bottom. In spring, the churning waters offer a striking spectacle.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Ruins of the Château des Évêques de Cavaillon dominate the village from a rocky spur. A steep path leads to the 12th-century vestiges, where the panorama plunges over the Sorgue Valley and Vaucluse cliffs.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The 11th-century Romanesque Église Saint-Véran-Sainte-Marie, a classified Historic Monument, houses a sarcophagus attributed to Saint Véran, Bishop of Cavaillon who supposedly slew the spring's dragon.
⏱️ 20 min
4
The Vallis Clausa paper mill perpetuates artisanal rag paper production. The paddle wheel drives the hammers in a building that served as a mill since the 15th century. Demonstrations of vat papermaking.
⏱️ 30 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Follow the path along the Sorgue from the village to the chasm. The shaded half-mile walk traces the emerald water between plane trees and rock faces.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Kayak down the Sorgue from Fontaine-de-Vaucluse. The clear waters cross a corridor of greenery before reaching L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Visit the Musée Jean Garcin dedicated to the Resistance in Vaucluse during World War II. Documents, objects, and testimonies trace the history of the Ventoux maquis.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
Gordes rises amphitheater-like on a rocky spur of the Vaucluse plateau. Ranked among France's Most Beautiful Villages, it aligns white stone houses, cobbled lanes, and lauze-stone roofs facing the Luberon Valley.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The Renaissance Château de Gordes, with medieval elements, dominates the central square. Its vaulted halls host contemporary art exhibitions. The great hall's monumental fireplace is one of France's largest.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Village des Bories groups around thirty dry-stone huts, classified Historic Monuments. Sheepfolds, bread ovens, wine vats, and dwellings document an agropastoral way of life from the 17th to 19th centuries.
⏱️ 60 min
4
The Abbaye Notre-Dame de Sénanque, founded in 1148 by Cistercian monks, remains home to a monastic community. The Romanesque church, vaulted dormitory, and scriptorium are open to visitors. Lavandin fields surrounding it bloom from mid-June to mid-July.
⏱️ 60 min
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Hike from Gordes to Sénanque Abbey along the ridge trail. The path descends through garrigue and holm oaks to the valley sheltering the abbey.
⏱️ 120 min
→
In season, the lavandin fields around Sénanque offer one of Provence's most iconic landscapes. Arrive early morning for the light and to avoid crowds.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Browse art galleries and artisan workshops scattered through Gordes's cobbled lanes. Ceramicists, painters, and sculptors work in studios open to visitors.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Roussillon ranks among France's Most Beautiful Villages. Its ochre-rendered facades display every shade from pale yellow to blood red, drawn from pigments extracted in nearby quarries.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The Sentier des Ocres, nicknamed the "Giants' Causeway," winds between ochre cliffs sculpted by erosion and former mining in red, orange, and yellow. Two marked routes of 30 and 50 minutes cross this mineral landscape shaded by pines.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Conservatoire des Ocres et Pigments Appliqués, housed in the old Mathieu factory, traces the history of Provence's ochre industry. Demonstrations of pigment production and hands-on workshops.
⏱️ 45 min
4
The Église Saint-Michel dominates Place de la Mairie with its square bell tower. From the nearby orientation table, the gaze sweeps across the Vaucluse plateau, Luberon mountains, and Ventoux.
⏱️ 20 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Follow the Belvédère circuit at Rustrel's Colorado Provençal. This two-hour trail climbs through forest to a panorama overlooking orange dunes and fairy chimneys.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Stroll through art galleries and painters' studios in the village. Ochre has inspired artists for centuries: natural pigments, watercolors, and colored earth appear in canvases.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Late in the day, head to the Castrum overlook to watch the setting sun ignite the ochre cliffs. The colors shift in intensity minute by minute.
