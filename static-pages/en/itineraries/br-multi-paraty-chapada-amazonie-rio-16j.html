Brazil Itinerary 14 Days: São Paulo, Paraty, Chapada dos Guimarães, Amazon & Rio de Janeiro

Five Brazils in one journey: São Paulo's megacity pulse, Paraty's colonial charm, Chapada's wild cerrado, primary Amazon viewed from a 50-meter canopy tower, and Rio's icons—14 days blending heritage, adventure, and nature.

🔄 Route type São Paulo (SP) → Paraty (RJ) → Chapada dos Guimarães (MT) → Nobres (MT) → Alta Floresta/Amazon (MT) → Rio de Janeiro (RJ) - linear circuit with domestic flights
📏 Daily average 45 km

This 14-day itinerary traverses five faces of Brazil. Two days in São Paulo to dive into the megacity, then the UNESCO-listed colonial streets of Paraty. Fly to Mato Grosso for the red cliffs of Chapada dos Guimarães and the crystal waters of Nobres, before three nights of Amazonian immersion at Cristalino Lodge—one of the world's top 25 ecolodges. The circuit concludes with three days in Rio de Janeiro, between Christ the Redeemer, Sugarloaf, and the legendary beaches of Copacabana and Ipanema.

  • São Paulo: two days in the megacity - MASP, Ibirapuera, Mercadão, and street art
  • Paraty: UNESCO colonial center, bay with 50 islands, and artisan cachaça distilleries
  • Chapada dos Guimarães: 86-meter Véu de Noiva waterfall and red sandstone formations
  • Nobres: crystal-clear snorkeling at Aquário Encantado and macaw sunset flights
  • Cristalino Lodge: 3 Amazon nights, 50-meter canopy tower, and exceptional wildlife
  • Rio de Janeiro: 3 days - Christ the Redeemer, Sugarloaf, Copacabana, Ipanema, and Dois Irmãos

The Mercado Municipal (Mercadão), a covered market hall from 1933, features stunning stained-glass mosaics. Browse spices, tropical fruits, smoked meats, artisan cheeses, and the legendary mortadella sandwich—a gastronomic emblem of the city that locals swear by.

Day

Sao Paulo Paulista

5 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
MASP (São Paulo Museum of Art) floats above Avenida Paulista on four iconic red pillars. Lina Bo Bardi designed it in 1968 so the space beneath would remain open to the public. Inside: Van Gogh, Rembrandt, Picasso, Monet, and an exceptional Brazilian art collection.
⏱️ 120 min
2
Ibirapuera Park, designed by Oscar Niemeyer and Roberto Burle Marx for São Paulo's 400th anniversary in 1954, sprawls across 1.6 square kilometers. Lakes, cycling paths, the Ibirapuera Auditorium, Afro Brasil Museum, and the Museum of Modern Art await.
⏱️ 180 min
3
Liberdade, São Paulo's Japanese quarter, is home to the largest Japanese community outside Japan. Red lanterns overhead, specialist groceries, authentic Japanese restaurants, and a vibrant Sunday street market.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Street art walk through Vila Madalena: Beco do Batman concentrates spectacular murals in a single alley. Independent galleries, designer boutiques, and hip bars line the surrounding streets.
⏱️ 150 min
→
Climb to Terraço Itália (41st floor of Edifício Itália) to watch São Paulo unfold infinitely beneath you at sunset.
⏱️ 60 min💡 Minimum consumption required at the rooftop restaurant

Paraty Centro Historico

5 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Paraty's historic center has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2019. Founded in 1667 as the export port for Minas Gerais gold, it's the finest concentration of Portuguese colonial architecture in Brazil. Four 18th-century churches, whitewashed houses with colorful shutters, cobblestone streets that flood at high tide.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Igreja Santa Rita, built in 1722 by freed Africans, is Paraty's oldest church. Its white baroque façade stands out against the palms and mountains. Today it houses the Museum of Sacred Art.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Trindade, a bohemian village 35 minutes from Paraty, is protected by Serra da Bocaina Park. A single road leads in. Praia Cepilho is renowned for surf, Praia Cachadaço for calm, crystal-clear waters.
⏱️ 180 min
🎯

Activities

→
Visit artisanal cachaça distilleries on foot or by bike from the center. Paraty was Brazil's cachaça capital in the 18th century.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Wander the art galleries and cobbled lanes of the historic center in the late afternoon. Colonial houses shelter painting studios, artisan jewelers, and independent bookshops. At high tide, seawater floods the streets—a spectacle unique to Paraty.
⏱️ 90 min

Paraty Centro Historico

Paraty Bay harbors over 50 islands and 100 beaches accessible only by sea. Boat tours (escuna) last 5 hours and stop at deserted islands with turquoise waters: Ilha Comprida and the natural swimming pool Caixa d'Aço, a rock formation sculpted by nature.

Activities

→
Kayak around Jabaquara Bay: navigate tropical islands, glide through mangroves, reach deserted beaches at your own pace.

Discover

1
The center of Chapada dos Guimarães is organized around the Igreja de Sant'Ana, founded in 1779 and protected by IPHAN. Shops sell semi-precious stones and cerrado wood crafts. Perched at 800 meters altitude, the village enjoys cooler temperatures compared to the Cuiabá plains.

Activities

→
Drive to Mirante Alto do Céu, 8 km from the village, for sunset. The sandstone cliffs turn blood-red as the sun plunges behind the plateau. King vultures circle overhead in the updrafts.

Day

Chapada Dos Guimaraes Pousada Do Parque

5 photos
Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
Cachoeira Véu de Noiva plunges 86 meters from a reddish sandstone amphitheater. A 650-meter trail leads to the viewpoint overlooking the Coxipózinho stream valley. Red-and-green macaws nest in cliff crevices. Below, brachiopod fossils embedded in the rock testify to a 400-million-year-old marine past.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Cidade de Pedra, atop the national park cliffs, features sandstone pillars sculpted by erosion into silhouettes resembling skyscrapers. From the viewpoint, your gaze plunges into canyons, veredas (buriti palm corridors), and river springs winding through the cerrado 300 meters below.
⏱️ 120 min
3
The Circuito das Cachoeiras links seven waterfalls over 8 km of trail through cerrado and gallery forest: Andorinhas, Prainha, Degrau, Pulo, and Sete de Setembro. Along the way, Casa de Pedra—a massive water-carved cave—opens natural chambers in the rock.
⏱️ 240 min
🎯

Activities

→
Birdwatching at dawn in the national park's cerrado. Red macaws, king vultures, orange-billed toucans, and swifts share the cliffs and canopy. An accredited guide is mandatory for most trails.
⏱️ 120 min💡 Accredited guide required
→
Swim in the natural pools of Cachoeirinha and Cachoeira dos Namorados, accessible on foot from the Véu de Noiva parking area. The cool water contrasts sharply with the cerrado heat.
⏱️ 60 min

Nobres Bom Jardim

3 photos
Day 7
🔍

Discover

1
Aquário Encantado is a 6-meter-deep basin fed by three springs from the Rio Salobra. The water, filtered through limestone, achieves total transparency. Over thirty fish species—red-finned piraputangas, golden dourados, cascudos—move between sand boils marking the springs. Touching the bottom is forbidden to preserve clarity.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Cachoeira Serra Azul, in the park managed by Sesc Pantanal, plunges 46 meters into a 30-meter azure basin populated with piraputangas. To reach it, count on 470 stairs down (and back up). A 700-meter zipline at 50 meters height offers a spectacular alternative to the staircase on the way back.
⏱️ 120 min
3
Refúgio das Águas, a few minutes from Aquário Encantado, conceals two natural pools fed by underground springs in the heart of gallery forest. The water is absolutely clear; the cool temperature contrasts with the cerrado heat.
⏱️ 90 min
4
Lagoa das Araras, 1 km from Bom Jardim's center, is a wetland bordered by buriti palms whose hollow trunks serve as nests. Every evening around 5pm, hundreds of blue-and-yellow macaws, maritacas, and parakeets converge on the lagoon in a spectacular revoada.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Float down the Rio Salobra in flutuação (surface snorkeling) for 1,000 meters. The limestone gives the water transparency that reveals every detail of the bottom: schools of piraputangas, freshwater rays hidden in the sand, golden dourados. Life jacket and mask provided, guide mandatory.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Sunscreen prohibited in the water
→
Boia Cross at Duto do Quebó: descend 1,800 meters of river on an inflatable tube, alternating calm passages and rapids. The highlight: crossing a 330-meter-long cave, where your headlamp reveals stalactites, bats, and rock formations in total darkness.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Helmet, vest, and sandals provided

Cristalino Lodge

3 photos
Day 8
🔍

Discover

1
Cristalino Lodge nestles on a meander of the Rio Cristalino, in the heart of an 11,399-hectare private reserve of primary Amazonian rainforest. Accessible only by boat after an hour's drive from Alta Floresta, it protects six distinct ecosystems. Over 596 bird species have been recorded here.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The Ficus Trail crosses terra firme forest to a giant fig tree whose buttress roots rise several meters high. Brown capuchin monkeys and red-nosed sakis feed in the canopy above the path.
⏱️ 120 min
🎯

Activities

→
Evening boat trip on the Rio Cristalino. Caimans surface, capybaras graze on the banks, and tiger herons stand motionless. Guides spot wildlife with flashlights in the gathering darkness.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Swim from the lodge's floating dock, anchored in the crystal-clear waters of the Rio Cristalino. The rapids upstream offer an invigorating dip after forest hikes.
⏱️ 60 min
9
Day

Cristalino Lodge

3 photos
Day 9
🔍

Discover

1
The 50-meter canopy tower, one of the tallest in the Americas, rises from the middle of the reserve. From the top, the forest stretches as far as the eye can see—an ocean of green rippling in the wind. Hawks, macaws, toucans, and colorful cotingas cross your field of vision. At sunrise, mist tears open over the canopy.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The Saleiro House, a wooden cabin perched in the reserve, overlooks a saladeiro—soil naturally rich in minerals. Tapirs, collared peccaries, giant anteaters, and tamanduás come to feed. The silent watch can last two hours before a rustle in the leaves betrays a visitor.
⏱️ 150 min
3
The Taboca Trail follows a seasonally flooded forest zone. White-whiskered spider monkeys—endemic species and emblem of the neighboring state park—travel in troops through the canopy. Bromeliads and heliconias carpet the damp understory.
⏱️ 150 min
🎯

Activities

→
Dawn canoe on the Rio Cristalino. Paddles glide silently over translucent water as capped herons, kingfishers, and anhingas dry their wings on low branches. With luck, a giant otter crosses the current ahead of your bow.
⏱️ 150 min💡 Absolute silence to maximize sightings
→
Boat excursion to the confluence of the Rio Teles Pires and Rio Cristalino. The dark waters of the Teles Pires meet the clear waters of the Cristalino. Parrots, parakeets, and storks nest on the neighboring island. The sunset here is spectacular.
⏱️ 180 min

Cristalino Lodge

3 photos
Day 10
🔍

Discover

1
The Sierra Trail crosses hilly terrain covered with semi-deciduous forest. Wild orchids cling to branches; scarlet bromeliads punctuate the understory. From rocky outcrops, the view plunges into the valley and canopy below.
⏱️ 180 min
2
The Cacao Trail follows an area where native cacao trees grow wild. Birds feed on the split pods. Anteaters, armadillos, and capuchin monkeys frequent this section, more open than the others.
⏱️ 120 min
🎯

Activities

→
Second climb to the canopy tower, this time at dawn. The forest awakens in a concert of calls: the metallic song of the bellbird, blue macaws crossing the sky in pairs, multicolored-billed toucans emerging from branches. Low-angle light transforms mist into gold.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Picnic on a sandbank of the Rio Cristalino, followed by a swim in the rapids. Guides prepare lunch in the middle of the forest while blue morpho butterflies, electric-blue wings flashing, flutter between the banks.
⏱️ 120 min

Rio De Janeiro Leme

2 photos
Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Corcovado rises to 710 meters. Christ the Redeemer, inaugurated in 1931, stands 28 meters tall, arms outstretched. From the summit platform: Copacabana, Ipanema, Guanabara Bay, Sugarloaf, and Tijuca Forest spread out 360 degrees.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Sugarloaf (396 m) is reached by two cable cars from Praia Vermelha. The view embraces the entire city, spectacular in late afternoon when golden light illuminates the bay.
⏱️ 120 min
3
Escadaria Selarón counts 215 steps covered with over 2,000 ceramic tiles collected from around the world. This staircase connects the Lapa neighborhood to Santa Teresa.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Santa Teresa is Rio's Montmartre: sloping cobbled lanes, colorful colonial houses, artists' studios.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Sunset cruise in Guanabara Bay: Sugarloaf and Corcovado seen from the water.
⏱️ 120 min

Rio De Janeiro Leme

2 photos
Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Tijuca Forest covers 33 square kilometers—the largest urban tropical forest in the world. Parque Lage combines gardens, waterfalls, a 19th-century mansion, and a contemporary art gallery.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The Metropolitan Cathedral of São Sebastião (1964-1979) is a conical pyramid 96 meters high and 104 meters in diameter. Four giant stained-glass windows run from floor to ceiling.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Rio's Jardim Botânico, founded in 1808 by Dom João VI, extends over 54 hectares at the foot of Corcovado. Its imperial avenue of royal palms, planted in 1842, is one of Brazil's most famous. Orchids, bromeliads, Victoria Amazonica, and a butterfly greenhouse.
⏱️ 90 min
4
Ipanema and Leblon, Rio's chicest beaches, stretch for 4 km facing the Atlantic. Numbered kiosks (postos) structure the social life of the beach: each posto has its vibe, its crowd, its street vendors selling mate and açaí.
⏱️ 120 min
🎯

Activities

→
Hike from Parque Lage to Corcovado: 3 hours through Tijuca's tropical jungle, +700 meters elevation gain.
⏱️ 180 min💡 Early start, guide recommended
→
Saturday feijoada lunch in Santa Teresa or Lapa: slow-cooked black bean and smoked meat stew, caipirinhas, and live samba. The carioca weekend ritual.
⏱️ 120 min

Rio De Janeiro Leme

2 photos
Day 14
🔍

Discover

1
Museu do Amanhã (Museum of Tomorrow), inaugurated in 2015 on the renovated port of Praça Mauá, is an architectural feat by Santiago Calatrava. Its interactive exhibitions explore humanity's major environmental and climate challenges.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Praça XV and its surroundings form Rio's colonial historic heart. The Paço Imperial (1743), former royal residence, the Igreja Nossa Senhora do Carmo, and the old Portuguese town's narrow streets tell Rio's story before the beaches.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Ipanema hippie market (Praça General Osório) on Sunday mornings: handicrafts, jewelry, paintings, semi-precious stones. Rio's largest street market.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Hike Dois Irmãos from Vidigal favela: 45 minutes climbing the two iconic peaks that dominate Ipanema. 360-degree views of the coast, lagoons, Christ, and bay.
⏱️ 120 min

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 12 and 18 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Brazil?

Carnival energy, Amazon rainforest, and pristine beaches create the ultimate South American adventure. Experience vibrant cities, incredible biodiversity, and passionate culture from Rio's beaches to Pantanal wetlands. Samba music, capoeira martial arts, and incredible football culture showcase national spirit. Brazil offers unparalleled natural wonders with infectious cultural energy.
